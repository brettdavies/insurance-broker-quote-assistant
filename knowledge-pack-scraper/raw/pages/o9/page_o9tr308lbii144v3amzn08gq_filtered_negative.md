Liability-only car insurance, also called [personal liability and property damage insurance](https://www.valuepenguin.com/plpd-auto-insurance "What Is PLPD Insurance?"), pays for other people's medical care and car repairs after an accident you cause. The most basic liability-only coverage you can buy is the [minimum amount of car insurance](https://www.valuepenguin.com/minimum-car-insurance-required-your-state "Minimum Car Insurance Required by State") you need to legally drive your car. But you can also get higher limits with a liability-only policy.
* [**Bodily injury liability**](https://www.valuepenguin.com/bodily-injury-car-insurance-coverage "Bodily Injury Car Insurance Coverage: How Does It Work?"): Pays for the medical bills of anyone injured in an accident you cause.
* [**Property damage liability**](https://www.valuepenguin.com/property-damage-liability-car-insurance "What Is Property Damage Liability Car Insurance?"): Pays for damage to other cars and property caused by an accident that's your fault.
* **[Personal injury protection](https://www.valuepenguin.com/personal-injury-protection-car-insurance "What is Personal Injury Protection \(PIP\) and Do You Need It?") or medical payments**: Covers medical bills for you and your passengers after a crash, no matter who is at fault.
* **[Uninsured motorist coverage](https://www.valuepenguin.com/uninsured-underinsured-motorist-coverage-car-insurance "Uninsured and Underinsured Motorist Coverage Car Insurance")**: Pays for your injuries or damage to your car if you're in an accident caused by a driver who doesn't have insurance.
