## Does Texas require RV insurance?

If you drive a motorhome, [RV liability coverage](https://www.progressive.com/answers/rv-liability-coverage/) is required by Texas law and can pay for damage or injuries you cause on the road. If you need more coverage than the state minimum, you can increase your liability limits to protect your assets. Here are the coverages and minimum limits required by Texas law:

| Coverage | What it covers | Amount |
| --- | --- | --- |
| [Bodily injury liability coverage (BI)](https://www.progressive.com/answers/bodily-injury-liability/) | What it coversInjuries you cause to others while driving your motorhome | Amount$30,000 per person $60,000 per accident |
| [Property damage liability coverage (PD)](https://www.progressive.com/answers/property-damage-liability/) | What it coversDamage you cause to another vehicle or property while driving your motorhome | Amount$25,000 per accident |

The state of Texas also mandates [uninsured/underinsured motorist bodily injury](https://www.progressive.com/answers/uninsured-motorist-insurance/) (UM/UIM BI), uninsured motorist property damage (UM PD), and [personal injury protection](https://www.progressive.com/answers/personal-injury-protection/) (PIP) coverages, but you may reject or lower these coverages with the submission of a signed form.

## Does Texas require camper insurance?

Texas law requires that you carry [auto liability coverage](https://www.progressive.com/answers/auto-liability-insurance/) for the vehicle pulling your camper, but the state doesn't require travel trailer insurance. It's important to note that your auto liability coverage won't cover damage to your camper, also known as a travel trailer. If you want to protect your travel trailer against damage, there are coverage options available. Learn more about [travel trailer insurance](https://www.progressive.com/rv-insurance/travel-trailer-insurance/).

### Comprehensive and collision coverage

If you're financing or leasing your motorhome or travel trailer, lenders typically require [RV comprehensive and collision coverage](https://www.progressive.com/answers/rv-collision-comprehensive-coverage/). But even if your RV is paid in full, comprehensive and collision can be valuable safeguards against damage to what may be one of your most prized assets. These coverages may cover up to the actual cash value of your RV if your motorhome or trailer is damaged, stolen, or totaled.

Comprehensive covers events out of your control, including [RV theft](https://www.progressive.com/answers/does-rv-insurance-cover-theft/), vandalism, fire, hitting an animal, and weather-related issues. Collision covers your RV if you collide with another vehicle, tree, fence, guardrail, or any object.

## What if I live in my RV full time?

If your RV is your permanent residence, you may need additional coverage that a recreational RV policy doesn't offer. However, a [full-time RV insurance policy](https://www.progressive.com/answers/full-time-rv-insurance/) may offer protection when your RV is your primary home with the following coverages:

* **Personal liability**: Liability coverage on a full-timers policy can protect against liability claims if you're responsible for someone else's injuries or damages, even when your RV is parked
* **Medical payments**: If someone hurts themselves in or around your RV, medical payments coverage may pay for their medical bills
* **Loss assessment**: This coverage can pay for fees or charges from your RV association, up to $5,000
