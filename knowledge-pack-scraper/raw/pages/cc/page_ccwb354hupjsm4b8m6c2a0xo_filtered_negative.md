**The company you choose has a much bigger impact on the cost of Kia insurance than the model you drive.** That's why it's important to [shop around for quotes from multiple insurance companies](https://www.valuepenguin.com/auto-insurance-quotes "Get Free Car Insurance Quotes Online") to find the cheapest rate for you.
For example, the average cost of full coverage insurance is $255 for a [Honda](https://www.valuepenguin.com/cost-of-honda-car-insurance "Honda Car Insurance") and $280 per month for a [Hyundai](https://www.valuepenguin.com/hyundai-car-insurance "Hyundai Car Insurance: Rates by Model"). Toyotas are typically a little cheaper to insure, at $212 per month, on average.

These companies have not provided a list of models or years that they won't cover. But you may not be able to get a policy from them if your car  doesn't have an engine immobilizer.
[State Farm](https://www.valuepenguin.com/state-farm-insurance-review "State Farm Insurance Review") offers the best mix of affordable rates and reliable customer service to Kia owners. While State Farm costs $64 per month more than the cheapest company, Geico, it offers much better customer service. State Farm could be worth the extra cost if you're ever in a crash.
Some Kia models don't have a basic piece of anti-theft technology in the form of an engine immobilizer. This makes them [easier to steal #challenge ](https://www.valuepenguin.com/auto-insurance/kia-insurance#challenge "#challenge"), and very expensive for insurance companies to cover. So, some companies won't cover these models, which include Kia Fortes, Optimas, Rios, Sedonas, Sorentos, Souls and Sportages with model years between 2015 and 2021.
Car insurance for Kias doesn't cost more than similar types of cars. The average [cost of insuring a Kia Cost of Kia insurance by model ](https://www.valuepenguin.com/auto-insurance/kia-insurance#model "Cost of Kia insurance by model") is $240 per month for a full coverage policy. In comparison, Toyotas cost an average of $212 per month to insure, while Hondas and Hyundais cost $255 per month and $280 per month, respectively.
