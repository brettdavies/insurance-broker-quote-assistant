Buyer's Guide to Get the Best Home and Auto Bundle

Bundling home and auto insurance saves policyholders about 13% on premiums while simplifying coverage with one provider. State Farm offers the best and cheapest bundle at $2,559 per year, combining affordability and service.

In some cases, separate policies can be cheaper. For example, Nationwide auto insurance and Progressive home insurance cost $3,224 annually less than Progressives bundle but more than State Farms or Allstates. Some bundles, like Travelers' at $5,285 per year, are much more expensive, making separate policies a better option for certain drivers.

To find the most cost-effective coverage, compare quotes for bundling and separate policies, especially if you have poor credit or a bad driving record.

How to Bundle Home and Auto Insurance the Right Way

Follow these steps to maximize your savings with a car and homeowners insurance bundle:

  1. 1
    Know your coverage

    Review your current policies to determine your coverage level. Then, you can get quotes for the same coverage limits.

  2. 2
    Get and compare quotes

    Contact your current insurance company. Then, compare at least three other insurance companies for home and auto coverage. Ask for bundle quotes and individual quotes to compare. Compare equal coverage levels from each company.

  3. 3
    Ask about discounts and deductibles

    Ask insurers about available discounts to maximize your savings. Some companies also provide a single deductible for home and car damage if an incident affected both.

  4. 4
    Consider customer service and claims handling

    A low premium isn't the only factor. Look at customer satisfaction ratings and claims processing times to ensure reliable service.

  5. 5
    Shop annually

    Rates frequently change, so complete this process every year or two for the best deal.

Questions to Ask When Buying a Home and Auto Bundle

1. How much will I actually save by bundling compared to separate policies? 

Ask for exact dollar amounts rather than just percentage discounts, as the savings can vary widely based on your specific coverage needs. Request quotes for bundled and separate policies to confirm you're getting the best deal, as bundling isn't always cheaper.

2. Will I have a single deductible if my home and car are damaged in the same incident?

Some companies let you pay just one deductible when the same disaster hits your home and car. So if a storm damages your house and vehicle, you'd pay one deductible instead of two. That can save you thousands during a claim, though not every insurer offers this option.

3. How will bundling affect my coverage limits and policy terms?

Ensure bundling doesn't result in reduced coverage or higher deductibles in exchange for lower premiums. Ask for a side-by-side comparison of your current coverage versus the proposed bundle to identify any differences in protection levels or policy terms.

4. What happens to my bundle discount if I need to file a claim?

Some insurers may reduce or eliminate bundle discounts after claims, while others maintain the same discount regardless of claim history. Understanding this policy beforehand can help you anticipate potential premium increases after filing a claim.

5. Can I have different renewal dates for each policy within my bundle?

While bundling typically aligns your policies to a single renewal date for convenience, some companies allow flexibility in setting different dates. This can help spread out payments throughout the year if you prefer not having both premiums due simultaneously.

Best Home and Auto Insurance Bundles: FAQ

Home and auto insurance bundles are among the most common discounts in the insurance industry. Below are answers to frequently asked questions about them:

How do I cancel my insurance and bundle with another company?

Before switching your car insurance or home insurance policy, compare quotes from other companies to ensure you get the rates and service you need. Purchase your new policy before canceling the old one to avoid a costly lapse in coverage. You can cancel your previous policy after securing a new one. However, check for cancellation fees or requirements, such as providing a formal letter.

What information do you need to bundle your policies?

Bundling policies requires the same personal information as applying for individual policies. You'll need:

  • Your personal details, including pet information
  • Car details like VIN, registration, mileage, make and model
  • Driving history
  • Property address
  • Property appraisal details
  • Lender agreements
  • Previous insurance information
  • A list of safety features in your home

Is GEICO or State Farm better for home and auto bundles?

Our analysis found that State Farm was cheaper overall and offered a discount up to 25%

Is it advisable to bundle home and auto insurance for seniors?

Yes, bundling home and auto insurance is often a smart choice for seniors. It can lower your overall premiums, simplify billing, and make claims easier to manage. However, savings vary by insurer and coverage level, so its best to compare quotes first.

Will bundling affect my ability to switch insurance companies in the future?

Bundling doesn't lock you into a company forever, but you may need to replace both policies simultaneously when switching. Some insurers offer "switch-and-save" promotions targeting customers looking to transfer multiple bundled policies.

Can I bundle a homeowners policy with a motorcycle or RV insurance instead of auto?

Most major insurers allow bundling homeowners insurance with motorcycles, RVs, boats and other vehicle types. These alternative bundles offer similar discount percentages but may vary by company.
