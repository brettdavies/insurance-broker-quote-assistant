Liability coverage is the most basic form of [**car insurance**](https://insurify.com/car-insurance/). It covers other people’s [**bodily injury**](https://insurify.com/car-insurance/coverage/bodily-injury-liability/) and [**property damage**](https://insurify.com/car-insurance/coverage/liability-insurance/) expenses if you cause an accident. California — like most states — requires all drivers to carry liability insurance at certain minimum limits before they can hit the road.
The [**average cost of car insurance in California**](https://insurify.com/car-insurance/california/average-cost/) is $146 per month, according to Insurify data. Drivers pay $93 per month for liability coverage and $200 for full coverage, on average.

[**Liability insurance**](https://insurify.com/car-insurance/coverage/liability-insurance/) covers bodily injuries and property damage to other drivers and their passengers after an accident you cause. The bodily injury portion of liability insurance pays for injuries or deaths of the other driver and their passengers, up to your chosen limits.
But liability car insurance doesn’t cover any injuries or damage to you, your passengers, or your vehicle. To get protection for these damages, you’ll need to buy medical payments, collision, and comprehensive coverages. The combination of liability, collision, and comprehensive coverage is also called [**full-coverage car insurance**](https://insurify.com/car-insurance/knowledge/full-coverage-car-insurance/).
For example, if you cause an accident and have only the state’s minimum liability coverage, none of your or your passengers’ injuries would be covered, nor would any damages to your car. But if you’re carrying full coverage and have [**medical payments coverage**](https://insurify.com/car-insurance/knowledge/medical-payments-coverage/), it would cover all these expenses up to your chosen limits.
As of Jan. 1, 2025, California has newly increased minimum car insurance requirements.[[1]](https://insurify.com/car-insurance/california/liability-only/#cite-1) But the types of coverage remain the same. You’ll need to carry the following coverages in at least the stated amounts before getting behind the wheel:
* **Keep your driving record clean.** A[**clean driving record**](https://insurify.com/car-insurance/driver/driving-record/clean-driving-record/)
* **Apply for discounts.**[**Car insurance discounts**](https://insurify.com/car-insurance/discounts/)[**bundling your home and auto insurance**](https://insurify.com/car-insurance/coverage/home-and-auto/)
* **Raise your deductible.** Increasing your [**car insurance deductible**](https://insurify.com/car-insurance/knowledge/car-insurance-deductible/) will automatically lower your monthly premium, as the two are inversely related. But you should only raise your deductible to an amount that you can afford to pay out of pocket in the event of a claim.
* **Compare car insurance quotes.** Always compare car insurance rates from multiple insurance companies before settling on a policy. Every insurer determines rates differently, so you could find a cheaper rate for the same policy if you take the time to compare. You should compare quotes at least every six months. [**Insurance comparison sites**](https://insurify.com/car-insurance/the-best-and-worst-sites-to-compare-car-insurance-quotes/) are generally a good place to start.
* **Take advantage of California’s Low Cost Auto (CLCA) insurance program.** California offers reduced rates for low-income drivers who meet certain vehicle and income requirements.[[2]](https://insurify.com/car-insurance/california/liability-only/#cite-2)
While liability insurance is enough to satisfy [**California’s legal requirements**](https://insurify.com/car-insurance/california/minimum-requirements/), most insurance professionals recommend buying additional coverage. And if you lease or finance your car, your lender will likely require you to have full coverage anyway.
[**Collision insurance**](https://insurify.com/car-insurance/coverage/collision-insurance/) pays for the cost of repairs or replacement after a collision with another car or object.
[**Comprehensive insurance**](https://insurify.com/car-insurance/coverage/comprehensive-insurance/) pays for any damage from non-collision events, such as theft, hail, or vandalism.
[**Uninsured/underinsured motorist coverage**](https://insurify.com/car-insurance/coverage/uninsured-motorist-coverage/) protects you if you’re in an accident with a driver who has no insurance or insufficient coverage.
[**Medical payments coverage**](https://insurify.com/car-insurance/knowledge/medical-payments-coverage/) helps pay for medical bills after an accident, no matter who was at fault.
[**Roadside assistance coverage**](https://insurify.com/car-insurance/knowledge/what-is-roadside-assistance/) pays for the cost of towing, emergency tire changes, and fuel delivery.
[**Gap insurance**](https://insurify.com/car-insurance/coverage/gap-insurance/) pays the difference between your car’s remaining balance and its actual cash value if you total it.

* ### Is California a no-fault state?
No. California is an at-fault state for car insurance.[[3]](https://insurify.com/car-insurance/california/liability-only/#cite-3) The state uses “comparative negligence,” meaning the fault can be shared by both drivers in an accident.
