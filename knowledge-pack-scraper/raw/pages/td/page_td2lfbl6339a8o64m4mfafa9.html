How to Get Your Progressive Insurance ID Card

Written by Megan Lee and 1 other
Updated Mar 20, 2025
Written by
Authors
Megan Lee
Writer and Editor
Editors
Annie Millerbernd
Writer and Editor

Progressive policyholders can access, download and print their insurance ID card 24/7 online or via the mobile app.

When you sign up for a Progressive car insurance policy, you receive an ID card that functions as proof of insurance. You can access that card 24/7 through the website and mobile app.

While Progressive sells several types of insurance, from homeowners to renters insurance and life insurance, you wont regularly need to provide proof of insurance for those types of coverage. Progressive car insurance, however, comes with an insurance card that you should keep on hand at all times. Heres how to get your Progressive insurance card.

How to get your Progressive insurance cards 

You can download a copy of your Progressive insurance card from your online account or request a copy by mail. 

Progressive policyholders can also access a digital insurance card through the companys mobile app.

  • Online: Log in to your Progressive account and select Get your ID card. Youll be directed to a page where you can view, save, print or email your ID card.
  • Mail: You can request a physical copy of your card by logging into your Progressive account or calling an agent. If you have a paperless discount on your policy, you could lose it by requesting documents via mail.

As soon as you buy a Progressive insurance policy, you should receive an email with a link to your insurance identification card. Download and print it, then keep a copy in your vehicle as proof of coverage in case youre pulled over or get in an accident.

Whats included on your Progressive insurance card

Your Progressive ID card acts as proof of financial responsibility, showing that youve purchased at least the minimum amount of insurance coverage required in your state. It usually wont show your specific coverage selections, limits or rates. Instead, it shows:

  • The name and address of your insurance company.
  • The companys National Association of Insurance Commissioners (NAIC) number.
  • Your policy number.
  • Your first and last name.
  • Your cars make, model and vehicle identification number (VIN).
  • The policys effective date and expiration date.

For more detailed insurance information, such as the exact coverage limits or deductibles on your policy, youll need to view your declarations page.

Can you show proof of insurance on your phone?

Every state except for New Mexico allows drivers to show electronic proof of insurance on their phone during a traffic stop. In other words, if youre pulled over and asked to show your drivers license, registration and proof of insurance, you can pull up your insurance ID card on the Progressive app unless youre in New Mexico.

Most major car insurance companies including GEICO, Allstate and State Farm use digital ID cards in addition to traditional physical cards.

Why do you need your insurance ID card?

Here are three instances when you would need your insurance ID card.

  • If youre pulled over: In a traffic stop, a police officer typically requests your drivers license, registration and proof of insurance.   
  • In an accident: If youre involved in an accident, youll likely need to exchange insurance information with the other driver.
  • Registering and renewing: Many states require proof of insurance when registering a vehicle or renewing your drivers license.

FAQ

  • What is the NAIC number for Progressive?

    Progressive has several NAIC numbers, but the default Payer ID for eBills is 24260.

  • How can I get physical Progressive insurance cards?

    Policyholders can print a copy of their ID card anytime from their online account anytime. If you prefer a physical card, it can be faxed or mailed to you.

  • How do I print my Progressive insurance card?

    The easiest way to print your Progressive insurance card is to log in to your online account. In your dashboard, navigate to the Get your ID card link under a list of Things to do. This will display your card, which you can then print.
