## Do you need RV insurance in North Dakota?

If you drive a motorhome, [RV liability coverage](https://www.progressive.com/answers/rv-liability-coverage/) is required by North Dakota law and can pay for damages or injuries you cause on the road. ## Do you have to have insurance on a camper in North Dakota?

If you have a camper, also known as a travel trailer, you're required to carry [auto liability coverage](https://www.progressive.com/answers/auto-liability-insurance/) for the vehicle you use to pull your camper. Note that liability coverage won't cover damage to your travel trailer or motorhome. You can purchase comprehensive and collision coverage to protect against RV damage, but they aren't required by state law. Learn more about [travel trailer insurance](https://www.progressive.com/rv-insurance/travel-trailer-insurance/).

### Comprehensive and collision coverage

If you're financing or leasing your motorhome or travel trailer, it's likely that your lender requires [RV comprehensive and collision coverage](https://www.progressive.com/answers/rv-collision-comprehensive-coverage/). Even if your RV is paid in full, comprehensive and collision coverages could be a worthwhile option. These coverages may cover the actual cash value of your RV if your motorhome or trailer is stolen or damaged beyond repair.

Comprehensive covers events out of your control, including [RV theft](https://www.progressive.com/answers/rv-theft-insurance-prevention-tips/), vandalism, fire, hitting an animal, and weather-related issues. Collision covers your RV if you collide with another vehicle, tree, fence, guardrail, or any object.

## What if I live in my RV full time?

If your RV is your permanent residence, you may need additional coverage that a recreational RV policy doesn't offer. However, a [full-time RV insurance policy](https://www.progressive.com/answers/full-time-rv-insurance/) may offer protection when your RV is your primary home with the follow coverages:

* **Personal liability**: Liability coverage on a full-timers policy can protect against liability claims if you're responsible for someone else's injuries or damages, even when your RV is parked.
* **Medical payments**: If someone hurts themselves in or around your RV, medical payments coverage may pay for their medical bills.
* **Loss assessment**: This coverage can pay for fees or charges from your RV association, up to $5,000.
