Jan. 9, 2025, 8:11 AM CST / Updated Jan. 11, 2025, 7:00 PM CST
The [wind-driven wildfires ](https://www.nbcnews.com/weather/wildfires/fire-pacific-palisades-los-angeles-evacuations-rcna186675)burning out of control in the Los Angeles area couldn't have emerged at a more perilous moment for California's homeowners, as officials try to rehab what they concede is a deepening "[insurance crisis](https://www.insurance.ca.gov/0400-news/0100-press-releases/2024/release062-2024.cfm)."
"Californians deserve a reliable insurance market that doesn't retreat from communities most vulnerable to wildfires and climate change," Insurance Commissioner Ricardo Lara [said in a pr](https://www.insurance.ca.gov/0400-news/0100-press-releases/2024/release065-2024.cfm)[ev](https://www.insurance.ca.gov/0400-news/0100-press-releases/2024/release065-2024.cfm)[i](https://www.insurance.ca.gov/0400-news/0100-press-releases/2024/release065-2024.cfm)[ous](https://www.insurance.ca.gov/0400-news/0100-press-releases/2024/release065-2024.cfm)[ statement](https://www.insurance.ca.gov/0400-news/0100-press-releases/2024/release065-2024.cfm). "This is a historic moment for California."

The ongoing Palisades Fire is poised to become one of the state's costliest: Fire officials said Wednesday that more than 11,800 acres have been destroyed and that 1,000 structures have been burned, while a J.P. Morgan Insurance analysis estimates insured losses from that blaze alone could approach $10 billion. At least four other significant fires have erupted, as well.
But there had been a glimmer that some insurance companies are willing to fill the voids in the market. On Tuesday, Mercury Insurance, an independent home insurer in California, announced it would begin writing new homeowners insurance policies in the town of Paradise, the site of the deadly [Camp Fire ](https://www.nbcnews.com/news/us-news/pg-e-pleads-guilty-84-counts-manslaughter-devastating-camp-fire-n1231256)in 2018, which is considered to be the worst wildfire in modern state history.
