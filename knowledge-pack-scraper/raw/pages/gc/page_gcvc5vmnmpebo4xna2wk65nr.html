How to Get Cheap SR-22 Car Insurance Rates

Finding cheap SR-22 insurance means picking the right provider and keeping continuous coverage. If your current insurer handles SR-22 filings, request one after paying a small fee (typically under $30). If not, switch to an insurer that does. Follow these steps:

  1. 1
    Check if your insurer offers SR-22 filings

    Contact your insurer to confirm it handles SR-22 filings. If yes, request the form and ask about fees. If no, find one that does.

  2. 2
    Compare quotes from SR-22-friendly insurers

    Not all companies insure high-risk drivers. Rates swing wildly between providers. Compare quotes from multiple providers that specialize in SR-22 insurance. Some offer discounts for high-risk drivers.

  3. 3
    Purchase a policy that meets state requirements

    Buy a policy that meets your state's minimum coverage requirements. If you already have coverage, your insurer may let you add an SR-22 to your existing policy. A higher deductible cuts your premium.

  4. 4
    Pay the SR-22 filing eee

    Your insurer submits the SR-22 form to your state for a one-time fee under $30 in most states. Some states also charge license reinstatement or processing fees; check with your local DMV for total costs.

  5. 5
    Keep your coverage active

    Most states require SR-22 filings for three years, though duration varies by violation severity and recency. If your policy lapses, your insurer notifies the state. This can trigger license suspension, penalties and difficulty finding affordable SR-22 insurance later. Set up automatic payments or renewal reminders to avoid coverage gaps.

  6. 6
    High-risk driver education training

    Court-ordered DUI classes or state-approved defensive driving courses can cut SR-22 premiums at participating companies. These programs show insurers you're working on rehabilitation.

SR-22 Car Insurance: FAQ

We answer common questions about SR-22 filings and insurance providers:

Which company has the cheapest insurance for someone with an SR-22?

Erie is the cheapest insurance company for drivers with an SR-22, with minimum coverage averaging $1,591 annually or $133 monthly. Nationally, GEICO and Progressive offer the most affordable rates at $144 and $153 monthly for liability-only coverage.

How much does insurance with an SR-22 cost on average?

SR-22 insurance costs vary by violation type. Hit-and-run offenses result in the highest premiums at $188 monthly for minimum coverage and $371 monthly for full coverage, while DUI violations average $184 monthly for minimum coverage and $361 monthly for full coverage.

Which company has the best insurance for an SR-22?

Travelers offers the best cheap SR-22 car insurance with a MoneyGeek score of 96, averaging $40 monthly for minimum coverage and $50 monthly for full coverage.

How long do you need to have an SR-22?

You usually need an SR-22 for three years, though the duration can range from one to five years based on your state and the severity of the violation.

When do you need SR-22 Insurance?

An SR-22 or FR-44 filing may be required for offenses like DUI, DWI, OWI, multiple or serious traffic violations, driving without insurance, causing an accident without coverage or unpaid child support.

Coverage Definitions

Minimum coverage refers to each state's mandated liability-only policy, offering the lowest cost but limited financial protection. Full coverage includes a 100/300/100 policy with a $1,000 deductible, meaning you pay $1,000 out-of-pocket before insurance covers damage in an at-fault accident.

The 100/300/100 industry standard provides:

  • $100,000 bodily injury coverage per person you injured
  • $300,000 total bodily injury coverage per accident
  • $100,000 property damage coverage
