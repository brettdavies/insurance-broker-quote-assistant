We reviewed average rates, third-party reviews and more to determine the best cheap homeowners insurance in the Windy City. Based on our analysis, the top companies include USAA, Chubb, Allstate, State Farm and American Family.

How much is home insurance in Chicago, Illinois?

The average cost of home insurance in Chicago is $2,647 per year for a policy with $300,000 in dwelling coverage. This cost is higher than the Illinois state average, which is $2,225 per year. Home insurance premiums can vary based on factors like claims history and home age and size, among other things, meaning your individual premium could be lower or higher than these averages. Comparing quotes from multiple providers can help you find the cheapest rates for your specific situation.

Chicago rates by credit tier

Credit history can significantly impact home insurance in Chicago, IL. Homeowners with poor credit scores are categorized as higher risk, which often leads to higher premiums. Conversely, those with good or excellent credit typically enjoy lower rates. For instance, the average cost of Chicago homeowners insurance for those with poor credit is 188 percent more than for homeowners with good credit. Improving your credit over time can be a useful strategy to help reduce your Chicago home insurance costs.

Chicago rates by deductible amount

The cost of home insurance in Chicago, IL can vary significantly based on the deductible amount you choose. Generally, selecting a higher deductible means paying more out of pocket if you file a claim, but it can also lead to a lower annual premium. Opting for a higher deductible might be a good strategy to reduce your overall insurance costs. Frequently asked questions

  • Flooding can happen anywhere, even if you don't live near a large body of water. An overflowing culvert, for example, may be enough to cause a wet basement. Many Chicagoans live near Lake Michigan, while others have homes near smaller bodies of water, such as the Des Plaines River all of which can flood in the right conditions. To find out your risk of flooding, check out FEMA's Flood Map Service Center, which allows you to input your address to find out if there is a high risk of flooding in your neighborhood. If you need flood insurance, your home insurance company may be able to help you, or you can look into purchasing a policy from the National Flood Insurance Program.

  • Homeowners insurance in Chicago typically covers several key areas to protect your home and belongings. Standard policies usually include dwelling coverage, which helps repair or rebuild your home if its damaged by covered perils like fire, wind or hail. Personal property coverage is also common, protecting your belongings such as furniture, electronics and clothing. Liability coverage is included to help pay for legal expenses if someone is injured on your property, and additional living expenses coverage can assist with temporary living costs if your home is uninhabitable due to a covered event.

  • As a general rule, most insurance professionals recommend you have enough dwelling insurance to cover your homes rebuild value and enough personal property insurance to cover the value of your personal items like clothing, furniture and appliances. Your liability coverage limit may be based on your personal assets, such as your income, savings and retirement accounts. When in doubt, you may find it helpful to consult with a licensed agent to determine if you have sufficient coverage.

  • More home insurance companies than ever allow people to request quotes and purchase home insurance online. You may want to start by researching insurance companies that offer the coverage youre looking for. Once youve identified a few, fill out the online quote request for each by providing your contact information and details about your home. After youve provided the necessary information, you should quickly receive a quote that you can compare to the other insurers. Once youve found a policy you want at a price you can afford, you can submit a payment online to purchase and initiate coverage.
