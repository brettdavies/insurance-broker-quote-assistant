Car insurance is affordable in [North Carolina](/auto-insurance/north-carolina-car-insurance/). The average car insurance premium in North Carolina is $865 per year — less than the [nationwide average](/auto-insurance/how-to-shop/average-auto-insurance/) by 39.4%.

Your car insurance rates are dictated by factors other than state lines. Auto insurance rates price based on myriad factors, such as your age, gender, marital status, credit, and driving history.

## Mean Car Insurance Rates in North Carolina by Age

It’s a fact of life: as you [age](/auto-insurance/driver/age/), your auto insurance rates shift. Generally speaking, auto insurance is more costly for young drivers, as car insurance companies consider inexperienced motorists as less responsible.

In North Carolina, car coverage costs $2,650 per year for a 16-year-old motorist and only $842 yearly for a driver in their 50s.

###### NORTH CAROLINA AUTO INSURANCE COSTS BY AGE

| Age | Average Annual Premium |
| --- | --- |
| 16 | $2,649.93 |
| 17 | $2,062.91 |
| 18 | $1,816.06 |
| 19 | $924.79 |
| 20s | $875.07 |
| 30s | $858.96 |
| 40s | $849.97 |
| 50s | $842.29 |
| 60s | $857.75 |
| 70s | $895.77 |

If you're shopping for car insurance for a teen driver, North Carolina is the 2nd cheapest state in which to buy auto insurance for a 16-year-old.

## Mean Auto Insurance Rates in North Carolina by Marital Status

One benefit of wedlock — in addition to the honeymoon — is the price cut you typically receive on your car insurance policy. In North Carolina, [married drivers](/auto-insurance/driver/status/) save $7 annually on car insurance policies. This is a smaller discount than the national post-nuptial discount of $76.

###### NORTH CAROLINA AUTO INSURANCE RATES BY MARITAL STATUS

| Marital Status | Average Annual Premium |
| --- | --- |
| Single | $859.45 |
| Married | $865.99 |
| Divorced | $859.45 |
| Widowed | $859.45 |

Auto insurance coverage for divorced drivers in North Carolina is the absolute cheapest in the nation. If you are paying too much for your policy, shop around. You could find some serious savings!

## Average Car Insurance Prices in North Carolina by Credit

Car insurance companies use credit score to predict how reliable a customer will be. Trended data show drivers who carry exceptional credit are usually more insurable car insurance clients: they less commonly file claims covered by insurance companies.

In the state of North Carolina, a driver with excellent credit can save $171.49 per year in car insurance premiums, contrasted with a driver with a less-favorable credit score. Read more about how credit score may impact auto insurance costs:

###### NORTH CAROLINA AUTO INSURANCE COSTS BY CREDIT

| Very Poor (300-579) | $1,006.36 |
| Fair (580-669) | $983.54 |

## Average Car Insurance Prices for High-Risk Drivers in North Carolina

If you’re determined to be at fault in an accident or convicted of a [vehicular violation](/auto-insurance/driver/other-factors/cheap-car-insurance-high-risk-drivers/), your insurance rates are bound to grow. The more infractions you amass, the more costly your auto insurance gets.

In North Carolina, your first minor at-fault crash results in an additional $311.77 annually in auto insurance premiums. A DUI — among the most serious driving infractions — will lift your yearly insurance rates in North Carolina by an average of $792.25.

###### NORTH CAROLINA CAR INSURANCE PRICES WITH VIOLATIONS

| Violation | Average Annual Rate |
| --- | --- |
| DUI or DWI | $1,657.53 |
| Reckless Driving | $3,635.90 |
| At-Fault Accident (<$1,000) | $1,177.06 |
| At-Fault Accident ($1,000-$2,000) | $2,750.30 |
| At-Fault Accident (>$2,000) | $1,301.59 |
| Open Container | $2,509.97 |
| Speeding (21-25 MPH > limit) | $2,268.49 |
| Speeding (16-20 MPH > limit) | $2,251.38 |

If you're in need of affordable auto insurance, think about shopping policies to get affordable coverage that covers your vehicle appropriately.

## Average Car Insurance Premiums by Coverage Amount in North Carolina

How much you pay every month for car insurance depends on the tier of protection you select. Liability-only coverage normally costs less, while low-deductible comprehensive coverage usually costs more. In North Carolina, the rate gap between liability-only coverage and full coverage with a $500 deductible is $404.

| $50K/$100K/$50K Bodily Injury/Property Damage — Liability Only | $394 |
| $50K/$100K/$50K Bodily Injury/Property Damage — $1,000 Comprehensive/Collision | $741 |
