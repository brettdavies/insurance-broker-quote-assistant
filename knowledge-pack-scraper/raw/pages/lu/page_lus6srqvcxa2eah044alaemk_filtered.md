#### Standard Coverages

**Personal Property:** This coverage includes items like clothing, electronics, furniture, tools, and appliances, protecting them against incidents such as fire, theft, vandalism, tornadoes, and water damage. Additional coverage may be necessary for jewelry or art and collectibles, which can be added during the quote process.

Example: If your apartment is burglarized, and your couch is stolen, personal property coverage may reimburse you for the cost of replacing the couch, up to your policy limits, minus your deductible.

**Liability:** If someone is injured at your residence, personal [liability](https://www.johnperryinsurance.com/progressive-general-liability-insurance/) coverage may pay for their injuries up to the limits of your policy.

Example: If a guest slips and falls at your party and you’re found liable, renters insurance may cover medical expenses, lost wages, and legal fees if you’re sued.

**Loss of Use:** If your home becomes uninhabitable due to a covered loss, loss of use coverage may help with lodging and grocery expenses above your normal spending.

Example: If your apartment’s kitchen is damaged by a cooking fire, and you must stay in a hotel during repairs, you may be reimbursed for hotel and dining expenses above your usual grocery or dining-out budget.

### Commonly asked questions about renters insurance

How much renters insurance do I need?

The amount of coverage depends on the value of your personal property and your net worth. Conducting a home inventory can help determine your coverage needs.

How much does renters insurance cost?

Renters insurance can cost less than $1 per day. Various factors influence the cost, including location and coverage limits.

Does renters insurance cover theft?

Personal property coverage may cover stolen belongings up to your policy’s limit, including theft from your car, storage unit, or while traveling.

Does renters insurance cover flooding?

Renters insurance typically does not cover flooding. However, liability coverage may apply if your pet damages someone else’s property.
