If saving on premiums is your main priority, State Farm comes out ahead. Its [average annual cost](https://www.moneygeek.com/insurance/homeowners/progressive-vs-state-farm-home-insurance/#cost-comparison) is roughly $2,454, while Progressive’s is about $5,445. Still, premiums are highly personal; your home’s age, location and credit standing all influence how much you’ll actually pay.
For service and claims experience, State Farm takes the lead. It earns a 4.58 out of 5 for [customer satisfaction](https://www.moneygeek.com/insurance/homeowners/progressive-vs-state-farm-home-insurance/#customer-service-comparison), compared with Progressive’s 3.87, according to J.D. Power data. Homeowners consistently report that State Farm handles claims faster and provides more consistent agent support.
