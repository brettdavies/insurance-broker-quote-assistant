## Progressive vs. State Farm: Which Company is Cheaper Overall?

**State Farm is far more affordable than Progressive**, with an [average home insurance premium](https://www.moneygeek.com/insurance/homeowners/average-cost-of-home-insurance/) of $2,454 per year compared to Progressive’s $5,445. Actual rates can still vary depending on your home’s location, coverage limits and credit history.

### State Farm & Progressive Insurance: FAQ

Comparing State Farm and Progressive means weighing what matters most to you: reliable service, lower premiums or flexible policy options. Comparing both companies helps you see which one fits your home and budget best. Here are some of the most common questions homeowners ask when deciding between them.

### Which company offers lower rates?

If saving on premiums is your main priority, State Farm comes out ahead. Its [average annual cost](#cost-comparison) is roughly $2,454, while Progressive’s is about $5,445. Still, premiums are highly personal; your home’s age, location and credit standing all influence how much you’ll actually pay.

### Who delivers better customer support?

For service and claims experience, State Farm takes the lead. It earns a 4.58 out of 5 for [customer satisfaction](#customer-service-comparison), compared with Progressive’s 3.87, according to J.D. Power data. Homeowners consistently report that State Farm handles claims faster and provides more consistent agent support.

### Which company offers more home insurance discounts?

Progressive offers more ways to save overall, with discounts for early quoting, paperless billing, automatic payments and new-home purchases. State Farm’s savings are more region-specific, focusing on home safety and risk reduction, such as roofing and wildfire mitigation discounts.

### Which insurer is the stronger choice overall?

State Farm stands out for its mix of affordability and top-tier service, while Progressive appeals to those who prefer flexibility and digital convenience. Both are solid options, but if you value lower premiums and a dependable claims process, State Farm is likely the better fit.

### Progressive & State Farm Home Insurance Rates: Our Methodology

MoneyGeek analyzed State Farm and Progressive home insurance using a single benchmark profile to ensure a fair, data-driven comparison across cost, service and coverage features.

We based our review on this representative homeowner:

* 40-year-old homeowner
* 2,500-square-foot single-family home
* $250,000 dwelling coverage
* $125,000 personal property coverage
* $200,000 liability coverage
* Good credit
* No home insurance claims in the past five years

To calculate overall scores, each company was evaluated using these weightings:

* **Affordability (55%)**: Compared each company’s average premiums against national benchmarks and key competitors.
* **Customer Experience (35%)**: Considered J.D. Power satisfaction data, claims service quality and ease of customer support.
* **Coverage (10%)**: Measured the range and distinctiveness of optional coverages offered by each provider.
