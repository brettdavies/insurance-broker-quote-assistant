The wrongful death case has been pending since December 2023 with the cement truck not listed on defendant's commercial auto policy.
Progressive’s commercial auto policy with Tycoon lists eight vehicles, but _“does not describe or list”_ the cement truck involved in the fatal accident, the insurer argued in court filings. The company maintains that the underlying negligence claims against Tycoon do not arise from the “ownership, maintenance, or use” of an automobile as required under the policy’s coverage terms.
The insurer also challenged coverage under a **“Form F”** endorsement designed to align with Texas transportation code requirements for commercial motor carriers. Progressive argued that while this endorsement provides coverage when Tycoon acts as an _“intrastate commercial motor carrier,”_ the underlying lawsuit alleges Tycoon hired Nava as an independent contractor rather than operating as a motor carrier itself.
_“Accordingly, Tycoon was not acting as a commercial motor carrier as envisioned by Texas law at the time of the accident,”_ Progressive stated in its filing.
