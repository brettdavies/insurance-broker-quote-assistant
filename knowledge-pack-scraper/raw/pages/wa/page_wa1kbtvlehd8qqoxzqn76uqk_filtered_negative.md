## How to Find the Cheapest Rates in New York
Here are some tips to ensure you find the most affordable auto insurance in New York:
* **Compare Quotes:** Always get quotes from multiple providers to see which offers the best rates for your specific situation. Get quotes from at least three providers for the same coverage.
* **Ask About Discounts:** Many providers offer discounts for safe driving, bundling home and auto insurance, and having anti-theft devices. Speak to a representative from your provider and make sure all discounts are being applied.
* **Consider Increasing Your Deductible:** A higher deductible can lower your premium, but be sure it fits within your budget in case of a claim. In general, a $1,000 deductible is a good middle ground, although ultimately, the decision will depend upon your personal finances.
* **Drive Safely:** Participating in telematics programs or staying accident-free can lead to lower premiums over time. Maintaining a clean driving record not only helps with your insurance rates but also promotes overall safety on the road.

## Minimum Car Insurance Requirements in New York
New York requires the following minimum auto insurance coverage for all drivers:
* Bodily injury liability: $25,000 per person/$50,000 per accident
* Property damage liability: $10,000 per accident
* Uninsured/underinsured motorist: $25,000 per person/$50,000 per accident
* [Personal injury protection](https://www.autoinsurance.com/states/new-york/pip-insurance/): $50,000 per person
Your liability coverage must be in effect until your vehicle registration expires, even if you don’t use your vehicle. It also must be New York State insurance coverage.[4](https://www.autoinsurance.com/states/new-york/cheap-car-insurance/#citation-4)
## Frequently Asked Questions
How can I lower my car insurance in New York?
![Navigation chevron](https://www.autoinsurance.com/assets/icons/icn-chevron-down.svg)
To lower your car insurance in New York, start by comparing quotes from multiple providers. Insurance rates can vary significantly, so getting several quotes will help you find the best deal for your coverage needs. Additionally, consider raising your deductible: This can lower your monthly premium. Just be sure you’re comfortable with the higher out-of-pocket costs in the event that you need to file a claim. Another way to reduce costs is by asking your insurance provider about available discounts, such as those for safe driving, bundling policies, installing anti-theft devices, or even for taking a defensive driving course. Finally, consider adjusting your coverage to reflect your current needs. How long can you go without insurance in New York?
![Navigation chevron](https://www.autoinsurance.com/assets/icons/icn-chevron-down.svg)
* In New York, you cannot go without insurance on a registered vehicle for any length of time without facing potential penalties. However, the severity of the consequences depends on the duration of the lapse:
* 1-90 days: You can pay a civil penalty to avoid license and registration suspension. The daily rates are:
* $8 per day for days 1-30
* $10 per day for days 31-60
* $12 per day for days 61-90
* 91 days or more: Your license and registration will be automatically suspended for a period equal to the duration of the lapse. You’ll also need to pay a $50 suspension termination fee to reinstate your license.
