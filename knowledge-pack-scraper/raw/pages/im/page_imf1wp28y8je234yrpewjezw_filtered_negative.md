Hurricane insurance refers to coverage you can add to your home insurance policy to cover damage caused by storms. Typically, hurricane insurance covers wind damage, damage to personal property, and additional living expenses if you can’t stay in your home. If you live in Florida, learning about hurricane insurance can help you [prepare for storms](https://www.palmparadiserealty.com/blog/how-to-prepare-your-home-for-hurricane-season/) while enjoying peace of mind all year long.
**Hurricane insurance isn’t a separate insurance plan. Instead, it refers to various coverages in homeowners insurance policies that protect against damage caused by hurricanes and other high-wind events.** This insurance is generally recommended for people [living by the beach](https://www.palmparadiserealty.com/blog/living-near-the-beach-pros-cons/) or in high flood-risk communities.
Flood damage is typically excluded from standard homeowners insurance. Therefore, homeowners must purchase a separate flood insurance policy to cover flood damage. This omission can lead to significant financial risks during hurricanes, highlighting the need for adequate flood insurance. [Ask about flood history](https://www.palmparadiserealty.com/blog/questions-to-ask-when-buying-waterfront-homes/) when buying a waterfront home to make a more informed purchase.
**The[National Flood Insurance Program (NFIP)](https://www.fema.gov/flood-insurance) and some private insurers offer flood insurance to fill in gaps left by your base home policy.** Mortgage lenders may require homeowners in high-risk flood areas to carry flood insurance.
Homeowners in hurricane-prone areas should confirm that their policies provide adequate personal property coverage.**This coverage protects belongings from hurricane damage.** Opting for replacement cost value coverage instead of actual cash value ensures maximum coverage for personal property.
Additional Living Expenses (ALE) cover costs exceeding normal living expenses when a home becomes uninhabitable after a disaster.**Homeowners insurance typically covers these, including temporary lodging, increased meal costs, and other expenses while displaced.** Homeowners remain responsible for normal expenses, like mortgage payments, during this time.
* For example, if your home is insured for $300,000 and you have a 2% hurricane deductible, you must pay the first $6,000 of hurricane damage before your insurance kicks in.
Reviewing your insurance policies regularly and consulting with professionals can help you tailor your coverage to meet your needs. Stay prepared and proactive to safeguard your home so you can enjoy the many [benefits of coastal living](https://www.palmparadiserealty.com/blog/health-benefits-coastal-living/).
A hurricane deductible is a specific amount applied to claims resulting from hurricanes. It is typically based on a percentage of the property's insured value. It can be paired with [FEMA programs for storm damage](https://www.palmparadiserealty.com/blog/fema-50-percent-rule/) to provide the most comprehensive coverage.
