* ### Minimum car insurance required in Michigan

  Michigan residents must carry liability insurance, personal property protection (known as PPI) and personal injury protection (known as PIP).

  You must select at least the following amounts:

  + $50,000 bodily injury liability per person.
  + $100,000 bodily injury liability per incident.
  + $1 million PPI for incidents that occur within Michigan.
  + $10,000 property damage liability per incident outside Michigan.
  + $250,000\* personal injury protection.

  \*Lower PIP limits available for certain Medicare and Medicaid recipients.

  [Liability car insurance](https://www.nerdwallet.com/article/insurance/auto/liability-car-insurance) covers the cost of any damage, injuries or deaths you cause in a car accident, up to your policy‚Äôs predefined limits.

  Property protection insurance, or PPI, pays to fix damage you cause to others‚Äô property, such as a fence or garage, while driving, or damage you cause to others‚Äô parked cars. It will pay up to $1 million for repairs if you‚Äôre driving in Michigan and damage another resident‚Äôs property.

  [Personal injury protection](https://www.nerdwallet.com/article/insurance/personal-injury-protection) will cover your medical bills and expenses if you‚Äôre injured in a car crash, up to your policy limits, no matter whose fault it was. Depending on your state, PIP may also pay for:

  + Lost wages if you‚Äôre unable to work due to injuries from an accident.
  + Services you can no longer perform, like house cleaning or child care, because of your injuries.
  + Funeral costs if an accident-related injury leads to death.
  + A small death benefit as a cash payout.

  Experts recommend buying more than the bare minimum liability insurance to avoid having to pay out of pocket for damage that exceeds your policy‚Äôs limits.

  Check out NerdWallet‚Äôs guide to [car insurance requirements by state](https://www.nerdwallet.com/article/insurance/minimum-car-insurance-requirements) to learn more about how the required and optional coverages of your Michigan auto policy work.

#### Risks of minimum car insurance

While it might seem like Michigan requires a lot of coverage, many drivers actually need more than the minimum amount. State requirements can be low and leave you on the hook for medical bills, damaged property and more if you‚Äôre in a serious accident. Unless you can pay those bills out of pocket, you‚Äôd benefit from full coverage.

Getting the least amount of car insurance possible might also mean missing out on optional coverage types that could help pay for damage in common situations. For example, full coverage would help cover damage to your car if it was vandalized, or if you slid off the road during a snowstorm.
