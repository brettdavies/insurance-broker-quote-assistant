Understanding the NAIC Number

The National Association of Insurance Commissioners (NAIC) is the U.S. standard-setting and regulatory support organization created and governed by the chief insurance regulators from the 50 states, the District of Columbia, and five U.S. territories. One of its key roles is to assign a unique identifier, known as the NAIC number, to each licensed insurance company. This number is critical for identifying and distinguishing between insurance companies, facilitating the exchange of information, and ensuring compliance with insurance regulations.
