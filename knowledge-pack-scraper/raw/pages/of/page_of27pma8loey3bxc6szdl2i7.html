Key Takeaways

  • For homeowners with a higher-risk credit score, such as in the low 600s, State Farm is one of the best options, based on our average rates analysis.
  • This provider ranks at No. 3 overall for the best home insurance and at No. 2 for the cheapest options, with a sample monthly rate of $129. However, it came in last place in our consumer survey for customer service, value, and claims handling.
  • State Farm adjusts your coverage for inflation annually at no extra cost. Add-on endorsement options include coverage for sewer line backup, identity theft, and home appliances. Flood insurance is only available through the government-run NFIP.

Despite low scores in our consumer survey, State Farm ranks at No. 3 overall in our best home insurance rating, based on its low cost, coverage options, discounts, and more.

On our cheapest home insurance list, this provider has a sample rate of $129 per month, which is second only to Amica’s $97 average premium. State Farm also offers several discounts; however, it did not qualify for our ranking of the best home and auto bundles.

State Farm’s coverage is fairly standard, insuring your dwelling, other structures, personal belongings, liability, and loss of use, as well as other additional coverages that vary by policy. Inflation coverage typically comes standard with State Farm home insurance.

State Farm operates mostly nationwide but does not currently offer new home insurance policies in California, Massachusetts, or Rhode Island. You can get a quote and buy a policy either online or through a local agent. Use the website or app to manage your policy, pay your bill, and file and track claims.

Insights from our consumer survey

State Farm placed last out of 10 companies for how it handles claims, scoring 3.53 out of 5; as a point of comparison, Amica topped the list with a score of 3.94 and completely satisfied 45% of policyholders with its claims resolution. Only 33% of State Farm customers were completely satisfied with how their claims were resolved, while 28% were somewhat satisfied and 32% felt neutral.

As for other categories related to claims, 30% of State Farm respondents were completely satisfied with the status updates on their claim, and only 26% were completely satisfied with the ease of filing.

State Farm came in last place for this category as well, scoring 3.81 out of 5 for customer service, compared to first-place Amica’s 4.35. While 58% of Amica’s consumers were completely satisfied with their customer service experience, only 38% of State Farm respondents felt completely satisfied. More than half felt somewhat satisfied or neutral about State Farm’s customer service. The ease of contacting customer service received a slightly higher satisfaction rating, as 40% were completely satisfied.

State Farm received a score of 3.92 out of 5 and a ranking of No. 7 out of 10 for its customer loyalty, given how respondents evaluated their likelihood of recommending the company and renewing their policy. Amica, in first place, has a score of 4.35, with 53% of its customers very likely to recommend Amica and 62% very likely to renew. By contrast, 38% of State Farm policyholders were very likely to recommend that provider, and 46% were very likely to renew their current policy.

State Farm ranks at No. 10 out of 10 for customer satisfaction with its value, scoring only 3.55 out of 5. Amica secured the No. 1 spot with a score of 3.86. State Farm completely satisfied 35% of customers with its value and somewhat satisfied 26%. A proportion of 10% reported some level of dissatisfaction.

Best for Low Credit

State Farm is worth considering if you’re concerned about how your credit score may affect the cost of your insurance. Typically, homeowners with lower credit scores are considered higher-risk and have to pay a more expensive premium. When we compared the average rates for homeowners with a 720 credit score to homeowners with a 620 credit score, the difference was about $50 per month. Compared to other providers, State Farm had one of the lowest cost increases based on credit score.

State Farm offers a relatively basic home insurance policy with protections for your home and personal property, plus a type of coverage rarely included in standard homeowners insurance.

Dwelling Coverage

After a covered claim, this pays to repair or rebuild your home and its attached structures, such as a garage. Dwelling insurance will cover some things inside your home, like flooring, walls, roof, and cabinetry.

Other Structures

This coverage protects structures on your property that are not directly attached to your house, such as a shed or detached garage.

Personal Property

This pays for your personal property to be repaired or replaced if damaged or stolen. Personal property coverage includes things not attached to your property, such as furniture or clothing.

Personal Liability

This protects against legal liability for bodily injury or property damage if an incident occurs on your property.

Guest Medical Payments

Also referred to as medical payments for others, this coverage helps pay the medical bills of someone injured on your property.

Additional Living Expenses

Loss of use coverage helps pay additional living expenses if you can’t live in your home after a covered peril.

Inflation Coverage

Inflation coverage automatically adjusts your coverage annually to account for inflation. While this coverage typically costs extra with other insurers, it is standard at State Farm.

Other Included Coverages

Depending on your policy, your coverage may also include credit card or bank card forgery, debris removal, trees and landscaping, volcanic action, refrigerated products, locks and remote devices, power interruption, and more.

Along with the standard coverages listed above, State Farm offers some optional endorsements or separate policies to enhance your coverage.

Water Backup

The Back-up of Sewer or Drain Endorsement provides some coverage against water damage when water backs up from the sewers or drains.

Home Systems Protection

Appliances that have broken down or become worn out over time are usually not covered by insurance, but you may be able to get some additional protection with State Farm’s Home Systems Protection.

Identity Theft

State Farm’s identity restoration coverage can be added to your homeowners policy for an annual fee of $25 to help pay for expenses related to identity fraud.

Personal Articles

If you want more personal property coverage than your home insurance policy provides, consider purchasing a separate personal articles policy. It offers replacement cost coverage to protect belongings like jewelry, sports equipment, and medical devices.

Earthquake Coverage

State Farm does not generally cover earthquake damage, but depending on where you live, you may have the option to add an endorsement to your policy or purchase a separate policy.

Flood Insurance

Standard home insurance typically excludes coverage for flood damage, but you could be at risk of flooding even if you don’t live in a high-risk flood zone. State Farm does not offer flood insurance, but you can protect your home with a separate flood insurance policy through the Federal Emergency Management Agency’s National Flood Insurance Program.

Umbrella Insurance

For extra liability coverage beyond the limits of your homeowners policy, you can buy a separate umbrella insurance policy. It can protect you from personal claims, such as libel, that may not be covered under your home insurance.

As with most providers, State Farm’s standard home insurance does not cover losses from earthquakes or flooding. You can get a separate flood policy through the government-run NFIP and ask your agent about your coverage options for earthquakes. Water backup damage is not covered unless you specifically add an endorsement.

Exclusions may vary by policy, but you typically cannot get coverage for damage from gradual deterioration over time, infestations of pests like rodents, neglect, malicious intentions, contamination, and nuclear hazards. Business-related pursuits and vehicle liability are also generally excluded from your standard homeowners policy.

State Farm has the No. 2 most affordable home insurance premium in our study of the cheapest homeowners insurance companies. The sample monthly premium for its homeowners policy is $129. This rate is for a policy with a dwelling coverage limit of $300,000 and a liability coverage limit of $100,000.

The cost of your premium will depend on many different variables, including the policy’s coverage limits and deductible, your age and credit score, the age and location of your home, your claims history, and more. It’s best to compare quotes from various providers and see what discounts are available before deciding.

State Farm offers many opportunities to save on your homeowners insurance premium.

Certain protective devices and security systems may qualify you for discounts, such as fire, smoke, and burglar alarms or an automatic indoor sprinkler system.

If you don’t file any claims for a certain period of time, you may become eligible for a lower rate.

Bundling your home and auto policies may save you some money.

If you update your home’s utilities with a qualified contractor, you may be able to get a cheaper premium.

You may save by using certain impact-resistant roofing products to protect your home. Check with your insurance agent before installing the shingles to ensure it will qualify.

If your home meets specific building construction standards that would protect it against wind damage, you may save on your premium. Availability varies by state.

This is specifically for California homeowners who currently hold a policy with State Farm. You can receive a discount for taking specific steps to protect your home from wildfires.

On State Farm’s website, you can apply for a quote or search for a local agent. Getting an online quote may be faster, but we recommend speaking to an agent if you’re insuring a new property and need help determining your coverage limits.

To get a quote, you’ll provide your house address, your date of birth, and the coverage start date. State Farm will access public records to fill in information about your home, and once you confirm it, you’ll be asked for more specific information.

Be prepared to provide details such as the year your house was built, its square footage, the number of stories, other structures on the property, safety features, and the number of people living there full time. You should also be able to describe your insurance history, which includes the name of your last provider, the dates of prior coverage, and any home insurance claims you’ve made in the last five years.

Once you receive a quote, you can finalize it and buy your policy either online or through your agent. If you go through this process online, you have up to 30 days after receiving your quote to buy the policy. Inform your mortgage lender once you buy a new policy, and don’t cancel your existing one until your new insurance takes effect.

You can file a claim with State Farm online, via the mobile app, or by calling 800-732-5246.

Before you start your claim, try to mitigate any further damage to your property, and take any notes, photos, and videos that may help State Farm process your claim. Keep any relevant documents, such as receipts for emergency repairs. Additionally, if you regularly maintain a home inventory that lists all your belongings, you can expedite the claims process when you experience a covered loss.

Once you file, State Farm will confirm receipt of your claim and assign you to a claim handler or team. They will reach out with questions to assess the damage and determine whether your policy will cover it.

If the claims representative concludes that you are owed a settlement, you may be able to choose from either direct deposit or a check. You can then use your settlement money to pay contractors or reimburse yourself if you’ve already done so.

Mold is typically excluded from homeowners insurance coverage. That’s because mold is commonly caused by a lack of maintenance, which isn’t a covered peril. Generally, you’ll need to show that the mold is caused by a sudden incident covered in your policy, such as a burst pipe or storm damage to a roof. You can speak to a State Farm agent for more details about events where mold might be covered under your policy.

State Farm homeowners policies cover water damage from perils included under your policy, such as a faulty appliance, water from fighting a fire, etc. Not all water damage is eligible to be covered, but you can purchase extra protection with an endorsement for water backup from sewers or drains and a separate flood insurance policy through FEMA’s National Flood Insurance Program.

Typically, homeowners policies cover roof leaks caused by sudden, accidental events such as storm damage or a tree falling onto the roof. However, leaks caused by lack of maintenance or typical wear and tear aren’t covered.

Your State Farm homeowners policy includes coverage for theft of your personal property. There are limits to the amount of coverage you’ll receive in a standard policy, so if you have more expensive items such as jewelry or a collection of antiques, you can also purchase a personal articles policy.

If a tree falls due to a covered event like a storm and damages your property, your insurance usually covers it. Also, if the tree lands on a covered structure such as your house or a detached garage, tree removal is also covered. Most policies do not pay for damage to the tree itself.

Homeowners policies generally cover damage from a fire and smoke damage, though each policy and claim will be handled differently. Depending on the extent of the damage, both dwelling protection and personal property coverage may apply.

The key exceptions are preventable fires, such as arson or failure to maintain a chimney. In these cases, your policy is unlikely to cover the damages.

You can cancel your State Farm home insurance policy by phone, mail, or in person. Cancellations by phone are effective immediately, or you can schedule it for a specific date. If you write a letter, mail it out at least 2 weeks before your intended cancellation date.

Speak to an agent for more guidance on the cancellation process.

Yes, State Farm is a good insurance company, ranking third in our best homeowners insurance companies rating and second in our cheapest homeowners insurance companies study. However, get a quote, review coverage options, and pricing to determine if it's a good company for your needs.
