## Cheapest Motorcycle Insurance Companies in Illinois

With median rates of $29 monthly and $344 per year, Progressive is the cheapest overall motorcycle insurer in Illinois. We also recommend looking into Harley Davidson, Geico, Shelter Insurance and Dairyland for affordable rates also.

| Cheapest Provider | Median Monthly Premium | Median Annual Premium |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Progressive | $29 | $344 |
| Harley Davidson | $33 | $394 |
| Geico | $41 | $493 |
| Shelter Insurance | $42 | $501 |
| Dairyland | $44 | $522 |

*\*Rates are a combined average of minimum and full coverage premiums for a 40-year-old rider driving a 2023 Honda CRF450RL with 5 years of riding experience and no driving violations.*

## How Much Is Motorcycle Insurance in Illinois?

The average annual cost of motorcycle insurance in Illinois is around $205 for minimum coverage and $901 for full coverage. These costs make the state the 45th in affordability for minimum coverage and the 5th most expensive overall for full coverage motorcycle policies.

## How To Save on Illinois Motorcycle Insurance

Below we'll detail key tips to help you save more on motorcycle insurance in Illinois.

![insurance2 icon](https://res.cloudinary.com/moneygeek/image/upload/v1585171420/assets/components/icon/insurance_2_iixd7m.svg)

**Choose a Coverage Level Beforehand**

Knowing what coverage you actually need can save you from being convinced you need a higher-level policy than necessary.

![find icon](https://res.cloudinary.com/moneygeek/image/upload/v1585171420/assets/components/icon/find_nzkmut.svg)

**Compare Multiple Providers**

In order to get the best pricing, comparing multiple providers apples to apples in terms of coverage can help you save up to 41% on motorcycle policies in Illinois. We recommend that you not only compare multiple companies, but also use multiple means

![money icon](https://res.cloudinary.com/moneygeek/image/upload/v1585171420/assets/components/icon/money_kmo8zz.svg)

**Take Advantage of Discounts**

Many discounts are available to help you save including a pay-in-full, autopay, and military/membership reduction for most providers. Bundling motorcycle insurance with other policies is also another great way to save if you want to have coverage with your current provider.

![driverLicense icon](https://res.cloudinary.com/moneygeek/image/upload/v1585171420/assets/components/icon/driver-license_qzgm49.svg)

**Get a Full Motorcycle License**

Getting a full motorcycle license instead of just an endorsement can save you a large sum on insurance for your bike. It works as a signal to insurers that you are less risky than other drivers, lowing premiums overall.

## Cheap Motorcycle Insurance Illinois: Bottom Line

Our top three picks for affordable motorcycle insurance in Illinois are Progressive, Harley Davidson and Geico. While these are our recommendations, we urge you to compare options for yourself to get the best deal possible.

### Illinois Motorcycle Insurance: FAQ

Below we've answered some frequently asked questions about Illinois motorcycle insurance:

### Which company has the cheapest car insurance in Illinois?

For minimum coverage policies in Illinois, Progressive offers the lowest cost policies in the state. However, for full coverage, Harley Davidson is the most affordable option.

### How much does motorcycle insurance cost for most riders in Illinois?

The average cost of motorcycle insurance in Illinois is $901 per year or $77 monthly for full coverage policies and $205 annually or $17 monthly for liability-only policies.

### Do you need motorcycle insurance in Illinois?

Yes, Illinois requires that all riders have motorcycle insurance with at least 25/50/20 in liability coverage.

### How We Chose The Cheapest Motorcycle Insurance in Illinois

We chose our cheapest providers based on the following profile for motorcycle riders:

* 40 years old
* Drives a 2023 Honda CRF450RL
* Has a valid motorcycle endorsement
* Clean driving record

For minimum coverage we used state minimum requirements and for full coverage policies, we used a 100/300/50 liability split and a $1,000 deductible for collision and comprehensive insurance.
