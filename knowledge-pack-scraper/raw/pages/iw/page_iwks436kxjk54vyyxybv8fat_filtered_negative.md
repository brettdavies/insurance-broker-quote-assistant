With all the different insurance policy options available, it's easy to get confused when shopping for insurance. To simplify the process, you may consider using an insurance broker.
Insurance brokers can help you compare multiple insurers to find the best fit (and price) for your lifestyle. While you can use an insurance broker to help you find all types of policies, you're most likely going to use a broker to shop for [life insurance](https://www.businessinsider.com/personal-finance/life-insurance/best-life-insurance-companies), [homeowners insurance](https://www.businessinsider.com/personal-finance/homeowners-insurance/best-homeowners-insurance-companies), and [auto insurance](https://www.businessinsider.com/personal-finance/auto-insurance/best-car-insurance-companies).
## Understanding the Role of an Insurance Broker
### What Does an Insurance Broker Do?
An insurance broker is someone who connects you with an insurance provider. Insurance brokers help consumers compare multiple insurance companies to find the best policy to meet their coverage needs.
A broker for insurance can also share expert advice on choosing the right coverage and help you better understand your policy. While anyone can use an insurance broker, they're often most helpful for those with complex insurance needs, such as business owners or someone who owns multiple cars or homes.
### How Insurance Brokers Differ from Insurance Agents
Insurance brokers differ from insurance agents in that they represent you (the consumer), whereas an insurance agent usually works for an insurance company. Insurance brokers will help you shop around and compare insurers. Alternatively, an insurance agent will likely try to sell you a policy from the company they represent.
### Types of Insurance Brokers (Independent vs. Captive Brokers)
Insurance brokers are usually classified by the type of coverage they specialize in. For instance, some insurance brokers are experts in finding you car insurance, and others have more experience in the homeowners insurance market.
Additionally, brokers for insurance can be categorized as independent or captive. Independent insurance brokers work with more than one insurer. Captive insurance brokers work with only one insurance company and can only sell you policies from that provider.
Unless you already know you want to work with a specific insurance company, it's smart to work with an independent broker to weigh your options.
## Benefits of Using an Insurance Broker
While using an insurance broker isn't exactly necessary, in the right situation, their expertise and experience can save you time and help you get more value for your money.
### How Brokers Help You Find the Best Policy
Brokers are an excellent resource to help you navigate the insurance market and find the best deals. Insurance companies are more likely to negotiate better terms with an insurance broker than a single customer, because the broker is more likely to bring their company more business.
With that in mind, insurance brokers can generally get the most competitive rates due to the potential for new business.
### Why Insurance Brokers Offer More Options Than Agents
Insurance agents typically only work for one company, so they're only able to offer products from their specific company. On the other hand, insurance brokers tend to work with multiple insurance companies.
Brokers work on behalf of you, the client, not a company. This means that they are able to access a broader range of options to tailor recommendations to meet your needs more effectively.
### Saving Money and Time by Working with a Broker
Working with an insurance broker will likely save you both time and money. Insurance brokers are highly knowledgeable professionals in their industry and understand the complexities of different policies. By leveraging a broker's knowledge, you'll have a better chance of finding the most cost-effective options, potentially saving you money on premiums.
In a way, brokers are like an insurance concierge. Insurance brokers do the legwork for you — they gather quotes, compare coverage, and handle paperwork, making the process more efficient than trying to do everything yourself. Rather than spending hours researching policies, you can rely on a broker for insurance to guide you to the best options.
## Types of Insurance Brokers
Insurance brokers vary by the type of insurance they specialize in and the number of insurance providers they work with. Captive insurance brokers only work with one insurer, whereas independent insurance brokers work with multiple providers.
You'll want to search for an insurance broker who specializes in the type of insurance you need. For instance, if you're in the market for homeowners insurance, you'll want to look for a broker with experience in that particular field of insurance rather than a broker specializing in another field, such as travel insurance.
Understanding the various types of brokers can ensure you find the right professional for your needs.
### Home Insurance Brokers
Home insurance brokers are focused on finding coverage for homeowners and renters. They can help you find a policy to protect your property, personal belongings, and liability against risks such as fire, theft, or natural disasters. A reputable home insurance broker will assess your property's needs and find a policy that makes sure you are fully covered in the event of damage or a loss.
### Auto Insurance Brokers
Auto insurance brokers specialize in finding car insurance policies that offer the best value for your needs based on what kind of driver you are and where you live.
Regardless of whether you're insuring a single car or multiple cars, an auto insurance broker can help you compare different plans, including coverage for accidents, theft, and damage. This type of broker's expertise can help you find affordable premiums while ensuring you're adequately covered when you're on the road.
### Business & Commercial Insurance Brokers
A quality insurance policy guards businesses from incredibly expensive incidents. Commercial insurance brokers assist companies in finding coverage for a variety of potential risks, such as property damage, liability claims, employee injuries, and more.
Professionals specializing in business insurance will work with you to understand the specific needs of your business and build a policy that provides comprehensive protection at a competitive rate.
### Life & Health Insurance Brokers
Life and health insurance brokers assist individuals in selecting the right life or health coverage.
These brokers for insurance are experts in explaining complex policies and can offer guidance on how to choose the best coverage based on your health needs, family, and financial goals. For example, if you want your life insurance policy to accrue value and support your financial goals while still guaranteeing a death benefit for your family, your agent can find the best policy for those purposes, like a [variable universal life insurance ](https://www.businessinsider.com/personal-finance/life-insurance/variable-life-insurance)policy.
So, if you're looking to buy individual health insurance, [family coverage](https://www.businessinsider.com/personal-finance/life-insurance/best-family-life-insurance-companies), or a life insurance policy to replace any income lost from your death, life and health insurance brokers are available to get you the best deal.
## How to Choose the Best Insurance Broker
Partnering with the right insurance broker will guarantee you receive the best value and coverage for your situation.
### What to Look for in an Insurance Broker
Here are factors worth considering when selecting an insurance broker:
* Strong reputation with positive reviews
* Experience in the specific field of insurance you're shopping for
* Valid license and registration to broker insurance (requirements may vary by state)
* Professionalism
In addition, an insurance broker should offer you personalized service, take time to explain policies in terms that you understand, and provide options from multiple insurers.
### Questions to Ask Before Hiring a Broker
Before you hire an insurance broker, you should ask the following questions:
* What type of insurance do you have experience in?
* How much experience do you have?
* Which insurance providers do you work with?
* How are you compensated for your work?
Be sure to ask about any fees, commissions, or potential conflicts of interest prior to any work being done. Understanding your insurance broker's approach and how they will work on your behalf can better help you make an informed decision.
### How Brokers Earn Commissions & What It Means for You
Insurance brokers typically earn commissions from the insurers they work with, which is usually a percentage of the premium you pay. However, some brokers may charge you a fee.
While this shouldn't impact your premium, it's helpful to understand how brokers make money so that you can determine whether their recommendations are truly the best for your needs or if they're pushing you toward a certain policy for their own purposes.
## How Insurance Brokers Find the Best Policies
Insurance brokers use their expertise and industry connections to find the best policies for their clients. They have insights into which insurers offer the most competitive rates and the most comprehensive coverage, making them an invaluable resource when it comes to comparing various insurance providers.
### Comparing Multiple Insurance Providers
One of the main roles of an insurance broker is comparing policies from multiple insurers. This is how they find the best value and coverage. Brokers will do the research by reviewing different options, including premiums, coverage limits, and policy terms, to maximize convenience for you.
### Customizing Coverage Based on Your Needs
One of the biggest advantages of using an insurance broker is that they can find you a policy to match your specific needs. Whether you need additional coverage for your vintage car collection or you're a landlord with several properties, brokers can customize policies to make certain you have the right level of coverage.
## Insurance Brokers vs. Online Insurance Marketplaces
Online marketplaces have become popular tools for obtaining quotes quickly. However, they don't offer the same level of personalized service as an insurance broker.
These online insurance marketplaces, such as [SquareMouth](https://www.businessinsider.com/personal-finance/travel-insurance/squaremouth-travel-insurance-review), let you compare a limited range of options, whereas brokers provide you with access to what's often a wider range of insurance companies and work for you to find the best policy.
### When to Use an Insurance Broker vs. an Online Quote Tool
An online quote tool is especially helpful for you if you're looking for a simple policy and need a quote quickly. These tools are usually free to use and can provide you with a quote in as little as a few minutes. For example, [Progressive auto insurance](https://www.businessinsider.com/personal-finance/auto-insurance/progressive-car-insurance-review) offers a free online quote tool that saves customers an average of 7%.
When you need expert advice, personalized service, and access to a broad range of options, consider using an insurance broker. Brokers provide a more tailored experience, which is especially beneficial for more complex insurance needs.
## Pros and Cons of Working with an Insurance Broker
### Insurance Broker Pros
* Convenience of someone else doing the research and comparing insurers for you
* Access to policies from multiple insurance companies
* Personalized service from an industry expert
* A professional to negotiate the best rates and coverage
### Insurance Broker Cons
* Expensive fees
* Potential for biased recommendations
* There may be a limited number of brokers who specialize in the type of insurance you need
## How to Get an Insurance Quote from a Broker
### Steps to Request a Quote Through a Broker
Here are the steps to request a quote from an insurance broker:
1. Find a reputable broker. You can do this by searching the internet for a particular type of insurance broker in your area. For instance, if you're in the market for new car insurance, you would search for "Auto insurance brokers near me." It's not a requirement that an insurance broker be local. However, they will need the proper licensing to sell insurance in your state. Another excellent resource for finding a broker is asking your friends and family for referrals.
2. Contact the broker and provide basic information regarding the type of insurance you're looking to purchase. It's important to provide the most up-to-date and accurate information to your insurance broker so that they can find the best policy for your needs.
3. The broker will ask you specific questions to gain a better understanding of the type of coverage you need. For instance, a home insurance broker may ask you about the value of a property, or a life insurance broker may ask about dangerous activities you participate in or details about your medical history.
4. The insurance broker will research and compare policies from different insurance providers with your personal details and preferences.
5. Once the insurance broker has compiled several options, they will present them to you and answer any questions you may have about coverage.
6. You choose the policy you'd like to sign, and the insurance broker contacts the insurer on your behalf. At this time, you may be required to provide additional documentation depending on the type of insurance you're buying.
### How Long It Takes to Get a Quote Through a Broker
The amount of time it takes to get a quote will vary depending on the insurance broker you choose to work with and the complexity of your coverage needs. Simple, straightforward policies may take less than a day, whereas more complicated coverage could take several days or weeks.
Turnaround time is something you should discuss with your broker before they begin the search process, so that you can set your expectations appropriately.

## FAQs
###  What is the difference between an insurance broker and an insurance agent?
Chevron icon It indicates an expandable section or menu, or sometimes previous / next navigation options.
The difference between insurance brokers and insurance agents is that insurance brokers work with several insurers to find the best coverage for you, while insurance agents usually only represent and sell policies from one insurance.
###  How does an insurance broker get paid?
Chevron icon It indicates an expandable section or menu, or sometimes previous / next navigation options.
Insurance brokers typically get paid by earning a commission from an insurance company when they sell a policy. Some brokers charge additional fees, such as upfront service fees.
###  Is it cheaper to buy insurance through a broker?
Chevron icon It indicates an expandable section or menu, or sometimes previous / next navigation options.
The exact costs will depend on the insurance company and coverage you choose. While it's not guaranteed that your insurance will be cheaper when you buy through a broker, an insurance broker can help you find the best deal by comparing multiple insurance providers and policies.
###  Can I get a home and auto insurance bundle through a broker?
Chevron icon It indicates an expandable section or menu, or sometimes previous / next navigation options.
Yes, it's possible to bundle home and auto insurance through a broker. Many brokers specialize in bundling insurance policies to help you save on coverage.
###  How do I find a trustworthy insurance broker?
Chevron icon It indicates an expandable section or menu, or sometimes previous / next navigation options.
To find a trustworthy insurance broker, look for someone with strong customer reviews, valid licensing, and experience in the type of insurance you are looking to purchase. Independent brokers often offer the most options.
