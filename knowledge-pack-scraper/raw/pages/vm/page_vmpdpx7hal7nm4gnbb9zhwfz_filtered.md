## Full coverage vs. liability: Which is right for you?

Throughout this article, we refer to two kinds of car insurance: full coverage and liability coverage, also known as minimum coverage. Here's the difference.

### Full coverage

* **What it is.** [Full coverage](https://www.nerdwallet.com/article/insurance/full-coverage-insurance) is typically a combination of comprehensive, collision and liability coverage.
* **What it does.** It will help pay for repairs in most events, including damage caused by weather, an at-fault accident, hitting an animal or vandalism.
* **Do you need it?** If you have a car loan or lease, you may be required to have full coverage. You may also want it if you own a new car, drive a car you couldn‚Äôt afford to replace or live in a place with extreme weather.

### Liability coverage

* **What it is.** [Liability coverage](https://www.nerdwallet.com/article/insurance/auto/liability-car-insurance) is usually the cheapest car insurance available, but it can leave you open to major expenses.
* **What it does.** It pays for injuries and damage you cause to other drivers and their property, but not your own.
* **Do you need it?** Every state requires liability insurance, but it‚Äôs not enough for most people. Consider buying full coverage if you can afford it. Our list of companies with [cheap full coverage](https://www.nerdwallet.com/insurance/auto/cheap-full-coverage-insurance) can help.

* ### Should you get the cheapest car insurance?

  Just because a company has cheap rates doesn‚Äôt mean that it‚Äôs short on quality. We‚Äôve reviewed plenty of great car insurance companies that have cheap rates and high-quality service.

  But how can you tell if the great deal you found is from a legit company? Or that an insurer with affordable rates will actually be there for you after an accident?

  These three factors can help you decide whether a cheap insurance company may be a fit:

  + **It has the coverage you need.** If an insurer has affordable rates but doesn‚Äôt offer the type of coverage you need, it‚Äôs likely not the best option for you.
  + **It has great customer service.** Some cheap insurance companies have earned our highest ratings for customer service, but others haven‚Äôt. If you can, try to balance service with affordability.
  + **It has a reliable reputation.** You want to have peace of mind that an insurer will still be in business if you need it one day. Look for a car insurance company that combines cheap rates with long-term stability.

## How to get the cheapest car insurance quotes

1. **Think about your needs.** One person might prioritize a company with strong customer service and a great mobile app while someone else prefers a company with a lot of available discounts. Only you know which details mean the most to you.
2. **Do your research.** We recommend [comparing car insurance quotes](https://www.nerdwallet.com/insurance/auto/car-insurance) from at least three insurers once a year. Insurers adjust rates regularly, so a company that gave you a cheap rate last year might not be as much of a bargain this year.
3. ****Ask for discounts.**** You may be able to save money with discounts you‚Äôre not currently getting. Common [car insurance discounts](https://www.nerdwallet.com/article/insurance/car-insurance-discounts) are given for insuring multiple cars on the same policy and having a student driver with good grades.
4. **Raise your deductibles.** The higher your [car insurance deductibles](https://www.nerdwallet.com/article/insurance/car-insurance-deductible), the more you pay out of pocket before insurance pays for a claim. By raising your deductibles, you may be able to lower your premium, which is the amount you pay for your policy. Just make sure you have enough money set aside to cover the deductibles if you ever get in an accident.
5. **Improve driving habits.** Most traffic violations impact your insurance for at least three to five years, whether it was an accident, speeding ticket or DUI. Taking a defensive driving course could earn you a discount on your car insurance policy ‚Äî and it‚Äôll help you develop safer driving skills to reduce the risk of future violations.
6. **Reconsider your vehicle.** The type of car you drive affects how much you pay for insurance. Check out NerdWallet‚Äôs analysis of the [cheapest cars to insure](https://www.nerdwallet.com/article/insurance/cars-cheapest-auto-insurance-rates) to compare rates for the most popular vehicles.
