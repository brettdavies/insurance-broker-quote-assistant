But, there are two different levels of dwelling coverage to choose from: [actual cash value and replacement cost](https://www.valuepenguin.com/actual-cash-value-vs-replacement-cost "Actual Cash Value vs. Replacement Cost in Home Insurance").
* Standard [liability coverage](https://www.valuepenguin.com/homeowners-insurance/personal-liability-insurance "Personal Liability Insurance: How Much Does It Cost?") generally starts at $100,000.
* Your [personal property coverage](https://www.valuepenguin.com/homeowners-insurance/personal-property-insurance "What Is Personal Property Insurance?") limit is usually 20% to 50% of your dwelling coverage limit.
Your deductible is the amount you must pay toward fixing or rebuilding your home when you use your insurance. [Choosing a higher deductible](https://www.valuepenguin.com/homeowners-insurance-deductibles#how-does "How your homeowners insurance deductible affects your rate") means lowering your potential payout, which makes your rates cheaper.
**[Renovations that make your home safer or more resistant to damage](https://www.valuepenguin.com/consumers-save-on-home-insurance-after-renovations "Home Reno Could Save You $114 a Year on Home Insurance") could lower your rates.** This can include replacing an old roof or installing a security system.
**Many insurance companies view a good[credit score](https://www.valuepenguin.com/how-credit-scores-affect-homeowners-insurance-rates "How Credit Scores Can Affect Home Insurance Premiums") as a sign of financial stability.** In some states, companies may reward a good credit score with lower rates. In others, they may not be allowed to use credit scores to determine rates, but good credit scores can benefit you in other ways.
While [improving your credit score](https://www.valuepenguin.com/whats-a-good-credit-score#how-to-improve-credit-score "How to Get a Good Credit Score") takes time, it might help you get more affordable rates.
Liability insurance covers any injuries your pets may cause. If you have a particularly ["aggressive" breed as a pet](https://www.valuepenguin.com/homeowners-insurance-restricted-dog-breeds "Restricted Dog Breeds for Home Insurance"), your insurance company may increase your rates.
Your [claims history](https://www.valuepenguin.com/will-home-insurance-go-up-after-claim "Does Homeowners Insurance Go Up After a Claim?") often shows how likely you are to file a claim in the future. The more claims you make, the higher your rates. Reducing the number of claims you file by only filing for expensive repairs could help you keep your rates lower.
[Swimming pools](https://www.valuepenguin.com/how-pool-affects-your-homeowners-insurance "How a Pool Affects Your Homeowners Insurance") and trampolines can make your home insurance rates go up. That's because guests visiting your home are more likely to hurt themselves by slipping on a wet pool deck or falling off a trampoline.

But see which ones other companies offer, too. You may be able to get  multipolicy discounts, loyalty discounts or  home safety discounts, depending on your insurer.
