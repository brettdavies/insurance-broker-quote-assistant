Third-largest auto insurer to drop average premium by $400 between 2025 and 2026

Anne Geggis
Palm Beach Post

On the heels of Floridas largest auto insurance company rolling out a collective $1 billion rebate to its policyholders, the states third-largest auto insurer is announcing another billion in rate reductions.

State Farm Insurance auto insurance policyholders will be getting an average 10% drop in their premiums, according to an Oct. 30 announcement of rates the insurer filed with state regulators. Taken with premium reductions from a year ago, it adds up to Floridians saving $1 billion on premiums, or an average $400 in savings per vehicle, according to a news release from State Farm.
