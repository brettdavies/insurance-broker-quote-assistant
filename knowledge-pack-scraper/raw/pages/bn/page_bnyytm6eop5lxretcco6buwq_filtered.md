# Third-largest auto insurer to drop average premium by $400 between 2025 and 2026

[![Portrait of Anne Geggis](/gcdn/authoring/authoring-images/2024/08/13/NPPP/74787824007-img-0068.jpeg?crop=1050,1051,x0,y188&width=48&height=48&format=pjpg&auto=webp) Anne Geggis](/staff/74283169007/anne-geggis/)

Palm Beach Post

On the heels of [Florida’s largest](/story/business/economy/2025/10/22/florida-auto-reform-brings-1b-progressive-rebates-desantis-says/86834736007/) auto insurance company rolling out a collective $1 billion rebate to its [policyholders](/story/weather/hurricane/2025/06/26/new-citizens-policy-could-make-disputes-more-costly-for-policyholders/84240732007/), the state’s third-largest [auto insurer](/story/news/2024/12/20/pay-by-the-mile-car-insurance-should-you-get-it-heres-what-to-know/76703420007/) is announcing another billion in rate reductions.

State Farm Insurance auto insurance policyholders will be getting an average 10% drop in their premiums, according to an Oct. 30 announcement of rates the insurer filed with state regulators. Taken with premium reductions from a year ago, it adds up to Floridians saving $1 billion on premiums, or an average $400 in savings per vehicle, according to a news release from State Farm.
