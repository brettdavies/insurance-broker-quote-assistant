## Identifying the Need for SR-22 Insurance

If a court recently revoked your license for reckless or drunk driving charges, multiple speeding tickets, or missed child support payments, then you will be required to file an SR-22 to reinstate your driving privileges. What does an SR-22 look like? It’s a certificate that proves you carry the minimum required insurance.

To learn where to get an SR-22, contact Geico or another insurance provider that specializes in high-risk policies. [Can I keep auto insurance with a revoked driver’s license?](https://www.autoinsurance.org/can-i-keep-auto-insurance-while-my-driver-license-is-revoked/) Contact your local DMV to find out what does SR-22 mean.

> SR-22 filings are for high-risk drivers who cannot find coverage with standard auto insurance companies due to their increased risk. Contact your local DMV to find out if you need to file an SR-22.

What types of drivers need high-risk auto insurance coverage? High-risk drivers have charges on their driving or criminal records that either increase the likelihood of filing a claim, cost them their driving privileges, or both. Typically, driving without a valid license or up-to-date insurance coverage will result in license revocation. Multiple moving violations can also cost you your license.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What should I do before purchasing Geico SR-22 insurance?

Before purchasing Geico SR-22, it’s recommended to compare quotes from different high-risk auto insurance companies to ensure you’re getting the best rates. [Enter your ZIP code](#mdabottom) to view companies that offer cheap auto insurance rates and make an informed decision.

### Is SR-22 insurance more expensive than regular insurance?

Yes, SR-22 insurance is generally more expensive than regular insurance. On average, it can cost between $140 to $150 per month. However, rates can vary based on your location and driving record. Learn how to find [cheap auto insurance for a bad driving record](https://www.autoinsurance.org/cheap-auto-insurance-for-bad-driving-record/).

### Who offers the cheapest SR-22 insurance?

Geico and USAA are often considered the cheapest options for drivers with SR-22 filings, particularly for those with DUIs. However, USAA is only available to active and retired military members and their families. It’s essential to compare quotes from multiple insurers to find the most affordable coverage for your specific situation.

### How do I know if I need SR-22 insurance?

If your license has been revoked due to offenses like reckless driving, DUI, multiple speeding tickets, or missed child support payments, you will likely need to file an SR-22 to reinstate your driving privileges. Contact your local DMV to find out what does SR-22 mean and confirm whether you need to file one.

### Can I switch insurance companies while I have an SR-22 filing?

Yes, you can [switch auto insurance companies](https://www.autoinsurance.org/is-it-bad-to-switch-auto-insurance-companies/) while you have an SR-22 filing. However, it’s important to ensure that the new insurance company is willing to file the SR-22 on your behalf before canceling your existing policy. Be sure to get Geico insurance quotes online before making a decision.

### Can I get SR-22 insurance if I don’t own a car?

Yes, you can get non-owner SR-22 insurance from Geico if you don’t own a car but still need to meet the SR-22 filing requirements. This type of insurance provides liability coverage when you borrow or rent vehicles. Be sure to ask about how much is SR-22 insurance with Geico before purchasing the policy. Start by [entering your ZIP code](#mdabottom) to view Geico auto quote options and see which companies offer the best rates in your area.

### Can I remove the SR-22 requirement before my mandated filing period ends?

No, the SR-22 requirement must be maintained for the entire duration mandated by your state (usually 3 years). If the policy lapses or is canceled, your insurance company must inform the DMV, which could result in license suspension or other penalties.

### How will my insurance rates change after the SR-22 requirement is lifted?

Once the SR-22 filing period ends, it may [lower your auto insurance rates](https://www.autoinsurance.org/how-to-lower-your-auto-insurance-rates/), but this depends on your overall driving history. If you maintain a clean record, you may qualify for standard insurance rates again, though some violations may continue to affect your premium for several years.

### Does SR-22 insurance cover other drivers who use my car?

SR-22 insurance typically covers the policyholder, but depending on your coverage, it might extend to other drivers listed on your policy. Always clarify with your insurance provider if others who drive your vehicle are covered under the SR-22 filing.

### Can I file SR-22 insurance if I move to another state?

Yes, if you move to another state while still under SR-22 requirements, you must maintain SR-22 insurance in your new state. Ensure your new insurance provider files the SR-22 in both your previous and current states if necessary.
