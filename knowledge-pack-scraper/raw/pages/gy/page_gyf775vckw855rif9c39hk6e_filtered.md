Older adults often have specific auto insurance needs. Below are answers to common questions to help you find the best coverage and rates for your situation.

### Does car insurance increase at age 70?

Car insurance rates often increase for seniors after age 70 due to higher crash death rates. Age-related vision changes, physical ability and certain medications can also impact seniors' driving skills.

### What car insurance discounts are offered to seniors?

Car insurance discounts for seniors include age-based benefits (50+ or 55+), retirement discounts, safe driving course rewards, low-mileage rates, AARP membership savings, usage-based programs, safety feature discounts and policy bundling. Discounts range from 5% to 20%, with top providers being AARP/The Hartford, GEICO, Allstate, Liberty and Farmers, though offerings vary by location.

### Does reduced driving after retirement affect car insurance rates for seniors?

Seniors who drive less after retirement can get cheaper car insurance rates through low-mileage discounts or pay-per-mile programs. Many insurers offer reduced premiums for seniors who drive under a certain threshold annually, with some companies using usage-based insurance to track actual miles driven.

### How much coverage do most seniors need?

Most financial experts recommend seniors maintain 100/300/100 liability coverage ($100,000 per person, $300,000 per accident for injuries, $100,000 for property damage) to protect their assets. ### Is it worth switching insurance companies as a senior?

Seniors shopping around for new quotes every six to 12 months can get cheaper car insurance rates since companies continually adjust their rating factors. Seniors who switch carriers often save hundreds of dollars annually on their car insurance premiums, especially those who have been with the same insurer for many years without comparing rates.

### How does taking a defensive driving course affect senior insurance rates?

Completing an approved defensive driving or mature driver course can save seniors 5% to 15% on their premiums. These courses refresh driving skills and demonstrate to insurers that you're committed to safe driving. The discount typically lasts for three years before the course needs to be retaken.
