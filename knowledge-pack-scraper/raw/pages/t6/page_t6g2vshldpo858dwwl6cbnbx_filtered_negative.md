4. What You Need to Know About the 2025 State Minimum Insurance Limit Increase
As of January 1, 2025, the state will raise its minimum California auto insurance coverage requirements through [Senate Bill 1107 (SB1107)](https://leginfo.legislature.ca.gov/faces/billNavClient.xhtml?bill_id=202120220SB1107), marking the first change in over half a century. This adjustment aims to offer drivers greater financial security in the event of an accident. Here’s an overview of these changes, why they matter, and how to ensure your auto-owners insurance coverage aligns with the new standards:
* **$15,000** for injury or death to one person.
* **$30,000** total for injury or death paid in any one accident.
* **$5,000** for property damage.
* **$30,000** for injury or death to one person.
* **$60,000** total for injury or death for any one accident.
* **$15,000** for property damage.
1. **Review Your Policy:** Review your current liability limits to ensure compliance with the 2025 requirements.
2. **Consider Additional Coverage:** Additional protections, like uninsured/underinsured motorist coverage, can further safeguard you from financial hardship.
3. **Compare Insurance Quotes:** Compare quotes from multiple providers to find the best deal, especially if your premiums are set to rise.
4. **Consult an Insurance Expert:** An insurance professional can help you navigate these changes and ensure you’re protected at a price you can afford.
