Homeowners in Washington state should protect themselves against [common natural disasters](https://www.valuepenguin.com/best-cheap-homeowners-insurance-washington#perils "#perils"), such as earthquakes, floods and wildfires. Fortunately, homeowners insurance policies typically cover wildfires.
However, you'll have to buy separate earthquake and [flood policies](https://www.valuepenguin.com/flood-insurance-coverage "What Does Flood Insurance Cover, and How Does It Work?") to protect against these perils.

Most homeowners insurance policies cover wildfires. However, Mutual of Enumclaw goes one step further by offering customers access to a private firefighting service at no extra cost. In addition, Mutual of Enumclaw sells several extra coverage options including [umbrella insurance](https://www.valuepenguin.com/umbrella-insurance "What Does an Umbrella Policy Cover?") and cybersecurity fraud protection.
Mutual of Enumclaw also sells auto insurance policies. Although it's cheaper than the Washington state average, it's not the [most affordable option in WA](https://www.valuepenguin.com/best-cheap-car-insurance-washington "Best Cheap Car Insurance Quotes in Washington"). However, it may make sense to buy both policies through Mutual of Enumclaw if you plan on taking advantage of its home-auto bundling discount.

Fortunately, most homeowners insurance policies cover damage from wildfires. However, if you live in a high-risk area, you may have a hard time finding an affordable policy. If that's the case, you may need to buy a more expensive type of home insurance of last resort, called a [FAIR plan policy](https://www.valuepenguin.com/homeowners-insurance/fair-plans-and-how-do-you-insure-high-risk-home "Compare FAIR Plans for High-Risk Home Insurance").
You may need to buy [flood insurance](https://www.valuepenguin.com/flood-insurance-coverage "What Does Flood Insurance Cover, and How Does It Work?") if you live in an area at high risk for flooding, such as river valleys and coastal areas. This includes many heavily populated parts of Washington including much of the Puget Sound region and parts of the greater Spokane area.
If you live west of the Cascade Mountains, you're at high risk for earthquakes. That means you should consider [getting earthquake insurance](https://www.valuepenguin.com/earthquake-insurance "Earthquake Insurance, Explained") if your own savings wouldn't cover the cost of repairing or rebuilding your house after a major earthquake.
