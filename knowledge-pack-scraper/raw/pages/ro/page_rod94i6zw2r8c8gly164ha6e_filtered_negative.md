# What minimum car coverage does Texas require?
![What minimum car coverage does Texas require?](https://ontiverosins.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/10/What-minimum-car-coverage-does-Texas-require-1.jpg)
Texas law mandates that all drivers carry a minimum amount of liability insurance to operate a vehicle legally. Known as the 30/60/25 rule, the state requires at least $30,000 in coverage for injuries or death per person, up to $60,000 per accident for all injuries or deaths, and $25,000 for property damage.
This coverage, often referred to as 30/60/25, ensures financial responsibility in the event of an at-fault collision. While this is the legal minimum, many drivers opt for higher limits to better protect their assets. Failure to maintain the required coverage can result in fines, license suspension, or vehicle impoundment.
