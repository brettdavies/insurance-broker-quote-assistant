What minimum car coverage does Texas require?

Texas law mandates that all drivers carry a minimum amount of liability insurance to operate a vehicle legally. Known as the 30/60/25 rule, the state requires at least $30,000 in coverage for injuries or death per person, up to $60,000 per accident for all injuries or deaths, and $25,000 for property damage.

This coverage, often referred to as 30/60/25, ensures financial responsibility in the event of an at-fault collision. While this is the legal minimum, many drivers opt for higher limits to better protect their assets. Failure to maintain the required coverage can result in fines, license suspension, or vehicle impoundment.

Understanding Texas’ Liability Insurance Requirements

Texas operates under a fault-based insurance system, meaning the driver responsible for an accident is also financially liable for the damages. To ensure drivers can cover these costs, the state enforces minimum liability insurance requirements: $30,000 for injuries per person, up to $60,000 per accident, and $25,000 for property damage.

This 30/60/25 rule is the baseline, and driving without it can result in fines, license suspension, or even vehicle impoundment. While some drivers opt for only the minimum, it’s important to consider that medical expenses and vehicle repairs can quickly exceed these limits, leaving you personally responsible for the difference. Consequences of Driving Without Insurance in Texas

Driving without valid auto insurance in Texas comes with significant penalties. If caught, you could face fines of up to $1,000 for a first offense, increased registration fees, and even the suspension of your driver’s license or vehicle registration. In some cases, your car may be impounded.

Additionally, if you’re involved in an at-fault accident without insurance, you could be held personally liable for all medical and repair costs which can amount to tens of thousands of dollars. The state may also require you to file an SR-22, a certificate proving future financial responsibility, which often leads to higher premiums.

Protecting yourself with a reliable policy from a trusted provider like Ontiveros Insurance not only keeps you compliant but also shields you from unexpected financial hardship.

Why Minimum Coverage Might Not Be Enough

While meeting the minimum insurance requirements in Texas keeps you legal on the road, it may leave you underprotected in serious accidents. For instance, a single hospital visit after a collision can exceed $30,000, quickly exhausting your bodily injury limit. If damages go beyond your coverage, you could be sued for the remaining costs, putting your personal assets at risk.

Furthermore, minimum policies don’t cover your own vehicle repairs or medical bills if you’re at fault that’s where optional coverages like collision, comprehensive, and personal injury protection come into play.

Why Higher Liability Coverage is Recommended in Texas

While Texas law requires a minimum liability coverage of $30,000 per injured person, up to $60,000 per accident, and $25,000 for property damage (30/60/25), these limits are often insufficient to cover the full costs of serious accidents.

Medical expenses, vehicle repairs, legal fees, and other damages can quickly exceed the minimum thresholds, leaving drivers personally liable for the remaining costs. Many financial and insurance experts recommend higher limits such as 100/300/100 to ensure better protection.

Given the rising costs of healthcare and vehicle repairs in Texas, increasing coverage helps reduce the risk of substantial out-of-pocket expenses after an at-fault accident.
