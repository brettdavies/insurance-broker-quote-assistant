State Farm charges a $15 to $25 filing fee for an SR-22, plus an increased premium. SR-22 insurance from State Farm costs an average of $1,765 per year, which is 146% more than a standard policy, since drivers who need an SR-22 are considered [high risk](https://wallethub.com/answers/ci/high-risk-driver-2140720827/). The exact amount that your insurance goes up due to an SR-22 ultimately depends on the violation that led to the SR-22 filling.

Most states require drivers to have an SR-22 on file for 3-5 years after being convicted of a serious moving violation, like DUI or reckless driving. Once you no longer [need an SR-22](https://wallethub.com/answers/ci/do-i-need-an-sr22-2140685748/), your State Farm rate should start to go back down. In the meantime, there are still ways that you can keep your State Farm premium affordable, including taking advantage of [discounts](https://wallethub.com/edu/ci/car-insurance-discounts/11950) and temporarily limiting your coverage to your state’s minimum requirements.
