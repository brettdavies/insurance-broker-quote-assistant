# What Happens When New York’s $50,000 No-Fault Insurance Minimum Can’t Cover Your Multi-Borough Auto Accident Medical Bills?

New York’s no-fault insurance system provides immediate medical coverage regardless of who caused the accident. Still, the basic coverage has significant limitations that can leave you facing substantial out-of-pocket expenses. The standard no-fault policy provides $50,000 in basic Personal Injury Protection (PIP) benefits, covering medical expenses, lost wages, and other essential services. However, when you’re dealing with serious injuries from a multi-borough accident, this amount often proves insufficient. Motor vehicle traffic crashes are the leading cause of injury-related death for New York State residents, and the severity of these injuries frequently results in medical bills that far exceed basic coverage limits. A [car accident lawyer in New York](https://www.napolilaw.com/en/practice-areas/personal-injury) can help you understand all available avenues for recovering additional compensation when your basic benefits are exhausted.
When your [basic no-fault benefits](https://www.nysenate.gov/legislation/laws/ISC/5102) are consumed, New York law provides several pathways to continue receiving coverage and compensation. Understanding this process helps you stay ahead of mounting bills and ensures you don’t miss critical deadlines. Working with a car accident lawyer in New York becomes essential at this stage, as navigating multiple insurance policies and potential lawsuits requires knowledge of complex regulations and strategic timing.
When insurance coverage falls short, pursuing legal action against the responsible party often becomes necessary to secure full compensation for your injuries and losses. The reality is that fatal and serious traffic crashes in New York resulted in $135.4 billion in economic and quality-of-life costs in 2023, highlighting the massive financial impact these accidents have on victims and their families. At Napoli Shkolnik, we understand that each case requires a comprehensive approach that examines all available insurance coverage, identifies all liable parties, and builds a strong case for maximum recovery. Our team works diligently to ensure that clients who have [exhausted their no-fault benefits](https://www.dfs.ny.gov/consumers/auto_insurance/nofault_faqs) can still obtain the medical treatment they need while pursuing additional compensation through litigation. A car accident lawyer in New York can help you navigate the complex intersection of insurance claims and personal injury lawsuits to achieve the best possible outcome.
