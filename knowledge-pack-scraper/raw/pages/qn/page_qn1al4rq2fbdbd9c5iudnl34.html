What Is an Uninsured Motorist Claim?

An uninsured motorist (UM) claim is a type of insurance claim that allows you to seek compensation through your own auto insurance policy when the at-fault driver does not have liability coverage. In New York, insurance companies are required to offer uninsured motorist coverage, which provides protection for accidents involving uninsured drivers or hit-and-run incidents.

Uninsured motorist coverage can help pay for medical expenses, lost wages, and other damages caused by the accident. However, filing a UM claim can be a complex process, and insurance companies may attempt to minimize payouts. This is where having an experienced uninsured motorist claims lawyer is essential.

How Uninsured Motorist Coverage Works in New York

Uninsured motorist (UM) coverage is a critical safety net required by law in all New York auto insurance policies. This coverage is designed to protect individuals who are involved in accidents with drivers who lack the necessary liability insurance or in situations where the at-fault party cannot be identified.

When the at-fault driver in an accident does not carry liability insurance, uninsured motorist coverage steps in to cover your losses. These losses can include medical expenses, lost wages, pain and suffering, and other damages caused by the accident. Without UM coverage, victims in these situations would likely face significant financial burdens, as there would be no other insurance policy to turn to for compensation.

In addition to accidents involving uninsured drivers, UM coverage also applies in hit-and-run scenarios. These incidents are all too common in a busy urban environment like New York City, where drivers may flee the scene of an accident to avoid legal consequences. If the responsible party cannot be identified, uninsured motorist coverage allows you to file a claim with your own insurance company to recover damages.

By law, every auto insurance policy issued in New York includes uninsured motorist coverage, providing peace of mind to drivers and passengers alike. However, the process of filing a UM claim can still be fraught with challenges, particularly when dealing with insurance companies that may attempt to limit or deny payouts. Understanding how this coverage works is essential to ensuring that you can access the compensation you are entitled to in the aftermath of an accident involving an uninsured or unknown driver.

Common Challenges in Uninsured Motorist Claims

Pursuing an uninsured motorist claim can be a daunting task, especially when dealing with uncooperative insurance companies or complicated legal procedures. From disputes over the value of your claim to proving the at-fault driver’s lack of insurance, numerous obstacles can hinder your ability to secure compensation. Understanding these challenges is essential to navigating the process effectively and protecting your rights.

Disputes Over the Value of Your Claim

Insurance companies often attempt to minimize payouts by questioning the severity of your injuries or the extent of your damages. They may challenge medical records, undervalue pain and suffering, or dispute lost wages to reduce the compensation you receive. These tactics can delay your financial recovery and add stress to an already difficult situation.

Delays or Denials of Coverage

One of the most common challenges in uninsured motorist claims is the insurance company delaying the processing of your claim or outright denying coverage. These delays can exacerbate financial strain as victims struggle to cover mounting medical bills and other expenses. Denials often occur under questionable reasoning, leaving victims uncertain about their next steps.

Proving the At-Fault Driver Was Uninsured

Demonstrating that the other driver lacked liability insurance can be a complex and time-sensitive process. Insurance companies may require accident reports, witness statements, or correspondence from the at-fault driver’s insurer verifying their lack of coverage. Gathering and presenting this evidence without professional assistance can be overwhelming for accident victims.

Challenges in Hit-and-Run Cases

In hit-and-run accidents, proving that the incident occurred and identifying the responsible party can be particularly difficult. Insurance companies often require substantial evidence, such as police reports, photos from the scene, and statements from witnesses. Without proper documentation, victims may struggle to validate their claims and access compensation.

Steps to Take After an Accident with an Uninsured Driver

Being involved in an accident with an uninsured driver can be a stressful and confusing experience. However, taking the right steps in the aftermath of the collision can help protect your rights and strengthen your uninsured motorist claim. Proper documentation, timely communication, and professional legal guidance are essential to ensuring that you receive the compensation you deserve.

Seek Immediate Medical Attention

Your health and safety should always be your first priority after an accident. Even if your injuries seem minor, seek medical attention immediately to ensure that no hidden injuries go untreated. Medical documentation is also crucial for building your claim, as it provides evidence of the injuries sustained and their connection to the accident.

Report the Accident to the Police

Filing a police report is an essential step in documenting the accident. The police report serves as an official record of the collision, detailing important information such as the circumstances of the accident, statements from involved parties, and any citations issued. Be sure to obtain a copy of the report for your records, as it will be a critical piece of evidence for your claim.

Notify Your Insurance Company

Inform your insurance company about the accident as soon as possible. While it is important to report the incident, avoid providing detailed statements or admitting fault until you have consulted with a lawyer. Insurance adjusters may attempt to use your statements against you to reduce or deny your claim.

Document the Scene of the Accident

Thorough documentation of the accident scene can strengthen your case. Take photos of the damage to your vehicle, road conditions, traffic signals, and any visible injuries. If there are witnesses, collect their contact information and statements, as their accounts may support your version of events.

Types of Compensation Available

Uninsured motorist claims offer a crucial safety net for victims of accidents involving uninsured drivers. By filing a claim, you can pursue compensation to address the financial, physical, and emotional burdens caused by the accident. Understanding the types of compensation available can help you ensure that all your damages are accounted for and adequately addressed.

Medical Expenses

One of the primary components of an uninsured motorist claim is compensation for medical expenses. This includes coverage for emergency room visits, surgeries, hospital stays, medications, physical therapy, and any ongoing or future medical care required due to your injuries. It ensures that you receive the necessary care to recover from your injuries. Severe accidents can lead to long-term medical needs, such as rehabilitation or specialized treatments. Your claim should reflect these anticipated costs to ensure comprehensive care.

Lost Wages and Diminished Earning Capacity

If your injuries prevent you from working, either temporarily or permanently, you may be entitled to recover lost wages. This can be done through your uninsured motorist claim. For individuals who suffer long-term disabilities that limit their ability to work or reduce their earning potential, compensation can also account for diminished earning capacity. This helps to address the financial impact of these limitations on your future income. This ensures that your financial stability is protected despite the challenges caused by the accident.

Pain and Suffering

Beyond financial losses, uninsured motorist claims can provide compensation for the physical pain and emotional trauma experienced as a result of the accident. This coverage helps you recover from the non-financial consequences of the incident. Pain and suffering damages address the impact of your injuries on your quality of life, including chronic pain, emotional distress, anxiety, depression, and the loss of enjoyment of activities you once valued. These damages acknowledge the non-economic toll the accident has taken on your well-being.

Property Damage

Compensation for property damage covers the repair or replacement of your vehicle and any personal belongings damaged in the accident. This may include items such as electronics, clothing, or other valuables that were inside the vehicle at the time of the collision. Your claim can help compensate for these losses as well. Ensuring that these losses are accounted for can alleviate additional financial stress as you work toward recovery.

Frequently Asked Questions About Uninsured Motorist Claims

What Should I Do If the At-Fault Driver Leaves the Scene?

If the at-fault driver flees the scene of the accident, it is considered a hit-and-run. You should immediately report the accident to the police and obtain a copy of the police report. This report will be critical for your uninsured motorist claim. Be sure to document as much information as possible about the fleeing vehicle. Include details such as its color, make, model, and license plate (if available). Contacting an experienced uninsured motorist claims lawyer can help ensure your claim is properly handled.

How Long Do I Have to File an Uninsured Motorist Claim?

In New York, the statute of limitations for uninsured motorist claims generally follows the same timeline as other personal injury claims. This timeline is three years from the date of the accident. However, insurance policies often impose much shorter deadlines for reporting claims. It’s essential to notify your insurance company promptly and consult a lawyer to ensure all deadlines are met.

Can I File an Uninsured Motorist Claim If I Was a Passenger?

Yes, passengers injured in an accident with an uninsured driver can file a claim under the driver’s uninsured motorist coverage. They can also file under their own policy, depending on the circumstances. An attorney can review your options and help determine the best course of action to pursue compensation for your injuries.

What Happens If My Insurance Company Denies My Claim?

Insurance companies may deny claims for various reasons. For example, they may dispute the severity of your injuries or argue that the at-fault driver was not uninsured. If your claim is denied, a lawyer can help you challenge the decision. A lawyer can present additional evidence, negotiate with the insurer, or file a lawsuit if necessary.

Can I Recover Compensation for a Hit-and-Run Accident on Foot or Bicycle?

Yes, uninsured motorist coverage can apply to pedestrians and bicyclists injured in hit-and-run accidents. If you are hit by a car as a pedestrian or cyclist and the driver cannot be identified, your uninsured motorist coverage may compensate you. This coverage may help with medical expenses, lost wages, and other damages.

What if I Was Partially at Fault for the Accident?

New York follows a comparative negligence rule. This means you can still recover compensation even if you were partially at fault for the accident. However, your compensation may be reduced by the percentage of your fault. For example, if you are found 20% at fault, your compensation will be reduced by 20%. A lawyer can help minimize the impact of shared fault on your claim.

Do I Need a Lawyer to File an Uninsured Motorist Claim?

While you are not required to have a lawyer, working with an experienced uninsured motorist claims attorney significantly increases your chances of receiving fair compensation. Insurance companies often attempt to minimize payouts. A lawyer can advocate on your behalf, gather evidence, and handle negotiations to protect your interests.
