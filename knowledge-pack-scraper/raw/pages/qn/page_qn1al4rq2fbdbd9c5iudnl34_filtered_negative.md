In New York City, a car accident is stressful enough—but when you find out the driver who hit you doesn’t have insurance, or nowhere near enough, the stress turns into confusion and anger. You’re left injured, unable to work, and suddenly forced to deal with your own insurance company—only to realize they’re just as quick to push back, delay, or deny. Uninsured and underinsured motorist (UM/UIM) claims are supposed to protect you, but navigating them can feel like falling into a maze of fine print, technicalities, and shifting responsibility.
