## Tips for getting cheaper car insurance in California

Your rates depend on personal details, like your age, driving record and ZIP code. This means that the cheapest insurer in California for you might not be the cheapest for someone else.

Because of this, it‚Äôs important to [compare prices](https://www.nerdwallet.com/insurance/auto/car-insurance) from a few insurance companies. These other tips may also help you lower your insurance rates.

* **Shop for car insurance at least once a year.** Everybody‚Äôs rates change over time. Check that you still get your best deal before renewing your coverage.
* **Commit to covering more of a future claim.** You can raise your [auto insurance deductible](https://www.nerdwallet.com/article/insurance/car-insurance-deductible), which is the part of a future claim that your insurance *won‚Äôt* cover.¬†Taking more responsibility later can get you lower rates now.
* **Drop coverage you don‚Äôt need anymore.** You might be able to carry less insurance, and pay less for your coverage, if you own a car that would cost more to insure than it‚Äôs worth.
* **Consider usage-based car insurance.** You could pay less for [car insurance that‚Äôs priced on driving habits](https://www.nerdwallet.com/article/insurance/pay-per-mile-car-insurance) like your speed, turning, braking and the miles you drive.

* ### Minimum auto insurance limits in California

  California drivers must carry liability insurance in the following amounts:

  + $30,000 bodily injury liability per person.
  + $60,000 bodily injury liability per accident.
  + $15,000 property damage liability per accident.

  [Liability car insurance](https://www.nerdwallet.com/article/insurance/auto/liability-car-insurance) covers the cost of any damage, injuries or deaths you cause in a car accident, up to your policy‚Äôs predefined limits. Experts recommend getting more than the bare minimum if you want to avoid dipping into your own savings to cover expenses.

  For instance, if you have only minimum coverage but cause an accident that totals another driver's $20,000 car, you would have to pay $5,000 out of pocket because the total exceeds your policy's $15,000 limit for property damage.

  Check out NerdWallet‚Äôs guide to [state car insurance requirements](https://www.nerdwallet.com/article/insurance/minimum-car-insurance-requirements) for more details on how the required and optional parts of your California policy work.

## Low-income car insurance in California

If you can‚Äôt afford car insurance premiums, you might qualify for [California‚Äôs Low Cost Auto Insurance program](https://www.mylowcostauto.com/), which has several payment plans and no broker fees. To be eligible, you'll need:

* A current California driver‚Äôs license.
* An automobile worth $25,000 or less.
* A household income that meets the program requirements.
* A clean or new driving record.

The California Department of Insurance sets household income limits based on the federal poverty level, which is used to determine eligibility for certain government programs. The federal poverty level (and program income requirement) depends on the year and the number of people in your household. For example, the program income limit was $39,125 for one person in 2025.

The program‚Äôs website has a calculator to determine income cutoffs for larger households, a rate calculator and a tool to find participating insurance agents. You‚Äôll have to provide documentation of income, as well as other requirements, to qualify.
