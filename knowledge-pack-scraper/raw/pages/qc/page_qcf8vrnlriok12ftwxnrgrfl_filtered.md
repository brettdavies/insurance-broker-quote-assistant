## What are the minimum requirements for motorcycle insurance in Illinois?

Illinois motorcycle law requires all riders to carry [motorcycle liability coverage](https://www.progressive.com/answers/motorcycle-liability-coverage/) for bodily injury and property damage. Liability coverage may pay for injuries and damages you cause to others while riding, up to your policy's limits. Property damage liability coverage, however, doesn't cover your own motorcycle. You can increase the amount of liability coverage, if necessary, to protect your assets more fully.

| Required coverage | What it covers | Minimum amount |
| --- | --- | --- |
| [Bodily injury liability](https://www.progressive.com/answers/bodily-injury-liability/) | What it coversInjuries you cause to others in an accident | Minimum amount$25,000 per person $50,000 per accident |
| [Property damage liability](https://www.progressive.com/answers/property-damage-liability/) | What it coversProperty damage you cause to others in an accident | Minimum amount$20,000 per accident |
| [Uninsured/underinsured motorist bodily injury](https://www.progressive.com/answers/uninsured-motorist-coverage-for-motorcycles/) | What it coversInjuries to you and your passengers if hit by an at-fault motorist who has no coverage or insufficient coverage | Minimum amount$25,000 per person |

### Do you need comprehensive and collision coverage?

Unless you're taking out a loan or lease for your motorcycle, [comprehensive motorcycle coverage](https://www.progressive.com/answers/motorcycle-comprehensive-insurance/) and [collision motorcycle coverage](https://www.progressive.com/answers/motorcycle-collision-coverage/) are optional. But we know many motorcycle owners are dedicated to protecting their bike, and they may want to safeguard it against physical damage. Comprehensive coverage may cover damage to your bike from events out of your control: [motorcycle theft](https://www.progressive.com/answers/motorcycle-theft/), vandalism, hitting an animal, fire, and weather-related issues. Collision can cover your motorcycle if it collides with another object or vehicle, regardless of who's [at fault](https://www.progressive.com/answers/at-fault-vs-no-fault-accidents/).

## Additional motorcycle insurance coverages available in Illinois

For increased protection, the following optional coverages may be added to your Progressive motorcycle policy:

* **[Motorcycle accessory coverage](https://www.progressive.com/answers/motorcycle-accessory-coverage/)**: Can pay for damage to the upgrades you've made to your bike and your equipment, such as chrome pieces, electronics, handlebars, and custom paint and wheels
* **Medical payments**: Can pay for injuries you or your passenger suffer, regardless of fault
* **Total loss coverage**: May pay for the full value of your bike in the event of a [total loss](https://www.progressive.com/answers/totaled-motorcycle/), protecting against depreciation (available only for bikes no more than one model year old on a new policy)
* **Carried contents and personal belongings**: Can pay to replace any personal effects you're carrying when a covered loss occurs, including theft
* **Enhanced injury protection**: May pay as much as $250 a week for two years if you can't work due to injuries sustained during a crash
* **Roadside assistance**: Covers the cost of towing your bike to the nearest repair shop

## Quick facts for riding in Illinois

1. To obtain a [motorcycle license or endorsement in Illinois](https://www.ilsos.gov/publications/pdf_publications/dsd_x140.pdf), you must pass a knowledge and skills test.
