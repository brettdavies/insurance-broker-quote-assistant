Florida has the most hurricane events of any state, with at least one hurricane affecting its coastline every year since 1850.[[1]](https://insurify.com/homeowners-insurance/florida/windstorm-insurance/#cite-1) As a result, residents in the Sunshine State may face higher rates on their [**homeowners insurance policies**](https://insurify.com/homeowners-insurance/).
The good news is that the standard homeowners policy includes windstorm insurance, which protects your home from damage caused by tornadoes, [**hurricanes**](https://insurify.com/homeowners-insurance/hurricane-insurance/), and tropical storms. But some homeowners — especially those in high-risk areas — might not qualify for insurance coverage with most insurers. That’s why Florida offers a government-backed alternative to assist high-risk applicants.
Windstorms bring heavy winds and flying debris, leading to costly and significant damage to your home’s roof, windows, and structure.[[2]](https://insurify.com/homeowners-insurance/florida/windstorm-insurance/#cite-2) That’s why most homeowners in Florida need windstorm insurance, which covers wind damage.
The average cost of a $300,000 homeowners policy in Florida is $5,640 per year, based on Insurify data. But your rate depends on various factors, including your location, your home’s age and condition, types of coverage, [**deductible amount**](https://insurify.com/homeowners-insurance/knowledge/home-insurance-deductible/), and more.
* **Flood damage:** A standard home insurance policy won’t cover damage from flooding. You can purchase a separate [**flood insurance**](https://insurify.com/homeowners-insurance/flood-insurance/) policy from the National Flood Insurance Program or from a private insurer.
Hurricane deductibles kick in after the National Hurricane Center issues a hurricane warning for any part of Florida and end 72 hours after it lifts the last hurricane watch. If you have to pay a hurricane deductible, you won’t have to cover your regular homeowners insurance deductible.[[3]](https://insurify.com/homeowners-insurance/florida/windstorm-insurance/#cite-3)
Windstorm deductibles are usually higher than your regular home insurance deductible. The Florida Department of Financial Services lists $500, 2%, 5%, and 10% as example deductible amounts, but your options depend on your insurer and [**dwelling coverage**](https://insurify.com/homeowners-insurance/knowledge/dwelling-insurance/) amount.
If you’re a Florida homeowner, you can get windstorm insurance from private homeowners insurance companies. To find an insurance company, use the [**Florida Market Assistance Program (FMAP)**](https://www.fmap.org/), a free service that connects you to a local insurance agent who can assist you with your search. You can also check out the [**best homeowners insurance in Florida**](https://insurify.com/homeowners-insurance/florida/) to kickstart your search.
Insurers may enforce a waiting period before coverage takes effect and stop selling new coverage as a storm approaches. Florida’s hurricane season runs from June 1 to Nov. 30, so it’s a good idea to secure coverage before a hurricane strikes.[[4]](https://insurify.com/homeowners-insurance/florida/windstorm-insurance/#cite-4)
The amount of windstorm coverage you’ll need depends on the cost of rebuilding your home and any external structures, as well as the value of your [**personal belongings**](https://insurify.com/homeowners-insurance/personal-property-coverage/). The best way to determine the appropriate coverage and amounts for your needs is to consult an insurance professional.
Your insurer will schedule an appointment with an [**insurance adjuster**](https://insurify.com/homeowners-insurance/knowledge/insurance-adjusters/) to inspect the extent of the damage. To save time, be prepared to share a list of damaged items and areas with the adjuster.
