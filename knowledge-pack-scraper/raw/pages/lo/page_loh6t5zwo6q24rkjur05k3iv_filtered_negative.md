##  Geico vs. Allstate: Coverage Options
Allstate takes the top spot in our study of the [best renters insurance companies](https://www.usnews.com/insurance/renters-insurance) and fifth spot in our ranking of the [cheapest renters insurance companies](https://www.usnews.com/insurance/renters-insurance/cheapest-renters-insurance-companies). However, Geico isn't rated in either study.
According to our study, Allstate's average monthly premium is $21.50, and Geico's website advertises an average rate as low as $12 per month. While Allstate offers more discounts than Geico, both insurers offer a multipolicy discount to help you save on your premiums.
Both companies offer standard renters insurance policies that include[ coverage](https://www.usnews.com/insurance/renters-insurance/what-does-renters-insurance-cover) for personal property, liability, loss of use, and guest medical expenses.
Additionally, Allstate offers the following optional coverages: scheduled personal property coverage, a personal umbrella policy, identity theft restoration, and flood insurance. Geico offers additional identity theft protection, replacement cost coverage, earthquake coverage, and extra coverage for high-value items such as jewelry.
Geico sells renters insurance policies through a third-party company, while Allstate writes its own. However, both companies offer a mobile app that allows customers to pay bills, file claims, and access a virtual insurance card. Both companies are available in all 50 states and Washington, D.C.
### Do Allstate and Geico Cover Bedbugs?
Neither company covers damage from bedbugs. Standard renters insurance policies typically don't cover bedbugs or pest infestations because they are considered preventable through standard home maintenance.
### Do Allstate and Geico Cover Water Damage?
Both insurers cover sudden and accidental water damage. This can include a burst pipe, interior plumbing failure, or water seeping in from a neighboring unit. However, water damage caused by flooding isn't covered.
### Do Allstate and Geico Cover Flood Damage?
Renters insurance policies typically don't cover flood damage. Flood damage is generally defined as water that originates from outside or underground sources.
### Do Allstate and Geico Cover Mold Damage?
Renters insurance policies don't cover mold damage or removal unless it occurs due to a covered peril, such as a burst pipe. Mold damage is generally considered preventable with standard maintenance.
### Do Allstate and Geico Cover Property Damage?
Allstate and Geico both cover personal property damage caused by a covered peril, up to your policy limits. They also both cover personal liability if you accidentally damage someone else's property.
### Do Allstate and Geico Cover Fire Damage?
Both insurers cover fire damage as a named peril. This includes personal property damage caused by fire and smoke, as well as additional living expenses if you are displaced from your home.
### Do Allstate and Geico Cover Car Theft?
While neither company covers your car if it's stolen from the rental property, they do cover personal items stolen from your vehicle.
### Do Allstate and Geico Cover Moving Costs?
Renters insurance doesn't typically cover moving costs. However, if you must temporarily relocate due to a covered peril, the additional living expenses portion of your policy may reimburse you for moving costs.
### Do Allstate and Geico Cover Temporary Housing?
Allstate and Geico cover temporary housing under the additional living expenses portion of your policy if you are displaced due to a covered peril. Additional living expenses may help pay for hotel and restaurant bills.
### Do Allstate and Geico Offer Renters Insurance Bundling?
Both insurers offer a discount for bundling your renters insurance with another insurance policy. Also known as multipolicy discounts, bundling is a common way to save on your premium.

## Bottom Line
Both insurers are solid options for renters insurance. However, Geico sells policies through third-party companies, while Allstate writes its own. Optional coverages and discounts vary per company, but Allstate offers more in each category.
Allstate ranks No. 5 in our study of the cheapest renters insurance companies, while Geico isn't rated in our analysis. However, its website claims that premiums can be as low as $12 per month, which is significantly less than Allstate's monthly average of $21.50. That said, the best way to determine your cost is to get quotes from both insurers.
