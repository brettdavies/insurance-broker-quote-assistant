Bundling your home and auto insurance means purchasing both policies from the same company, often at a [**discounted rate**](https://insurify.com/car-insurance/discounts/).[[1]](https://insurify.com/car-insurance/coverage/home-and-auto/#cite-1) Think of it like signing up for an internet and cable television package. You get the convenience of one insurance company and a lower total bill. Here’s how to do it:
Before seeking out new quotes, think about your priorities. Do you want higher liability limits, a lower deductible, or [**broader coverage for valuables**](https://insurify.com/homeowners-insurance/knowledge/jewelry-insurance/)?[[2]](https://insurify.com/car-insurance/coverage/home-and-auto/#cite-2) Knowing this helps you avoid buying based only on price.
Don’t just look at the total price. Review coverage limits, exclusions, deductibles, and the insurance company’s financial ratings.[[3]](https://insurify.com/car-insurance/coverage/home-and-auto/#cite-3) A lower premium isn’t worth it if coverage gaps leave you exposed.
Check out these tips for finding the best home and auto insurance:[[4]](https://insurify.com/car-insurance/coverage/home-and-auto/#cite-4)
* **Consider your coverage needs.** Consider what[**coverage you need**](https://insurify.com/car-insurance/knowledge/how-much-car-insurance-do-i-need/) — especially if you want to bundle your home and auto policies. Check insurance companies’ websites to see what types of coverage they offer.
For example, if an insurance company offers great car insurance rates but higher-than-average [**home insurance premiums**](https://insurify.com/homeowners-insurance/) — or vice versa — you might save more by keeping policies separate. Always compare stand-alone and bundled quotes before deciding, since [**savings opportunities**](https://insurify.com/homeowners-insurance/home-insurance-discounts/) vary by insurance company and state.
* **Not always cheapest:** An insurer may offer the [**lowest car insurance rates**](https://insurify.com/car-insurance/cheap-car-insurance/) but charge more for homeowners coverage, or vice versa.
