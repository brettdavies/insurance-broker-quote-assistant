Why You Might Need SR22 Insurance in California?

Do you need SR22 insurance in California? If your license was suspended or revoked, the California DMV may require you to file an SR22 form to prove you have the state’s minimum liability coverage. Here are the most common reasons drivers need an SR22:

  • DUI or DWI – A conviction for driving under the influence of alcohol or drugs often requires SR22 insurance to reinstate your license.
  • Driving Without Insurance – If you were caught driving uninsured, you may need to file an SR22 before getting your license back.
  • Too Many Traffic Violations – Accumulating multiple violations within a short period can classify you as a high-risk driver, requiring an SR22 filing.
  • At-Fault Accidents Without Insurance – If you caused an accident while uninsured, the DMV may require an SR22 to reinstate your driving privileges.
  • License Suspension or Revocation – Reckless driving, excessive points on your record, or other serious infractions can lead to an SR22 requirement before you can legally drive again.

How Long Do You Need SR22 Insurance in California?

Most drivers must carry SR22 insurance for three to five years, depending on the violation. Failing to maintain coverage can lead to license suspension and additional penalties.

What Are California’s SR22 Insurance Requirements?

Do you need SR22 insurance in California? If you’ve had a license suspension due to a DUI, at-fault accident, or driving without insurance, the state requires you to carry minimum liability coverage to reinstate your driving privileges.

California’s minimum SR22 insurance requirements include:

  • $30,000 for bodily injury per person
  • $60,000 for bodily injury per accident
  • $15,000 for property damage

These limits protect others if you’re at fault in an accident, but they don’t cover your own vehicle. If you want extra protection, consider full coverage with collision and comprehensive insurance.

How Much Does SR22 Insurance Cost in California?

Worried about SR22 insurance fees in California? The SR22 filing itself is usually inexpensive, but your driving history—not the SR22—determines your premium.

SR22 Filing Fees in California

  • Most insurance companies charge a one-time filing fee of $0 to $25.
  • This fee covers submitting your SR22 form to the California DMV to prove financial responsibility.

What Affects SR22 Insurance Costs?

While the SR22 filing fee is low, your overall premium can increase based on:

  • Number of Violations – More infractions mean higher insurance costs.
  • DUI or At-Fault Accidents – High-risk violations lead to higher rates.
  • Driving Without Insurance – A lapse in coverage can make your policy more expensive.
