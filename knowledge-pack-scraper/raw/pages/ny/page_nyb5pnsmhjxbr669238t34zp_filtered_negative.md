[**Florida homeowners**](https://insurify.com/homeowners-insurance/florida/) are at risk of severe damage from violent storms during the June through November hurricane season. State law in Florida requires insurance companies to offer hurricane deductibles to their customers.[[1]](https://insurify.com/homeowners-insurance/knowledge/florida-hurricane-deductible/#cite-1) A hurricane deductible is a special deductible that applies only to hurricane losses and operates separately from your regular home insurance deductible.
Here’s what you need to know about hurricane deductibles in Florida, including how they work and interact with [**standard home insurance coverage**](https://insurify.com/homeowners-insurance/).
Insurers must offer hurricane deductibles as part of standard home insurance policies. Hurricane deductibles don’t replace the existing standard [**home insurance deductible**](https://insurify.com/homeowners-insurance/knowledge/home-insurance-deductible/). You can use the hurricane deductible only for hurricane damage, while your regular deductible applies to everything else.
![](https://a.storyblok.com/f/162273/1600x1600/22967c3a90/florida-hurricane-coverage-and-deductible-timeline.png/m/1600x1600/smart/filters:quality\(90\))
Storm classification matters when determining whether hurricane deductibles apply. For a storm to be classified as a hurricane, the National Hurricane Center must issue a formal hurricane declaration. Otherwise, the storm may be classified as a named storm or general wind/hail, and your hurricane deductible wouldn’t apply.[[2]](https://insurify.com/homeowners-insurance/knowledge/florida-hurricane-deductible/#cite-2)

You have a few different options for your hurricane deductible, including a specific percentage deductible. Florida state law requires insurers to show the dollar value of your hurricane deductible on [**policy declaration pages**](https://insurify.com/homeowners-insurance/knowledge/homeowners-insurance-declaration-page/).
Like standard home insurance deductibles, your hurricane deductible doesn’t cover flood damage. You’ll need to buy a separate [**flood insurance policy**](https://insurify.com/homeowners-insurance/florida-flood-insurance/) from an insurer or through the National Flood Insurance Program (NFIP). State law doesn’t require flood insurance, but your mortgage lender typically will.
[![Learn More: What Does Home Insurance Cover?](https://a.storyblok.com/f/162273/2267x1700/17156af0d6/gray-house.jpg/m/400x300/smart/filters:quality\(90\)) Learn More: What Does Home Insurance Cover?](https://insurify.com/homeowners-insurance/knowledge/what-does-homeowners-insurance-cover/)
[**Filing a home insurance claim**](https://insurify.com/homeowners-insurance/knowledge/how-to-file-a-home-insurance-claim/)
Under Florida law, you must file property damage claims within one year after the date of loss, so it’s important not to wait to file a claim after a hurricane — even if the amount is below your hurricane deductible.
If you live in Florida, you can consider some other types of insurance. First, you should have flood insurance because standard home insurance doesn’t cover flood damage. Water damage from storm surges can be extremely expensive without coverage. The National Flood NFIP offers excellent flood insurance, which you have to get anyway if you’re in a high-risk area.[[3]](https://insurify.com/homeowners-insurance/knowledge/florida-hurricane-deductible/#cite-3)
It’s also a good idea to have [**windstorm insurance**](https://insurify.com/homeowners-insurance/windstorm-insurance/) in Florida. Your standard homeowners policy might already cover wind damage, but having separate windstorm insurance can provide extra protection against hurricanes and other windstorm damage.

* ### What’s the hurricane deductible assistance program in Florida?
Florida offers a state deductible assistance program to homeowners who can’t afford the cost of their hurricane deductible after a hurricane. This program is event-specific, and available support varies by hurricane.[[4]](https://insurify.com/homeowners-insurance/knowledge/florida-hurricane-deductible/#cite-4)
* ### What are Florida’s claim-filing deadlines?
Florida state law requires homeowners to file all property damage claims within one year of the damage occurring — including hurricane deductible claims.
