* Dealing with the aftermath of a car accident is never simple. Insurance paperwork, calls with adjusters, and questions about evidence preservation can quickly overwhelm even the most organized driver. When the insurer is GEICO, the process may appear straightforward thanks to its online tools and mobile app. Yet for claimants in California, Texas, and Illinois, there are important legal and procedural differences that can change how your claim is filed, how it is handled, and how it is ultimately resolved.
* GEICO will either schedule a mobile inspection or direct you to a GEICO Auto Repair Xpress® location.
* These repair facilities provide same-day inspections and repairs, with GEICO’s lifetime repair guarantee.
* You are not required to use a GEICO partner shop; you may select your own repair facility.
* Bodily injury claims are handled separately and may involve a different adjuster.
* GEICO typically requires medical records, billing statements, and sometimes signed authorizations.
* Adjusters may apply “usual and customary” reductions to medical charges, paying less than the billed amount.
* GEICO generally contacts claimants within 24–48 hours of filing.
* Vehicle inspections are often scheduled within a week.
* Injury claims may take longer, as GEICO may wait until treatment concludes before discussing a settlement.
* Quick settlements: GEICO may offer fast, low settlements to close files cheaply.
* Recorded statements: Adjusters often request them, but you are not legally required to give one unless compelled.
* Comparative fault arguments: GEICO may assign you partial fault, lowering the payout.

## FAQ
###  [ What is GEICO? ](https://gosuits.com/knowledge-base/how-to-file-a-geico-accident-claim-gosuits/#faq-0)
GEICO (Government Employees Insurance Company) is one of the largest U.S. auto insurers and a Berkshire Hathaway subsidiary. It services claims through a centralized system with local adjusters and inspection shops.
###  [ What happens after I file a GEICO claim? ](https://gosuits.com/knowledge-base/how-to-file-a-geico-accident-claim-gosuits/#faq-1)
GEICO assigns a claim number and adjuster. You can track progress and upload documents via the online portal or app; the adjuster is your main point of contact.
###  [ Do I have to use a GEICO Auto Repair Xpress shop? ](https://gosuits.com/knowledge-base/how-to-file-a-geico-accident-claim-gosuits/#faq-2)
No. GEICO may schedule a mobile inspection or direct you to a Repair Xpress location, but you can choose any repair facility.
###  [ What is GEICO’s lifetime repair guarantee? ](https://gosuits.com/knowledge-base/how-to-file-a-geico-accident-claim-gosuits/#faq-3)
If you use a participating GEICO Auto Repair Xpress shop, GEICO offers a lifetime guarantee on covered repairs performed through that program.
###  [ How are bodily injury claims handled? ](https://gosuits.com/knowledge-base/how-to-file-a-geico-accident-claim-gosuits/#faq-4)
They are typically handled by a separate adjuster. GEICO usually requests medical records and bills and may apply “usual and customary” reductions. Settlement discussions often occur after treatment concludes.
###  [ How long does a GEICO claim take to settle? ](https://gosuits.com/knowledge-base/how-to-file-a-geico-accident-claim-gosuits/#faq-5)
Timelines vary. Property damage claims may resolve quickly; injury claims often take longer and may not settle until treatment ends. Evidence availability and negotiation affect timing.
###  [ When will GEICO contact me and schedule an inspection? ](https://gosuits.com/knowledge-base/how-to-file-a-geico-accident-claim-gosuits/#faq-6)
Claimants are generally contacted within 24–48 hours of filing. Vehicle inspections are often set within about a week.
###  [ Do I have to give GEICO a recorded statement? ](https://gosuits.com/knowledge-base/how-to-file-a-geico-accident-claim-gosuits/#faq-7)
No, not unless legally compelled. Adjusters often request one, but you can decline and consult an attorney first.
###  [ What documents should I gather for an injury claim? ](https://gosuits.com/knowledge-base/how-to-file-a-geico-accident-claim-gosuits/#faq-8)
Medical treatment records, billing statements, any police report, photos, and witness info. GEICO may also request signed authorizations.
###  [ What common claim issues should I watch for with GEICO? ](https://gosuits.com/knowledge-base/how-to-file-a-geico-accident-claim-gosuits/#faq-9)
Quick low settlement offers, recorded statement requests, and comparative fault arguments that reduce payout.
###  [ How does comparative fault affect my payout? ](https://gosuits.com/knowledge-base/how-to-file-a-geico-accident-claim-gosuits/#faq-10)
Your recovery can be reduced by your percentage of fault. Insurers may argue you share responsibility to lower the settlement.
###  [ What should California claimants know? ](https://gosuits.com/knowledge-base/how-to-file-a-geico-accident-claim-gosuits/#faq-11)
California uses comparative negligence. Proposition 213 restricts non-economic damages for uninsured drivers. UM/UIM claims require careful medical documentation.
###  [ What should Texas claimants know? ](https://gosuits.com/knowledge-base/how-to-file-a-geico-accident-claim-gosuits/#faq-12)
Insurers must acknowledge a claim within 15 days of notice. Filing digitally creates a clear timeline record. Reference PIP/MedPay if your policy includes them (see Texas DOI).
###  [ What should Illinois claimants know? ](https://gosuits.com/knowledge-base/how-to-file-a-geico-accident-claim-gosuits/#faq-13)
Illinois is fault-based. Adjusters may assert comparative fault. Recorded statements are often requested but not required unless mandated (see Illinois DOI).
###  [ How can I send legal correspondence to GEICO without a claim number? ](https://gosuits.com/knowledge-base/how-to-file-a-geico-accident-claim-gosuits/#faq-14)
Use fax to GEICO’s legal intake for timestamped proof (call 800-841-3000 or email executiveoffice@geico.com to obtain the correct fax). For critical items, send USPS Certified Mail with Return Receipt.
###  [ Where should I mail GEICO? ](https://gosuits.com/knowledge-base/how-to-file-a-geico-accident-claim-gosuits/#faq-15)
Most policy-related mail: GEICO, P.O. Box 803285, Dallas, TX 75380-3285. For official or critical correspondence: GEICO Headquarters, 5260 Western Avenue, Chevy Chase, MD 20815.
###  [ What should be included in a letter of representation or spoliation notice? ](https://gosuits.com/knowledge-base/how-to-file-a-geico-accident-claim-gosuits/#faq-16)
Insured’s name and policy number (if known), accident date/time/location, police report number, vehicle description and license plate, representation statement, and a request to preserve evidence.
###  [ Why send notice before a claim number is assigned? ](https://gosuits.com/knowledge-base/how-to-file-a-geico-accident-claim-gosuits/#faq-17)
Once the insurer receives notice, state laws (e.g., Texas Insurance Code) trigger acknowledgment deadlines. Early notice forces routing to the correct department and prompt adjuster assignment.
###  [ Can I email or upload documents to GEICO? ](https://gosuits.com/knowledge-base/how-to-file-a-geico-accident-claim-gosuits/#faq-18)
For existing claims, use the GEICO Claim Center to upload documents. For new matters without a claim number, use fax or certified mail to ensure receipt.
###  [ What is GEICO subrogation, and how could it affect me? ](https://gosuits.com/knowledge-base/how-to-file-a-geico-accident-claim-gosuits/#faq-19)
After paying you, GEICO may seek reimbursement from the at-fault party’s insurer. You might need to cooperate; an attorney can advise on obligations and protect your interests.
###  [ What if GEICO denies my claim? ](https://gosuits.com/knowledge-base/how-to-file-a-geico-accident-claim-gosuits/#faq-20)
You can appeal. Gather additional evidence and consult an attorney, who can negotiate or file a lawsuit if needed.
###  [ What should I do if the GEICO adjuster isn’t being fair? ](https://gosuits.com/knowledge-base/how-to-file-a-geico-accident-claim-gosuits/#faq-21)
Document all communications and consult an attorney. They can negotiate on your behalf and, if necessary, litigate.
###  [ What are the steps to file a GEICO claim? ](https://gosuits.com/knowledge-base/how-to-file-a-geico-accident-claim-gosuits/#faq-22)
1) File via app, online, or call 1-800-841-3000. 2) Receive a claim number (usually within 24–48 hours). 3) Speak with the adjuster. 4) Arrange vehicle inspection/repairs. 5) Provide medical documentation. 6) Review initial offer. 7) Negotiate or litigate if needed.
###  [ What are best practices when dealing with GEICO? ](https://gosuits.com/knowledge-base/how-to-file-a-geico-accident-claim-gosuits/#faq-23)
Don’t rush to settle; decline recorded statements unless required; send early preservation/representation letters; cite state-specific protections in CA, TX, and IL.
