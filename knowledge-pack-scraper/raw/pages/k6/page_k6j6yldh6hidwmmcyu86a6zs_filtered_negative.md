**[Adjust your home insurance coverage.](https://www.valuepenguin.com/how-much-homeowners-insurance-do-i-need "How Much Homeowners Insurance Do I Need?")** The coverage limits and types you choose can have a big impact on how much you pay for home insurance. Review your current coverage to make sure you actually need everything you're paying for.
For example, [replacement cost coverage](https://www.valuepenguin.com/actual-cash-value-vs-replacement-cost "Actual Cash Value vs Replacement Cost in Home Insurance") for your personal belongings can be very expensive. That's because it pays to replace your damaged belongings with brand-new items. But, if you don't mind buying used or inexpensive furniture, then this coverage may not be worth it.
**You can also consider lowering your[home insurance deductible](https://www.valuepenguin.com/homeowners-insurance-deductibles "How Do Homeowners Insurance Deductibles Work?").** A lower deductible means the insurance company pays you less to fix your home. That's why it typically results in a lower home insurance quote. However, make sure you choose a deductible you can easily pay in an emergency situation.
* * *
**[Bundle your home and car insurance policies with one company.](https://www.valuepenguin.com/best-home-car-insurance-bundle "Best Companies for Home and Auto Insurance Bundles")** This is typically the largest home insurance discount you can get.
For example, [State Farm customers typically save around 24%](https://www.valuepenguin.com/best-home-car-insurance-bundle#statefarm "Best overall bundle: State Farm") on their home and auto insurance bill when they buy both policies from the company.

**[Consider a home renovation project.](https://www.valuepenguin.com/home-renovation-insurance-savings "Can Renovating Your House Lower Your Insurance Costs?")** Insurance companies may lower your rates or give you a discount if you make updates to your home that make it more safe.
