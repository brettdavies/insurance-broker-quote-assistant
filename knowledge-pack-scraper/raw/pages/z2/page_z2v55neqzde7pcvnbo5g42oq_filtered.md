## Mississippi SR-22 Insurance Requirements

To comply with Mississippi laws, you must carry these [minimum liability limits](https://www.moneygeek.com/insurance/auto/state-minimum-car-insurance-requirements/):

* $25,000 personal injury liability per person
* $50,000 personal injury liability per accident
* $25,000 property damage liability per accident

In the state, SR-22 certificates are required for three years. This period can extend beyond three years if you don't comply with requirements or have repeated offenses.

![mglogo icon](https://res.cloudinary.com/moneygeek/image/upload/v1585171420/assets/components/icon/mg-logo_wxc8wu.svg)

TYPES OF SR-22 FILINGS

For drivers who need comprehensive coverage, Mississippi offers an SR-22 Operators-Owner Certificate option. This combines traditional and non-owner policies, providing coverage whether you're driving your own vehicle or someone else's car. This option proves particularly useful for drivers who own a vehicle but regularly operate others' cars.

### Other Mississippi SR-22 Insurance Laws and Regulations

Mississippi drivers need to understand various insurance laws and regulations beyond just filing an SR-22 to meet the state's minimum coverage requirements.

![money2 icon](https://res.cloudinary.com/moneygeek/image/upload/v1585171420/assets/components/icon/money_c8wtrb.svg)

SR-22 Filing and Reinstatement Fees

SR-22 filing fees in Mississippi range from $25 to $50, while license reinstatement fees run from $100 to $175 based on your violation type. The state offers payment plans for total fees exceeding $200 to help make costs more manageable.

![uninsured icon](https://res.cloudinary.com/moneygeek/image/upload/v1585171420/assets/components/icon/uninsured_jck1df.svg)

Coverage Lapses and Cancellations

Your insurance company must notify the Mississippi Department of Public Safety immediately if your SR-22 coverage lapses or gets canceled. This results in automatic license suspension until you file a new SR-22 and pay reinstatement fees.

![clock icon](https://res.cloudinary.com/moneygeek/image/upload/v1585171420/assets/components/icon/clock_klyche.svg)

SR-22 Requirement Duration

Mississippi requires SR-22 insurance for three years in most cases, but this extends to five years for serious violations involving injuries, death, or major property damage. For non-driving violations, the requirements are:

+ **First offense:** requires one year of SR-22 coverage
+ **Second offense within five years:** leads to a two-year suspension with continued SR-22 requirement
+ **Third offense within five years:** results in a three-year suspension with ongoing SR-22 requirement.

![insurance2 icon](https://res.cloudinary.com/moneygeek/image/upload/v1585171420/assets/components/icon/insurance_2_iixd7m.svg)

SR-22 Filing Process

To file an SR-22 in Mississippi, contact an insurance provider licensed in the state and purchase a policy meeting Mississippi's minimum coverage requirements. Your insurance company files the SR-22 electronically with the Department of Public Safety, with typical processing taking 72 hours.

![mglogo icon](https://res.cloudinary.com/moneygeek/image/upload/v1585171420/assets/components/icon/mg-logo_wxc8wu.svg)

WHAT TO DO IF PROVIDERS DENY YOU COVERAGE

Unlike many other states, Mississippi doesn't operate a state-sponsored high-risk insurance program for drivers with trouble finding coverage. However, Mississippi residents who need SR-22 insurance can still secure a policy through private insurance companies that specialize in high-risk auto insurance. Working with an independent insurance agent can help drivers compare multiple quotes from carriers willing to cover higher-risk drivers.
