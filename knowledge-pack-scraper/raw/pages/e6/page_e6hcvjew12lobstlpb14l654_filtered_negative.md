Car insurance costs vary significantly across the United States. But everything is bigger in Texas, including car insurance premiums. After a 15% average rate increase for full-coverage policies in 2024, rates in Texas continue to climb.[[1]](https://www.compare.com/auto-insurance/by-state/texas/texas-auto-insurance-requirements#cite-1) That means understanding your insurance needs has never been more crucial.
The recent surge in costs, combined with the Lone Star State’s fault-based insurance system, highlights the importance of having proper[ ](https://www.compare.com/auto-insurance/by-state/texas)[Texas car insurance coverage](https://www.compare.com/auto-insurance/by-state/texas). If you’re the at-fault driver in an accident, you face not just[ ](https://www.compare.com/auto-insurance/resources/claims-explained)[insurance claims](https://www.compare.com/auto-insurance/resources/claims-explained) but potentially significant lawsuits.
![A barn with the Texas flag painted on it](https://a.storyblok.com/f/295508/800x450/71513da760/texas-barn.jpg/m/800x450/smart/filters:quality\(90\))
Texas law currently requires all drivers to have a policy with at least 30/60/25 coverage.[[2]](https://www.compare.com/auto-insurance/by-state/texas/texas-auto-insurance-requirements#cite-2) That means:
A[ ](https://www.compare.com/auto-insurance/by-state/minimum-state-requirements)[minimum-coverage](https://www.compare.com/auto-insurance/by-state/minimum-state-requirements) policy isn’t likely to provide enough coverage unless you’re in a minor fender bender. The basic limits could fall short in scenarios like multi-vehicle collisions or if you’re responsible for[ ](https://www.compare.com/auto-insurance/resources/what-is-a-total-loss)[totaling another driver’s car](https://www.compare.com/auto-insurance/resources/what-is-a-total-loss).
![A woman with glasses driving](https://a.storyblok.com/f/295508/800x450/61962f8a92/woman-with-glasses-driving-2.jpg/m/800x450/smart/filters:quality\(90\))
If you’re one of the 20% of Texas drivers hitting the road without insurance,[[3]](https://www.compare.com/auto-insurance/by-state/texas/texas-auto-insurance-requirements#cite-3) understand that you’re facing the following penalties.
* First offense: $175–$350 fine[[4]](https://www.compare.com/auto-insurance/by-state/texas/texas-auto-insurance-requirements#cite-4)
You could have your vehicle impounded for 180 days if a court convicts you of driving without insurance.[[5]](https://www.compare.com/auto-insurance/by-state/texas/texas-auto-insurance-requirements#cite-5) After that time, you’ll need to provide proof of insurance to retrieve your vehicle.
After a second conviction of driving without insurance, you’ll need to have an SR-22 on file for two years.[[6]](https://www.compare.com/auto-insurance/by-state/texas/texas-auto-insurance-requirements#cite-6) If you don’t meet these requirements, you could face yet another license and registration suspension.
