It costs an average of $1,272 per year to insure a [VW Jetta](https://www.moneygeek.com/www.moneygeek.com/insurance/auto/vw-jetta-insurance//) across all model years. This model is the second most expensive of the six VW models included in MoneyGeek’s analysis. However, compared to all other car makes and models, it is still generally inexpensive to insure.
---|---
* The cost of insurance depends on the [model year of your VW Jetta](https://www.moneygeek.com/www.moneygeek.com/insurance/auto/vw-jetta-insurance//#vw-jetta-insurance-by-year). It can range from an average of $1,061 to $1,392 per year.
* On average, the cost of a policy for an [18-year-old with a VW Jetta](https://www.moneygeek.com/www.moneygeek.com/insurance/auto/vw-jetta-insurance//#vw-jetta-insurance-by-driver-age) is $4,152 per year. That's approximately $2,880 more expensive than the average rate for a 40-year-old driver.
* MoneyGeek evaluated various insurance companies by claims ratings, user satisfaction and financial stability to find the [best car insurance for a VW Jetta](https://www.moneygeek.com/www.moneygeek.com/insurance/auto/vw-jetta-insurance//#best-vw-jetta-insurance) for drivers who aren’t just focused on price.

[On average, the cost of VW insurance is $1,245 per year](https://www.moneygeek.com/insurance/auto/vw-car-insurance/#k). However, rates may vary depending on your car’s model.
MoneyGeek’s analysis found that the VW ID.4 is the [most expensive VW model to insure](https://www.moneygeek.com/insurance/auto/vw-car-insurance/#vw-id-insurance-costs). The average annual insurance rate for a VW ID. 4 is $1,679.
The VW Tiguan is the [cheapest VW model to insure](https://www.moneygeek.com/insurance/auto/vw-car-insurance/#vw-tiguan-insurance-costs). A policy for this model costs an average of $1,165 per year.
