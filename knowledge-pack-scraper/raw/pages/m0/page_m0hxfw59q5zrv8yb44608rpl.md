## Allstate vs. State Farm: Coverage Options
Allstate and State Farm rank No. 1 and 2, respectively, in our study of the[ best renters insurance companies](https://www.usnews.com/insurance/renters-insurance). State Farm tops our rating of the [cheapest renters insurance companies](https://www.usnews.com/insurance/renters-insurance/cheapest-renters-insurance-companies), with an average monthly premium of $13.04, while Allstate ranks fifth, at $21.50 per month.
Both insurers provide standard renters insurance policies that include [coverage](https://www.usnews.com/insurance/renters-insurance/what-does-renters-insurance-cover) for personal property, liability, loss of use, and guest medical expenses.
They also offer optional coverages that differ. Allstate offers scheduled personal property coverage, a personal umbrella policy, identity theft restoration, and flood insurance. In contrast, State Farm offers back-up for sewer or drain, earthquake, cyber event, identity restoration, and fraud loss.
Both companies offer multipolicy discounts when you bundle your renters and auto insurance policies. However, Allstate offers more ways to save than State Farm, including a discount for those aged 55 and up, an easy pay plan discount, and a windstorm mitigation discount for Florida homeowners. State Farm offers a home-alert protection discount and a claims-free discount.
Allstate is available in all 50 states and Washington, D.C., while State Farm is available in 47 states and Washington, D.C., excluding California, Massachusetts, and Rhode Island.
### Do Allstate and State Farm Cover Bedbugs?
Neither company covers damage caused by bedbugs. A standard renters insurance policy typically doesn't cover pest infestations, as they are considered preventable with regular home maintenance.
### Do Allstate and State Farm Cover Water Damage?
Allstate and State Farm cover accidental water damage, such as broken plumbing or appliances, as well as water that seeps in from other units. However, neither company covers flood damage.
### Do Allstate and State Farm Cover Flood Damage?
Flood damage is excluded from most renters insurance policies, including Allstate and State Farm. Flood damage is typically defined as water that intrudes from outside or underground sources.
### Do Allstate and State Farm Cover Mold Damage?
Mold damage isn't typically covered by a standard policy. However, if mold damage occurs due to a covered peril, such as accidental water damage, it may be covered.
### Do Allstate and State Farm Cover Property Damage?
Both companies cover personal property, such as clothing, furniture, and electronics, up to a specified amount. However, if you have high-value items, such as expensive jewelry or artwork, consider purchasing additional coverage to ensure your belongings are fully protected.
### Do Allstate and State Farm Cover Fire Damage?
Allstate and State Farm both cover damage caused by fire, as it's included in their list of covered perils. It can protect your personal property and cover additional living expenses if you need to relocate due to a fire.
### Do Allstate and State Farm Cover Car Theft?
Renters insurance policies don't cover car theft. However, your auto insurance policy should kick in if your car is stolen. On the other hand, both companies will cover you if personal items are stolen from your vehicle, whether on or off the rental property.
### Do Allstate and State Farm Cover Moving Costs?
Neither company covers standard moving costs. However, if you're displaced from your home due to a covered peril, the additional living expenses portion of your policy may reimburse you for moving costs.
### Do Allstate and State Farm Cover Temporary Housing?
Both companies cover temporary housing under the additional living expenses portion of your renters insurance policy. This coverage can help pay for increased living costs, including restaurant and hotel bills.
### Do Allstate and State Farm Offer Renters Insurance Bundling?
You can bundle your Allstate or State Farm renters insurance policy with another insurance policy to receive a discount. Multipolicy discounts are a common way to save on your premium.

## Bottom Line
Allstate and State Farm are solid choices for renters insurance. They both offer standard policies and various optional coverages and discounts. You can also get an online quote with both companies.
