## Texas SR-22 Insurance Requirements

To comply with Texas laws, you must carry these [minimum liability limits](https://www.moneygeek.com/insurance/auto/state-minimum-car-insurance-requirements/):

* $30,000 personal injury liability per person
* $60,000 personal injury liability per accident
* $25,000 property damage liability per accident

In the state, SR-22 certificates are usually required for two years. It can be extended to three years or more, depending on the violation and if there is noncompliance with requirements or repeated offenses."

TYPES OF SR-22 FILINGS

Texas drivers can also opt for a comprehensive solution called the SR-22 Operators-Owner Certificate. This combined filing offers the best of both worlds, providing coverage whether you're driving your own vehicle or someone else's car. This option proves particularly useful for drivers who regularly operate both owned and borrowed vehicles.

### Other Texas SR-22 Insurance Laws and Regulations

While filing an SR-22 helps meet Texas's basic insurance requirements, drivers must understand several additional coverage laws and regulations.

![money2 icon](https://res.cloudinary.com/moneygeek/image/upload/v1585171420/assets/components/icon/money_c8wtrb.svg)

SR-22 Filing and Reinstatement Fees

SR-22 filing fees in Texas range from $15 to $25, while license reinstatement fees range from $100 to $325, based on your violation type. Texas offers payment plans for fees exceeding $100 through the Department of Public Safety.

![uninsured icon](https://res.cloudinary.com/moneygeek/image/upload/v1585171420/assets/components/icon/uninsured_jck1df.svg)

Coverage Lapses and Cancellations

Your insurance company must notify the Texas Department of Public Safety if your SR-22 coverage lapses or gets canceled. This results in immediate license suspension until you file a new SR-22 and pay reinstatement fees.

![clock icon](https://res.cloudinary.com/moneygeek/image/upload/v1585171420/assets/components/icon/clock_klyche.svg)

SR-22 Requirement Length

Texas requires SR-22 insurance for 2 years, but this extends to three years for serious violations involving injuries, death, or major property damage. For non-driving violations:

+ **First offense:** requires two years of SR-22 coverage
+ **Second offense within five years:** adds a 180-day license suspension plus continued SR-22 requirement
+ **Third offense within five years:** results in a one-year license suspension plus continued SR-22 requirement.

![insurance2 icon](https://res.cloudinary.com/moneygeek/image/upload/v1585171420/assets/components/icon/insurance_2_iixd7m.svg)

SR-22 Filing Process

Contact an insurance company licensed in Texas to purchase a policy meeting state minimum requirements. The insurer files the SR-22 electronically with the Texas Department of Public Safety. Standard processing takes about 72 hours.

![mglogo icon](https://res.cloudinary.com/moneygeek/image/upload/v1585171420/assets/components/icon/mg-logo_wxc8wu.svg)

WHAT TO DO IF PROVIDERS DENY YOU COVERAGE

Texas provides coverage options through the Texas Automobile Insurance Plan Association (TAIPA) for drivers who can't secure standard coverage. To qualify for TAIPA coverage, drivers must show proof of rejection from at least two insurance companies within the past 60 days.
