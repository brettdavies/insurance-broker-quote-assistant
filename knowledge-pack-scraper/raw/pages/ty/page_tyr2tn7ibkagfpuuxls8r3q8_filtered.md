# Financial goals by age: a decade-by-decade approach

Written by [State Farm® Editorial Team](https://www.statefarm.com/simple-insights/editorial-team-and-process)

Help meet your financial goals and stay on track for retirement with these age-based saving tips.

The specifics of your [financial plan](/simple-insights/financial/follow-these-steps-to-achieve-financial-wellness) and goals may not resemble anyone else's. Maybe you want to trek the globe after you retire while your neighbor plans to dive into full-time volunteer work. Regardless, some shared steps can help get you on track for your future.

A common financial regret for many people is not [saving early enough for retirement](/simple-insights/retirement/retirement-advice-for-millennials-and-gen-x). Here's a breakdown of some money goals by age.

## Financial goals for your 20s

* Establish a consistent income and [good spending habits](/simple-insights/financial/the-psychology-of-spending-habits-and-mistakes).
* Sustain (and live with) a [budget](/simple-insights/financial/budget-breakdown).
* [Eliminate debt](/simple-insights/financial/how-to-get-out-of-debt) (credit cards or loans), beginning with debt that has the highest interest.
* Enroll in [health insurance](/insurance/health).
* Start investing in a 401(k) and remember to contribute enough to receive the [maximum employer match](/simple-insights/financial/how-employer-401k-matching-works).
* Begin to develop an [emergency fund](/simple-insights/financial/build-an-emergency-fund) to help prepare for any unexpected financial situations.
* Open savings accounts for big expenses and investment accounts for retirement.

**Retirement savings for your 20s:** Some experts recommend saving funds equivalent to one year of your annual salary or saving 15 to 25% of your pay as you receive it.

## Financial goals for your 30s

* Fill your emergency fund with [three to six months' salary](/simple-insights/financial/building-an-emergency-fund-calculate-how-much-to-save).
* Eliminate additional non-mortgage debt, including [student loans](/simple-insights/financial/should-you-refinance-student-loans).
* Work on building up a [good credit score](/simple-insights/financial/what-is-a-good-credit-score) in the 700s or higher, if possible.
* Create a [will](/simple-insights/family/what-is-a-will-and-why-do-you-need-one) and get [life insurance](/insurance/life).
* Maximize the match on [employer-sponsored retirement plans](/simple-insights/financial/how-employer-401k-matching-works).
* Put [20% down on a home](/simple-insights/residence/how-to-save-for-a-down-payment-on-a-house).
* Build kids' [education savings funds](/simple-insights/college/how-a-529-plan-works).

**Retirement savings by age 30:** Some experts recommend having two to three times your annual salary saved.

## Financial goals for your 40s

* Maximize personal contributions to [retirement accounts](/simple-insights/retirement/steps-you-can-take-now-to-save-for-retirement)[,](https://www.statefarm.com/simple-insights/retirement/steps-you-can-take-now-to-save-for-retirement) [such as Roth and Traditional IRAs](/finances/retirement-plans-iras/individual-retirement-accounts).
* Increase income (negotiate a raise or add an income stream).
* Consider additional investments to help diversify your portfolio.
* Pay extra on your mortgage, and [consider paying it off early](/simple-insights/residence/pay-off-mortgage-early).
* Discuss your [parents' finances](/simple-insights/financial/how-to-talk-about-money-with-aging-parents) and [estate plans](/simple-insights/estate/does-everyone-need-estate-planning).
* Map out [college payment options](/simple-insights/college/give-the-gift-of-education) with your kids (without touching retirement savings).

**Retirement savings by age 40:** Some experts recommend making extra contributions and having six times your annual salary saved.

## Financial goals for your 50s

* Take advantage of [catch-up contributions](https://www.irs.gov/retirement-plans/plan-participant-employee/retirement-topics-catch-up-contributions).
* Get [long-term care](/simple-insights/health-insurance/dont-wait-too-long-to-get-longterm-care-coverage) insurance.
* Check on your investment portfolio regularly, looking for ways to maximize value.
* Look into [financial advisors](/simple-insights/financial/when-do-i-need-a-financial-advisor) who can help you manage your situation.
* Get educated on Medicare, Social Security and other retirement benefits.
* Increase your contributions to your 401(k) plan now that you can use the over 50 tax advantage.
* Put aside medical savings and maximize your [health savings account](/simple-insights/health-insurance/key-health-savings-account-benefits).
* Revisit your will.

**Retirement savings by age 50:** Some experts recommend having seven to eight times your annual salary saved.

* Begin to develop an emergency fund.

* Create a will and get life insurance.
* Build kids’ education savings funds.
* Build a good credit score in the 700s.
* Eliminate additional non-mortgage debt.
* Fill your emergency fund with 3-6 months’ salary.
* Maximize match on employer-sponsored retirement plans.

**Retirement savings by 30**: Some experts recommend saving **2-3x** your annual salary.

## Financial goals: Your 40s

* Increase income.
* Map out college payment options with your kids.
* Discuss your parents’ finances and estate plans.
* Maximize contributions to Roth and Traditional IRAs.
* Consider additional investments to diversify your portfolio.
* Pay extra on your mortgage and consider paying it off early.

**Retirement savings by 40**: Some experts recommend saving **6x** your annual salary or making extra catch-up contributions.

## Financial goals: Your 50s

* Revisit your will.
* Look into financial advisors.
* Get long-term care insurance.
* Maximize your health savings account.
* Take advantage of catch-up contributions.
* Check on your investment portfolio regularly.
* Increase 401(k) plan contributions using the over 50 tax advantage.
* Get educated on Medicare, Social Security & other retirement benefits.

**Retirement savings by 50**: Some experts recommend saving **7-8x** your annual salary.

## Financial goals: Your 60s

* Enroll in Medicare.
* Finalize an estate plan.
* Finish paying off all remaining debt.
* Decide when to take Social Security.
* Plan out a retirement income strategy.
* Make retirement plans and tweak contributions.
* Think about downsizing or selling off unneeded assets.

**Retirement savings by 60**: Some experts recommend having **10x** your annual salary saved.
