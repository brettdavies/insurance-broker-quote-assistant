## How are homeowners insurance rates calculated in New York?

Ever wonder why you pay what you do for home insurance? Some things may be obvious, like the age of your home. But other factors that contribute to the price of homeowners insurance in New York may not be as obvious. Here’s a list of some of the things homeowners insurance companies consider when quoting you a rate:

* The level of coverage required
* The size of your mortgage
* Your credit score
* The square footage of your home
* The geographic location of your home in New York
* The condition of your home
* The age of your home
* The proximity of your home to a fire station
* Your previous claims record
* The amount of your deductible
* Optional add-ons like umbrella insurance

[Zillow](https://www.zillow.com/mortgage-learning/homeowners-insurance/) estimates that homeowners can expect to pay approximately $35 each month for every $100,000 of your home's value.

The average cost of homeowners insurance in New York is cheaper than the national average cost that Americans pay for home insurance. Nearby states like Massachusetts and Connecticut pay more on average for the same amount of coverage. New York's lower home insurance premiums might be due to the fact that fewer homeowners are competing for policies.

When looking around for home insurance in New York, you'll come across several coverage options. To determine what type of home insurance coverage you need, you need to learn the most common causes of loss in the state. There are a few common causes of property losses that apply to most of New York: natural disasters, vandalism, burglary, and fire.

A standard policy in New York includes: dwelling coverage, liability coverage, personal property coverage, medical payments coverage, and additional living expenses. In order to tailor your homeowners policy to your needs, you might want to consider some optional coverage types. Extra coverage options such as: flood insurance, identity theft coverage, and personal property replacement cost can help you better protect your finances.

Talking to a licensed agent might help you figure out what coverages types are the most appropriate for your unique situation.
