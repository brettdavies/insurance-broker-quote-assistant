Texas Minimum Coverage Requirements & What They Cover

If you drive in Texas, the law says you’ve gotta have liability insurance to cover any injuries or damage you might cause in a crash. These Texas car insurance requirements help ensure you’re financially responsible if you’re at fault for someone else’s medical bills or car repairs.

You’ll need to show proof of insurance if you get pulled over, get in a wreck, or when it’s time to renew your registration—skip it, and you could end up with a fine, a suspended license, or even your car getting impounded. However, basic liability doesn’t cover your damages. That’s why many drivers add collision coverage to pay for repairs to their vehicle, regardless of fault.

In Texas, meeting the 30/60/25 liability minimum is required, but it won’t cover your own vehicle after a crash.

Comprehensive coverage protects against theft, vandalism, and weather-related damage like hail. Personal injury protection (PIP) auto insurance kicks in to help with your medical bills and lost wages after a crash, even if you were the one who caused it. It’s a solid backup when things go wrong and helps you meet car insurance requirements in Texas while avoiding major financial setbacks.

Auto Insurance Requirements in Texas

To drive legally in Texas, your auto insurance policy must include liability coverage with the following state-mandated minimum limits:

  • $30,000 for bodily injury to one person in a single crash
  • $60,000 total for bodily injuries to multiple people per accident
  • $25,000 for damage to someone else’s vehicle or property

This 30/60/25 liability coverage is the minimum allowed under Texas auto insurance requirements and won’t cover your own vehicle or medical costs. You’ll need to show proof of insurance if you’re pulled over, involved in a crash, or registering your vehicle.

If you don’t meet these requirements, you could face a $350 fine the first time, up to $1,000 for repeat offenses, plus license suspension and even vehicle impoundment. The bottom line is that having this coverage isn’t optional; it’s the law.

Auto Insurance Coverage Options in Texas

If you drive in Texas, knowing what your insurance covers matters. Liability insurance is the minimum, covering injuries or damage you cause. But it won’t cover your car. That’s what collision insurance is for. It helps pay for repairs if you crash into another vehicle, a pole, or a pothole.

How to Prove Financial Responsibility in Texas

It’s not enough in Texas to have car insurance—you also need to show you’re financially responsible. The state gives you three ways to do that, each with its own rules.

  • Insurance Card: This is the easiest and most common option. Your insurance company issues it, and you must show it if you’re pulled over or in an accident. Forgetting it can lead to fines or even losing your license.
  • Surety Bond: This means you pay a bond company to cover any damage you might cause. It usually costs more than regular insurance and requires a clean driving history.
  • Self-Insurance: Usually for people or businesses with lots of vehicles. You must prove to the DMV that you have enough money to cover any accidents.

Whichever option you choose, it’s how you stay on the right side of Texas law. Your proof of responsibility shows you’re covered if something goes wrong. It also helps you avoid penalties that could hit your wallet or your driving record.

Shopping for auto insurance in Texas isn’t just about picking the cheapest deal. It’s about knowing what affects your rate. Where you live can make a big difference. If you’re in Houston, you might pay around $91 a month thanks to higher accident and theft rates, while drivers in Killeen or Lubbock might see rates closer to $72.

Age plays a big part, too. An 18-year-old guy could pay over $330 monthly, but by the time you’re in your 40s, it might drop to under $75. And if you’ve had a ticket or accident? That could bump your rate up fast.

Getting covered is pretty easy once you’ve got a handle on what impacts your price. Start by checking out quotes from big-name companies like Geico, State Farm, or Progressive. To drive legally, you’ll need to meet Texas auto insurance minimums, which follow the 30/60/25 liability limits, but you can always build more coverage on top.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is car insurance required in Texas?

Yes, you must carry liability insurance in Texas to legally drive. The state requires proof of financial responsibility at all times.

What is the minimum car insurance required in Texas?

Texas law says you need at least 30/60/25 liability coverage—$30,000 for one person’s injuries, $60,000 total per accident, and $25,000 for property damage.

What insurance is mandatory in Texas?

In Texas, you’re only legally required to have liability insurance. That covers injuries or damage you cause to someone else if you’re at fault in a crash. But if you ever need to file a car accident claim to fix your car, liability won’t cut it—you’ll need collision or comprehensive coverage.

Do I have to have comprehensive and collision coverage in Texas?

No, but your lender will likely require both if you’re financing or leasing a car. Without them, damage to your vehicle isn’t covered.

Do I have to add my teen to my car insurance in Texas?

Yes, once your teen is licensed, Texas law requires them to be listed on your policy to ensure coverage while driving your vehicle.

What is the most affordable auto insurance in Texas?

USAA and State Farm offer the cheapest monthly minimum coverage—around $36 and $52 per month, respectively—based on driver profile and location. According to recent State Farm insurance reviews, the company consistently earns high marks for affordability, especially for drivers meeting Texas minimum liability requirements.

How do you not have auto insurance in Texas?

If you don’t carry insurance, you must file a surety bond or self-insure, requiring state approval and significant financial backing.

How much auto insurance should I have in Texas?

While 30/60/25 is the legal minimum, many drivers carry higher limits, plus collision and comprehensive coverage to protect their vehicle and finances.

Who has the best auto insurance in Texas?

USAA, State Farm, and Geico are top-rated for monthly rates, customer satisfaction, and complaint ratios, especially for meeting Texas minimum liability coverage. Recent Geico insurance reviews highlight the company’s strong performance in affordability and reliability for Texas drivers.

Do all household members need to be on car insurance in Texas?

Any licensed driver in your household should be listed or excluded in writing. Otherwise, claims could be denied after an accident.

What happens if the driver at fault in an accident has no insurance in Texas?

If the other driver doesn’t have insurance, you’ll probably have to lean on your uninsured motorist coverage to cover the costs. They could also get hit with fines, lose their license, and be required to file an SR-22.
