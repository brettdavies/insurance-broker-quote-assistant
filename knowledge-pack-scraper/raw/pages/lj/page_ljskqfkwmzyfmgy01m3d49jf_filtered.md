### Accident-free or 'good driver' discount

## Personal traits discounts

Car insurance discounts aren‚Äôt always based on your driving history or vehicle. You could earn one for getting good grades or owning a home.

### Good student discount

Most insurers offer discounts to full-time students who earn good grades (typically at least a B average). However, age restrictions vary by carrier. For example, [Nationwide](https://www.nerdwallet.com/reviews/insurance/nationwide-insurance) offers this discount to students ages 16 to 24.

### Homeowner discount

Do you own a house? You could earn a car insurance discount, even if you don‚Äôt buy homeowners insurance from your auto insurer.

### Student away-at-school discount

Students at college who leave their cars behind will naturally be driving less than usual. Because of this, certain insurers offer a discount. Requirements vary by company but usually include a minimum distance to the school and age limits. For example, Progressive requires drivers to be in school 100 miles away from home and be 22 or younger to qualify.

## Customer loyalty discounts

Insurers reward loyalty with discounts for longtime customers and those who have multiple policies or cars insured under the same policy.

A word of caution about loyalty discounts: Some auto insurers use a practice called [price optimization](https://www.nerdwallet.com/article/insurance/price-optimization) to charge loyal customers higher rates because they‚Äôre less likely to switch companies. That‚Äôs why it‚Äôs so important to [compare car insurance rates](https://www.nerdwallet.com/a/insurance/car-insurance), even if you‚Äôre receiving a loyalty (or other) discount. Your final rate may still be higher with a loyalty discount at your current insurer than with another company.

### Loyalty discount

Some insurers reward drivers who continue to renew policies with them.

### Multiple cars discount

Most insurers offer this discount if you have multiple vehicles insured with their company. Certain companies, such as [Progressive](https://www.nerdwallet.com/reviews/insurance/progressive), let you add cars you don‚Äôt own to your policy, so long as the vehicles are parked at your address.

### Multiple policy (bundling) discount

This discount applies if you buy more than one policy from the same insurer. For example, you might qualify when you get home and auto insurance from the same company. You can usually bundle a wide range of products, from boat to RV to [renters insurance](https://www.nerdwallet.com/blog/insurance/find-best-renters-insurance/).

## Policy-related discounts

Policy-related car insurance discounts are some of the easiest to qualify for. They are earned based on how you buy and pay for your policy.

### Automatic payments discount

Do you have trouble remembering to pay your bills? Switching to automatic payments could score you a discount and solve any worries about forgetting to pay on time.

ü§ìNerdy Tip

Before using auto-pay, make sure you‚Äôll have enough funds in your account each month to cover your insurance costs. You may not receive a warning if you don‚Äôt.

### Early shopper discount

As long as you don‚Äôt need insurance right away, you may be able to get an early shopper discount. To qualify, some insurers require getting a quote with them before your current policy expires. Others want you to get a quote a full seven days before your new policy goes into effect.

### Paid-in-full discount

While it‚Äôs not always easy to pull off, you could earn a discount for paying your entire policy upfront, whether it‚Äôs a six-month or yearlong policy. If you need to cancel your policy later, most insurers will refund you for any remaining months‚Äô worth of premiums left on your policy. Just make sure to confirm these details with your company before paying in full.

### Paperless discount

One of the easiest ways to earn a discount is by switching to electronic statements, which eliminates getting a monthly bill in the mail.

## How to maximize discounts and savings

Insurance discounts aren‚Äôt always straightforward. Just because one company has an accident-free discount after three years doesn‚Äôt mean the next company will have the same guidelines. Follow these tips when looking at auto insurance discounts.

### Ask about discounts

You don‚Äôt need to wait until you renew your policy to see whether you‚Äôre eligible for a discount on auto insurance. Review the discounts on your company‚Äôs website, and ask a company representative or your agent whether you qualify. You should always talk to a representative to confirm available discounts because terms can change and discounts aren‚Äôt always automatically applied.

¬ª MORE: [Get free car insurance quotes](https://www.nerdwallet.com/p/insurance/car-insurance-quotes)

### Consider overall premium cost, not just discounts

Although discounts can save you money on your car insurance, remember to look at the overall cost and not just the number of discounts you receive. For example, a company that offers no discounts could end up providing a lower [car insurance premium](https://www.nerdwallet.com/article/insurance/car-insurance-premium) than another insurer that charges a higher premium and offers 20 discounts.

### Shop around

To find the best price and company for you, shop around for [car insurance quotes](https://www.nerdwallet.com/p/insurance/car-insurance-quotes) at least once a year. Comparing rates from three or more companies will help you find the lowest premium. When shopping around for quotes, you may even be provided with a list of auto insurance discounts you qualify for.
