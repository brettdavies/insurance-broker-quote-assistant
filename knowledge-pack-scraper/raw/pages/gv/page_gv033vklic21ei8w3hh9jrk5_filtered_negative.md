# Home Insurance Facts and Statistics: Cost, Coverage & Claims
![](https://res.cloudinary.com/value-penguin/image/upload/w_30/employee_photos/Lindsay_Bishop_VP_V2)
Written by [ Lindsay Bishop ](https://www.valuepenguin.com/author/lindsay-bishop)
Updated Sep 3, 2025
On this page: Rate trends Coverage trends Types of claims Claims by state Largest companies
* Homeowners pay an [average of $2,151 per year for insurance Trends in the average cost of home insurance ](https://www.valuepenguin.com/home-insurance-statistics#cost "Trends in the average cost of home insurance") in the U.S.
* Homeowners are also [buying more coverage Property insurance coverage trends ](https://www.valuepenguin.com/home-insurance-statistics#coverage "Property insurance coverage trends"). The most common amount of dwelling coverage was between $325,000 and $349,999 in 2022. That's up from $200,000 to $299,999 in 2017.
* The majority of [home insurance claims Household insurance claims statistics ](https://www.valuepenguin.com/home-insurance-statistics#claims "Household insurance claims statistics") are for wind damage. In 2023, homeowners filed over 1.7 million claims for wind damage, which was 32.6% of the total claims filed. Most claims were due to severe thunderstorms.
* Colorado was the [state with the most  wildfire and flood losses in 2023 Common home insurance claims by state ](https://www.valuepenguin.com/home-insurance-statistics#claims-state "Common home insurance claims by state"). Hawaii had the highest average cost per claim.
* The [20 largest home insurance companies Largest homeowners insurance companies ](https://www.valuepenguin.com/home-insurance-statistics#companies "Largest homeowners insurance companies") account for nearly 74% of the U.S. insurance market. State Farm leads the market with an 18.2% share.

Severe weather events, such as hurricanes and tropical storms, made up 62% of wind-related insurance claims in 2023, according to  LexisNexis.
This is partially because there were 20  named storms in 2023, including Hurricane Lee and Hurricane Idalia. This is much higher than the average of 14 storms per year.
---|---
**The majority of water claims aren't caused by weather.** In 2023, burst pipes and leaky appliances made up  20.1% of home insurance claims. The cost of these claims decreased by 11.2% from 2022 to 2023.
