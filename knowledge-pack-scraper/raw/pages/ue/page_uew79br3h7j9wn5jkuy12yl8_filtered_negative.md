Here’s a clear and[**organized commission chart**](https://healthnewsreporting.com/schemes-and-policies/oriental-insurance-agent-commission-chart-2025/) for National Insurance Company Limited (NICL) agents:
---|---|---
*     * **Life Insurance:** [**Higher national insurance agent commission**](https://healthnewsreporting.com/schemes-and-policies/motor-insurance-agent-commission-chart-2025/)on first-year premiums (40%–100%), lower on renewals.
* **Health Insurance:** 15%–25% for new policies; renewal commissions are lower (5%–10%).
* **Motor Insurance:**
* **Own Damage (OD):** 10%–15% commission.
* **Third-Party (TP):** 2%–3% commission.
* **Commercial Insurance:** Varies based on risk, typically 10%–20% of the premium.
*     * **Long-Term Policies:** Often provide[**better commission rates**](https://healthnewsreporting.com/schemes-and-policies/pnb-metlife-agent-commission-chart-2025/).
* **Short-Term Policies:** Lower commissions and quicker renewals.
*     * **New Policies:** Usually have a **higher commission rate**.
* **Renewals:** Offer **lower commission rates**, but create **steady income** over time.
*     * High-performing agents may receive [**bonus commissions**](https://healthnewsreporting.com/schemes-and-policies/sahara-agent-commission-chart-2025/), **incentives**, or **recognition rewards**.
* Consistent policy sales and **renewal retention rates** can increase total earnings.
*     * **Single-Year Policies:** Pay commissions yearly.
* **Multi-Year Policies:** Often provide **upfront commissions** for the entire duration.
* **Regulatory Changes:****IRDAI policies** directly impact commission caps and structures across different insurance sectors.
* **Geographic Location:****Urban vs. Rural:** Commission rates can differ based on **market demand** and **company strategy** in different regions.
