* [Bodily injury liability ](https://www.agilerates.com/advice/auto/bodily-injury-liability/)
The state of Illinois only imposes fines and a short, temporary suspension of driving privileges for the offending motorist. The fines are on the expensive side, and an [SR-22 certificate](https://www.agilerates.com/advice/auto/car-insurance-with-an-sr-22/) will be required every time, starting with the second offense.
---|---|---
* [**UM/UIM property damage liability**](https://www.agilerates.com/advice/auto/uninsured-and-underinsured-motorist-coverage/): Illinois only mandates UM/UIM bodily injury coverage, so if a driver wants protection for their damage coverage, UM/UIM property damage coverage will help
* [**Comprehensive coverage**](https://www.agilerates.com/advice/auto/comprehensive-car-insurance/): This coverage pays out claims for damages to the vehicle due to acts of god or other accidental circumstances; all the policyholder has to do is pay a deductible
* [**Gap coverage**](https://www.agilerates.com/advice/auto/gap-insurance/): Helps the driver of a newer vehicle pay off their loan if it gets totaled in an accident

* $100,000/$300,000 [bodily injury](https://www.agilerates.com/advice/auto/bodily-injury-liability/) liability coverage per person/per accident
* [Collision](https://www.agilerates.com/advice/auto/collision-insurance/) & comprehensive coverage
Once a driver receives [notice of a cancellation](https://www.agilerates.com/advice/auto/what-to-do-if-your-auto-insurance-policy-is-canceled/), they have 30 days to find new coverage. Failure to find coverage can result in legal consequences if the motorist is caught driving without insurance.
PIP ([personal injury protection](https://www.agilerates.com/advice/auto/personal-injury-protection/)) is not required in Illinois. This type of coverage is usually only required in no-fault states because it helps pay for medical expenses for drivers who are hurt in an accident that isn’t their fault.
Mandatory minimum auto insurance coverage isn’t very extensive or complicated in Illinois. But motorists do have to make sure their vehicle is in good shape in order to avoid policy cancellation.
