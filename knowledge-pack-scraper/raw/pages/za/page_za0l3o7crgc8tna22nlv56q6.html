Washington SR-22 Insurance Requirements

To comply with Washington laws, you must carry these minimum liability limits:

  • $25,000 personal injury liability per person
  • $50,000 personal injury liability per accident
  • $10,000 property damage liability per accident

In the state, SR-22 certificates are usually required for three years. It can be extended beyond three years, depending on the violation and if there is noncompliance with requirements or repeated offenses.

TYPES OF SR-22 FILINGS

For the most comprehensive coverage, Washington drivers can choose an SR-22 Operators-Owner Certificate. This joint filing combines traditional and non-owner policies, ensuring you're covered whether driving your own vehicle or someone else's. This option proves particularly useful for those who both own a car and regularly operate other vehicles.

Other Washington SR-22 Insurance Laws and Regulations

While SR-22 certification meets Washington's basic insurance requirements, drivers must also comply with several additional state regulations and coverage rules.

    SR-22 Filing and Reinstatement Fees

    SR-22 filing fees in Washington range from $15 to $25, while license reinstatement fees run from $75 to $150 based on violation type. Washington offers payment plans for fees exceeding $100, making costs more manageable for drivers.

    Coverage Lapses and Cancellations

    If your SR-22 insurance lapses or gets canceled, your insurance company must notify the Washington Department of Licensing immediately. Your license will be suspended again until you file a new SR-22 and satisfy all reinstatement requirements.

    SR-22 Requirement Duration

    SR-22 requirements typically last three years in Washington, but can extend to 5 years for serious violations involving injuries, death, or major property damage. For non-driving-related suspensions: 

    • First offense: requires three years of SR-22 coverage
    • Second offense within five years: leads to a four-year suspension with continued SR-22 requirement
    • Third offense within five years: results in a five-year suspension with ongoing SR-22 requirement.
    SR-22 Filing Process

    To file an SR-22 in Washington, contact an insurance company licensed in the state and purchase a policy meeting Washington's minimum requirements. The insurance company files the SR-22 electronically with the Department of Licensing, with processing typically completed within 72 hours.

WHAT TO DO IF PROVIDERS DENY YOU COVERAGE

Washington drivers can find cover through the Washington Automobile Insurance Plan (WAIP). This program helps drivers who have been denied coverage by matching them with participating insurance companies.

Cheap SR-22 Insurance in Washington: Bottom Line

In Washington, drivers with severe violations must file an SR-22 to prove they have minimum coverage. This requirement lasts three years from the conviction date, depending on the offense. While an SR-22 costs more, cheap car insurance options are available for these drivers.
