# Everything You Should Know About Progressive Flood Insurance
* Post published:
Standard [homeowner’s insurance](https://www.johnperryinsurance.com/personal/home-owners-insurance.html) and renter’s insurance do not cover flood damage. And even a little flooding might have catastrophic financial repercussions. One foot of water may inflict $27,150 in damages to a 1,000-square-foot property, according to [FEMA](https://www.fema.gov/), and the average claim exceeds $38,000.
In this article, **John Perry Insurance** will discuss the coverage of Progressive Flood Insurance.
Call **John Perry Insurance** at [**239-204-3211**](tel:2392043211) or [**message us online**](https://www.johnperryinsurance.com/contact-us.html) to request pricing.
A [flood insurance policy](https://www.johnperryinsurance.com/personal/flood-insurance.html) will typically cover you if groundwater rises and floods your home, which is not typically covered by homeowners insurance policies. In most cases, flood insurance will cover the majority of your home.
The [National Flood Insurance Program ](https://www.floodsmart.gov/)provides up to $250,000 in coverage for the structure of your home. Unless it’s in the basement, carpeting and personal property are usually covered as well.
The [**National Flood Insurance Program**](https://www.fema.gov/flood-insurance) recommends that homeowners purchase flood insurance, especially if they live in a high-risk area. If you live near a river or body of water, you may be required to have it.
Whether you buy a flood policy from the NFIP or [John Perry Insurance](https://www.johnperryinsurance.com/), the following exclusions may apply:
If you live in a high-risk area, your mortgage lender may require you to purchase a [flood insurance policy](https://www.johnperryinsurance.com/5-advantages-of-having-flood-insurance-in-southwest-florida/) as a condition of the loan. Otherwise, you are not legally required to purchase one. According to FEMA, an inch of water can cause $25,000 in damage to your home.
If you have [comprehensive coverage](https://www.johnperryinsurance.com/what-are-the-three-primary-types-of-car-insurance-coverage/) on a car that has been flooded, your [auto insurance policy](https://www.johnperryinsurance.com/personal/auto-insurance.html) may pay to repair or replace it. A deductible is usually required.
[John Perry Insurance](https://www.johnperryinsurance.com/) agents have years of experience in preparing your flood insurance policy to cover this unexpected devastation. In most cases, your flood insurance will cover such disasters as a sewer line backing up into your house.
