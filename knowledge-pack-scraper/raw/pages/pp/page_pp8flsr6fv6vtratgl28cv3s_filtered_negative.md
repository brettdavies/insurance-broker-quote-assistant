![Is Bodily Injury Liability Insurance Required in Florida](https://www.meldonlaw.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/06/Is-Bodily-Injury-Liability-Insurance-Required-in-Florida-200x200.jpg)
1. Bodily Injury Liability coverage is not required to drive an automobile in Florida legally. However, it can be required if you have been convicted of a DUI. [Under Florida Statutes § 324.023](https://www.flsenate.gov/Laws/Statutes/2023/0324.023), _“Financial responsibility for bodily injury or death,”_ **you must have Bodily Injury insurance if you are convicted of a DUI**. If you were convicted on or before October 1, 2007, the minimum requirement is $10,000 per person and $20,000 per incident. If you were convicted after October 1, 2007, the minimum required is $100,000 per person and $300,000 per accident. According to the Statute, you must have BI coverage for 3 years after the return of your driving privileges.
Bodily Injury Liability insurance will cover the accident-related medical expenses of the policyholder injuries in a car accident. Personal Injury Protection will help cover **the policyholder’s own medical expenses,[regardless of who caused the traffic accident](https://www.meldonlaw.com/blog/is-florida-a-no-fault-state-car-accidents/)**.
