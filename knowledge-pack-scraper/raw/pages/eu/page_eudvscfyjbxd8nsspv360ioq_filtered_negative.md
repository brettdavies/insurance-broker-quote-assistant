* Extends your existing policies and covers claims excluded by basic policies, such as libel or slander.
* Helps protect your personal assets — home, retirement savings, and more — from being used to cover legal or medical expenses.
* Provides millions in liability protection at a relatively low cost.
* If a lawsuit exceeds your policy limits, your personal assets (home, savings, future income) could be at risk.
* Even if you win a lawsuit, legal fees can be substantial. Without umbrella insurance, these costs come out of pocket.
* A large judgment against you could jeopardize your financial future, including your retirement plans, education funds, or lifestyle.
---|---
* Provides an additional layer of protection beyond standard policies.
* Generally low-cost; it’s often just a few hundred dollars a year for substantial coverage.
* Safeguards your home, savings, and future earnings against lawsuits that exceed standard policy limits.
* Offers protection for a wider range of claims.
* It is an extra expense, which could be a consideration for those on tight budgets.
* Policy limitations could include injuries to you and your household, liability you assume under a contract, and business-related losses.
* May not be needed if you have minimal assets or low risk of being sued.
