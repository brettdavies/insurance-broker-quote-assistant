### Does Texas Allow Non-Owner Car Insurance?
Texas permits non-owner car insurance for drivers without a car but needing coverage. Affordable options are available to pair with an SR-22 if required.
![carInsurance icon](https://res.cloudinary.com/moneygeek/image/upload/v1585171420/assets/components/icon/car_insurance_aud02m.svg)
TEXAS MINIMUM INSURANCE REQUIREMENTS
[Texas' minimum coverage requirements](https://www.moneygeek.com/insurance/auto/state-minimum-car-insurance-requirements/) apply to non-owner car insurance. The limits are:
  * $30,000 in bodily injury liability per person
  * $60,000 in bodily injury liability per accident
  * $25,000 in property damage liability per accident


These minimums may not fully protect you. Consider [how much coverage you need](https://www.moneygeek.com/insurance/auto/how-much-car-insurance-do-i-need/) before purchasing a policy to ensure it aligns with your circumstances.
### Should You Get Non-Owner Car Insurance in Texas?
Non-owner car insurance is a practical option for those who drive but don’t own a car. It provides coverage for renting or borrowing vehicles, meets state minimum requirements and helps maintain continuous insurance history, potentially reducing future costs.
![insurance2 icon](https://res.cloudinary.com/moneygeek/image/upload/v1585171420/assets/components/icon/insurance_2_iixd7m.svg)
AVOID ONE-DAY CAR INSURANCE
Be cautious of "one-day insurance" offers in Texas, as these could be fraudulent. For [temporary coverage](https://www.moneygeek.com/insurance/auto/temporary-car-insurance-texas/), such as during a move or road trip, it's safer to purchase insurance through a car rental company.

WHAT IS SR-22 INSURANCE?
An [SR-22](https://www.moneygeek.com/insurance/auto/sr22-car-insurance-texas/), or Certificate of Financial Responsibility, is a document your insurer files with the DMV to prove you have minimum auto insurance coverage. It's often required for drivers with issues like a DUI or driving without insurance to regain driving privileges.
