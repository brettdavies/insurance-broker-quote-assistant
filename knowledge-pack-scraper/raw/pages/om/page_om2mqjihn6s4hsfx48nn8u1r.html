The Ultimate Guide to Auto Insurance in the USA

When you drive in the USA, auto insurance isn't just a safeguard - it's a legal obligation set by each state. You must carry minimum liability coverage to comply with regulations and protect yourself from hefty costs tied to accidents or theft. Read Guide

  • Auto insurance is legally required in almost every state to protect drivers from financial losses after accidents or damages.
  • Each state sets minimum coverage standards, so drivers must ensure their policy meets local legal requirements.
  • Insurance policies offer various protections, including liability, property damage, medical costs, and uninsured motorist coverage.
  • Driving without insurance results in severe penalties, such as fines, license suspension, and higher future premiums.
  • Customizable coverage options allow drivers to enhance financial security and safeguard assets beyond state minimums.

SR22 Insurance Explained

SR22 insurance is not a policy but a state-mandated certificate proving a high-risk driver carries at least the minimum required auto liability coverage. It is often required after DUI convictions, repeated violations, or driving without insurance, and must be filed by an insurer directly with the DMV.
