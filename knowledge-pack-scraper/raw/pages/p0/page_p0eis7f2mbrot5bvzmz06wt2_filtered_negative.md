#  What Is PIP Coverage?
In fact, in New York, you can _only_ file a claim under an at-fault driver’s policy if you suffer a “serious” injury in a crash. If none of your injuries qualify as “serious,” you will be limited to filing a claim under your own personal injury protection (PIP) policy.
[PIP coverage allows you to recover financial compensation for injuries you sustain in a car accident on a no-fault basis](https://www.dreyerboyajian.com/blog/new-york-no-fault-accident-claims/). What does this mean, exactly?
[If you suffer a serious injury as defined by law in a motor vehicle accident](https://www.dreyerboyajian.com/personal-injury/car-accident-lawyers/), you can file a fault-based claim under the other driver’s bodily injury liability insurance policy – **provided the other driver was at fault**. In addition to requiring PIP coverage, New York law also requires drivers to carry bodily injury coverage with minimum policy limits of $25,000 per person and $50,000 per accident for non-fatal injuries.
