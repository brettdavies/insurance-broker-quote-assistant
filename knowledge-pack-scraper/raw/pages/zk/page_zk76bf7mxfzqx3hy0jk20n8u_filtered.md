## Is RV insurance required in Washington state?

If you drive a motorhome, [RV liability coverage](https://www.progressive.com/answers/rv-liability-coverage/) is required by Washington law and can pay for damage or injuries you cause on the road. If you need more coverage than the state minimum, you can increase your liability limits to protect your assets.

| Coverage | What it covers | Amount |
| --- | --- | --- |
| [Bodily injury liability (BI)](https://www.progressive.com/answers/bodily-injury-liability/ ) | What it coversInjuries you cause to others while driving your motorhome | Amount$25,000 per person $50,000 per accident |
| [Property damage liability (PD)](https://www.progressive.com/answers/property-damage-liability/) | What it coversDamage you cause to another vehicle or property while driving your motorhome | Amount$10,000 per accident |

The state of Washington also mandates [uninsured/underinsured motorist bodily injury coverage](https://www.progressive.com/answers/uninsured-motorist-insurance/), underinsured motorist property damage coverage, and [personal injury protection (PIP) coverage](https://www.progressive.com/answers/personal-injury-protection/), but you may reject these coverages with the submission of a signed form.

## Is insurance required on a travel trailer in Washington state?

Washington law requires that you carry [auto liability coverage](https://www.progressive.com/answers/auto-liability-insurance/) for the vehicle pulling your travel trailer. It's important to note that your auto liability coverage won't cover damage to your travel trailer. If you want to protect your camper against damage, there are coverage options available. Learn more about [travel trailer insurance](https://www.progressive.com/rv-insurance/travel-trailer-insurance/).

### Comprehensive and collision coverage

[RV comprehensive and collision coverages](https://www.progressive.com/answers/rv-collision-comprehensive-coverage/) are optional if you own your RV outright. But we understand the value that many motorhome or travel trailer owners place on their home away from home. It may be worthwhile to add comprehensive and collision coverage if you want to protect your RV against theft, damage and loss. If you're financing or leasing your RV, your lender likely requires comprehensive and collision coverages.

Comprehensive covers events out of your control, including [RV theft](https://www.progressive.com/answers/rv-theft-insurance-prevention-tips/), vandalism, fire, hitting an animal, and weather-related issues. Collision covers your RV if you collide with another vehicle, tree, fence, guardrail, or any object.

## What if I live in my RV full time?

If your RV is your permanent residence, you may need additional coverage that a recreational RV policy doesn't offer. However, a [full-time RV insurance policy](https://www.progressive.com/answers/full-time-rv-insurance/) may offer protection when your RV is your primary home with the following coverages:

* **Personal liability**: Liability coverage on a full-timers policy can protect against liability claims if you're responsible for someone else's injuries or damages, even when your RV is parked
* **Medical payments**: If someone hurts themselves in or around your RV, medical payments coverage may pay for their medical bills
* **Loss assessment**: This coverage can pay for fees or charges from your RV association, up to $5000

## Fast facts for Washington RVers

1. You don't need a commercial driver's license (CDL) if you're driving an RV for non-commercial purposes, according to [Washington state law](https://dol.wa.gov/driver-licenses-and-permits/commercial-driver-licenses-cdl/do-i-need-cdl).
2. [Washington law](https://dol.wa.gov/vehicles-and-boats/vehicle-registration/register-other-vehicles-and-other-services/registering-campers) no longer recognizes a permanently attached camper and pickup as a motorhome. The camper and the truck must have separate, individual titles and registrations.
3. It is [legal to live full-time in your RV in the state of Washington](https://www.rvdoctor.org/blog/what-states-allow-you-to-live-in-an-rv/), but laws vary by city. Check local regulations before settling.
