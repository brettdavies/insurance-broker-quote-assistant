## What are South Carolina's minimum auto insurance requirements?

Drivers in South Carolina are required to have [auto liability coverage](https://www.progressive.com/answers/auto-liability-insurance/) ([BI liability](https://www.progressive.com/answers/bodily-injury-liability/) and [PD liability](https://www.progressive.com/answers/property-damage-liability/)), and [uninsured/underinsured motorist (UM/UIM)](https://www.progressive.com/answers/uninsured-motorist-insurance/) coverage at the following minimum car insurance limits:

### Coverage

| Coverage | What it covers | Minimum amount |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Bodily injury liability | What it covers Injuries you cause to someone else in an accident. | Minimum amount $25,000 per person  $50,000 per accident |
| Property damage liability (PD) | What it covers Damage you cause to someone else's property while driving, such as a vehicle. | Minimum amount $25,000 per accident |
| UM/UIM bodily injury | What it covers Your medical expenses an uninsured or underinsured driver causes | Minimum amount $25,000 per person  $50,000 per accident |
| UM/UIM property damage | What it covers Damage to your vehicle and other property caused by an uninsured driver. | Minimum amount $25,000 per accident (with a $200 deductible) |

While these are South Carolina's minimum required car [insurance limits](https://www.progressive.com/answers/insurance-limits/), you can choose higher coverage amounts to better protect your family's finances. Learn [how much car insurance you may need](https://www.progressive.com/answers/how-much-car-insurance-do-i-need/) based on your circumstances and preferences.

### Is South Carolina a no-fault state?

No. South Carolina is an at-fault state, which means the party responsible for an accident must cover the injuries of the other driver and their passengers. Find out [what happens if you're at fault in an accident](https://www.progressive.com/answers/at-fault-vs-no-fault-accidents/).
