The [**average cost of homeowners insurance**](https://insurify.com/homeowners-insurance/knowledge/average-cost-of-homeowners-insurance/) in New York City is $3,080 per year for a policy with $300,000 in dwelling coverage and a $500 deductible. The average cost with a $1,000 deductible is $2,992. Your exact premium will depend on your location within the city, how much home insurance coverage you need, the insurance company you choose, and other factors. The higher coverage limits you choose, the more expensive your rate will be.
Other factors that can affect your rate include the size of your house, its age, local building costs, your deductible, and even your credit history.[[2]](https://insurify.com/homeowners-insurance/new-york/nyc/#cite-2) No matter what your unique characteristics are, it’s essential to compare home insurance quotes from multiple insurers before buying a policy.
The factor that’ll most significantly affect how much you pay for home insurance is your [**dwelling coverage amount**](https://insurify.com/homeowners-insurance/knowledge/how-much-dwelling-insurance-do-i-need/). It’s the most crucial piece of your home insurance policy, covering damage to the structure of your home in the event of a covered peril, such as wind damage. The higher your dwelling coverage limit, the higher your premium will be.

Your [**home insurance deductible**](https://insurify.com/homeowners-insurance/knowledge/home-insurance-deductible/) is how much you pay out of pocket after a covered incident before your homeowners coverage kicks in. Home insurance deductibles typically sit between $500 and $2,000. Your deductible also affects your premium. The lower your deductible is, the higher your premium will be.
