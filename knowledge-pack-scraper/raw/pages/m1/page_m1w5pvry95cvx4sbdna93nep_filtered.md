Car insurance is affordable in Washington. The average car insurance rate in [Washington](/auto-insurance/washington-car-insurance/) is $1,160 per year — 18.7% less than the [national average](/auto-insurance/how-to-shop/average-auto-insurance/).

But auto insurance premiums are affected by more than just the state in which you live. Auto insurance rates consider a number of details, including one's age, gender, marital status, credit, and driving history.

## Average Auto Insurance Prices in Washington by Age

It's a common phenomenon: as you [age](/auto-insurance/driver/age/), your auto insurance prices shift. Generally, car insurance is more expensive for young drivers, as car insurers see inexperienced motorists as riskier.

In Washington, auto insurance costs $6,301 each year for a [16-year-old](/auto-insurance/driver/age/car-insurance-16-year-olds/) driver, but just $999 per year for a driver in their [50s](/auto-insurance/discounts/senior-citizen-discount/).

###### WASHINGTON CAR INSURANCE RATES BY AGE BRACKET

| Age | Average Yearly Rate |
| --- | --- |
| 16 | $6,301.42 |
| 17 | $4,893.49 |
| 18 | $4,157.56 |
| 19 | $2,716.21 |
| 20s | $1,511.22 |
| 30s | $1,112.68 |
| 40s | $1,057.87 |
| 50s | $998.70 |
| 60s | $1,024.50 |
| 70s | $1,283.54 |

If you're shopping for car insurance for a teen driver, Washington is the 27th cheapest state in which to buy auto insurance for a 16-year-old.

## Average Car Insurance Rates in Washington by Marital Status

One benefit of [marriage](/auto-insurance/driver/status/) — in addition to the wedding gifts — is the lower prices you tend to receive on auto insurance. In Washington, wedded drivers save $49 per year on auto insurance. This is a smaller discount than the national average of $76.

## Average Car Insurance Rates in Washington by Credit

Car insurers reference credit history as a prediction of a potential customer's insurability. Historical data indicate motorists who carry exceptional credit are more dependable auto insurance customers: they less frequently file claims covered by insurance companies.

In Washington, a driver who maintains exceptional credit saves $1,195.44 per year on auto insurance rates, contrasted with a driver with a less-favorable credit history. Learn more about how credit score affects car insurance premiums:

## Average Car Insurance Costs for Bad Drivers in Washington

If you've been found at fault in a car accident or convicted of an [on-road offense](/auto-insurance/driver/other-factors/cheap-car-insurance-high-risk-drivers/), your auto insurance costs are going to climb. The more violations you amass, the more expensive your auto insurance gets.

In Washington, your first minor at-fault collision results in a penalty of $326.07 per year in auto insurance premiums. A DUI — thought of as one of the most severe driving violations — will raise your annual car insurance costs in Washington by an average of $520.40.

## Median Auto Insurance Prices by Coverage Amount in Washington

How much you pay each month for auto insurance is dependent on the [tier of coverage](/auto-insurance/coverage/) you elect to carry. Liability-only coverage generally costs less, while lower-deductible comprehensive coverage costs more. In Washington, the cost gap between liability-only coverage and full coverage with a $500 deductible is $507.

| $100K/$300K/$100K Bodily Injury/Property Damage — $500 Comprehensive/Collision | $1,262 |
| $50K/$100K/$50K Bodily Injury/Property Damage — Liability Only | $677 |
| $50K/$100K/$50K Bodily Injury/Property Damage — $1,000 Comprehensive/Collision | $1,086 |
| $50K/$100K/$50K Bodily Injury/Property Damage — $500 Comprehensive/Collision | $1,183 |
| State Minimum — Liability Only | $607 |
| State Minimum — $1,000 Comprehensive/Collision | $1,018 |
| State Minimum — $500 Comprehensive/Collision | $1,114 |
