##  Why Home Insurance Often Covers Winter Storm Claims
##  When Is Winter Storm Damage Usually Covered?
* **Unpreventable damage from fallen trees or branches:** Besides covering direct damage caused by wind, snow, ice, or any other covered peril, homeowners insurance usually covers _indirect_ damage if caused by a covered peril. In other words, if a tree on your property topples over onto your home’s siding or frozen tree branches fall and damage your roof due to a blizzard or ice storm, your insurer may cover the cost of repairs since the underlying cause of the loss—also known as the **proximate cause** —is covered under your policy.
