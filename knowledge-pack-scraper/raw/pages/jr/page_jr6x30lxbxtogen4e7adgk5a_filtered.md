## What are Nebraska's minimum auto insurance requirements?

Drivers in Nebraska are required to have [auto liability coverage](https://www.progressive.com/answers/auto-liability-insurance/) ([BI liability](https://www.progressive.com/answers/bodily-injury-liability/) and [PD liability](https://www.progressive.com/answers/property-damage-liability/)) and [uninsured/underinsured motorist (UM/UIM) coverage](https://www.progressive.com/answers/uninsured-motorist-insurance/) at the following minimum limits:

### Coverage

| Coverage | What it covers | Minimum amount |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Bodily injury liability | What it covers Injuries you cause to others in an accident | Minimum amount $25,000 per person $50,000 per accident |
| Property damage liability | What it covers Damage you cause to other people's property in an accident | Minimum amount $25,000 per accident |
| UM/UIM bodily injury | What it covers Your medical expenses an uninsured or underinsured driver causes | Minimum amount $25,000 per person $50,000 per accident |

While these are Nebraska's minimum required car [insurance limits](https://www.progressive.com/answers/insurance-limits/), higher coverage amounts may better protect your family's financial wellbeing. Learn [how much car insurance you may need](https://www.progressive.com/answers/how-much-car-insurance-do-i-need/) based on your circumstances and preferences.

### Is Nebraska a no-fault state?

No. Nebraska is an at-fault state, which means the party responsible for an accident must cover the injuries of the other driver and their passengers. Learn more about [what happens if you're at fault in an accident](https://www.progressive.com/answers/at-fault-vs-no-fault-accidents/).

## Additional car insurance coverages to consider

If you're leasing or financing a vehicle, most lenders require you to carry [comprehensive auto coverage](https://www.progressive.com/answers/comprehensive-car-insurance/) and [collision coverage](https://www.progressive.com/answers/auto-collision-coverage/) in addition to Nebraska's state-required coverages. Comprehensive and collision pay to repair or replace your car regardless of fault, if it's in an accident, gets stolen, or is otherwise damaged due to a covered incident. It also makes sense to add these physical damage coverages if you're not sure you can afford a new car or major repairs after an accident.
