![How Long Do You Have to File a Property Damage Claim in Texas?](https://kirklawfirm.com/wp-content/uploads/2024/11/broken-glass-286098_1280.jpg)
The first step after an accident or weather event should always be to contact an attorney who can help identify any deadlines for filing your claim. The reporting deadline can be found within your insurance policy, but the law might provide a different deadline. It is crucial to notify your insurance carrier as soon as you become aware of the damage, because failure to do so could lead to denial of your claim. These deadlines are often fairly short, so it is important to act quickly.
Another important deadline is the statute of limitations, which dictates when you can file suit. In Texas, the statute of limitations generally gives you two years to file a lawsuit against your insurance company for [denying](https://kirklawfirm.com/blog/insurance-claim-denied-heres-what-texas-law-says-about-your-rights/) or underpaying your property damage claim, and that clock generally begins on the day you receive their claim determination. In regard to an [auto accident](https://kirklawfirm.com/houston-car-accident-lawyer/), you generally have two years from the date of the accident to sue the person that caused the wreck. However, these deadlines may vary, so it is important to consult an attorney.
If you miss the two-year statute of limitations, your right to seek compensation from your insurance company or the person who damaged your property may be lost forever. Similarly, if you fail to notify your insurance carrier of damage within the time limits specified in your policy, you may also lose access to the coverage that you bargained for.
