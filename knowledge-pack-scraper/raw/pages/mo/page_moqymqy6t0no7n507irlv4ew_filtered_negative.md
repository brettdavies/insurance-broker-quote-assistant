If you’re a driver in the Lone Star State, it’s important to understand the nuances of [**Texas car insurance**](https://insurify.com/car-insurance/texas/) laws. Read on to learn about the state’s mandatory bodily injury and property damage liability coverage so you can hit the road with confidence.
To drive legally in Texas, you must carry these minimum coverage amounts:[[1]](https://insurify.com/car-insurance/texas/minimum-requirements/#cite-1)
**Coverage**![sort asc](https://insurifycdn.com/ui/icons/ExpandArrowOutline.svg)![sort desc](https://insurifycdn.com/ui/icons/ExpandArrowOutline.svg) |  **Minimum Requirements**![sort asc](https://insurifycdn.com/ui/icons/ExpandArrowOutline.svg)![sort desc](https://insurifycdn.com/ui/icons/ExpandArrowOutline.svg)

Texas also requires insurance companies to offer [**uninsured/underinsured motorist (UIM) coverage**](https://insurify.com/car-insurance/texas/uninsured-motorist-coverage-texas/) and $2,500 in [**personal injury protection (PIP)**](https://insurify.com/car-insurance/texas/personal-injury-protection-texas/) to policyholders who purchase liability insurance. But you can opt out of both coverages by notifying your car insurance company in writing that you don’t want UIM or PIP.
Your insurance policy’s [**bodily injury liability**](https://insurify.com/car-insurance/coverage/bodily-injury-liability/) pays for medical bills, loss of income due to injury, emotional and physical pain and suffering, and funeral expenses after an accident. If you’re responsible for a car accident, this type of coverage pays for injury-related costs for the driver and passengers in the other vehicle.
Damage you cause to other property, like a fence, utility pole, or guardrail, also falls under property damage liability. It’s important to note this coverage type doesn’t apply to damage to your vehicle, which [**collision and comprehensive insurance**](https://insurify.com/car-insurance/coverage/full-coverage/) typically covers.
[**High-risk drivers**](https://insurify.com/car-insurance/driver/driving-record/high-risk/), meaning people with severe violations such as DWIs and reckless driving, coverage lapses, and multiple at-fault [**accidents**](https://insurify.com/car-insurance/driver/driving-record/after-accident/), often have difficulty securing coverage because insurance companies view them as too risky to insure. The Texas Automobile Insurance Plan Association (TAIPA) is a state-run system that provides insurance for drivers who can’t find coverage through standard insurers.
TAIPA assigns high-risk drivers to insurance companies participating in the association, but its premiums are typically more expensive than standard policies. TAIPA also provides only the state minimum 30/60/25 liability coverage. Drivers are eligible if two traditional insurance companies have rejected them within 60 days.[[2]](https://insurify.com/car-insurance/texas/minimum-requirements/#cite-2)
Texas is an [**at-fault state**](https://insurify.com/car-insurance/texas/is-texas-a-no-fault-state/), meaning if an injured party’s damages exceed your liability limits, they may be able to sue you for the difference.
A [**full-coverage car insurance policy**](https://insurify.com/car-insurance/texas/full-coverage/) also increases your financial protection. Full-coverage policies generally include collision and comprehensive coverages, which pay for repair and replacement costs for your vehicle.
