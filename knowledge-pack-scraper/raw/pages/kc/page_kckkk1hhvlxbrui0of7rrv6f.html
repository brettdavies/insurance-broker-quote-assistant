With that out of the way, letâs look at the coverage you need. Like any other home, a manufactured home insurance policy provides coverage for the dwelling itself, for personal property, and for liability. Coverage also includes add-ons like attached porches and other structures such as detached garages, as well as a manufactured home on permanent foundation insurance.

Some older mobile or manufactured homes may not qualify for full replacement cost coverage, but most modern homes should be able to get it. That means that if your manufactured home is destroyed, you will be paid out the amount of money that is required to replace it with a similar home, rather than the depreciated cost.

Choose homeowners insurance for your manufactured home that fully covers weather risks, structural damage, and liability, while ensuring replacement cost coverage .

Specifics of Manufactured Home Insurance

The biggest difference between a manufactured home and a site-built home is that it is mobile. That means you need to be able to cover the home when itâs being transported to the location where you plan to keep it.

Manufactured home insurance includes this coverage, usually as an endorsement. If your home is in a permanent location and you have no plans to move it, you do not need this coverage. Bear in mind that even as the home is being transported from the factory to the lot where it will be placed, you need to insure it.

There are some special discounts offered for manufactured homes, depending on the company, such as a discount for being the original owner of the home. Multi-policy discounts are usually available for bundling auto insurance, similar to any other home insurance policy, as noted in homeowners insurance for manufactured home reviews.
