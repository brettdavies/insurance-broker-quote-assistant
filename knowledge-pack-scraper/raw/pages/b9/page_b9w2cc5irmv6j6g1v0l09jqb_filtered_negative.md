You can get a quote and [buy car insurance online](https://www.valuepenguin.com/auto-insurance/buy-car-insurance-online "How to Get Insurance Online") immediately from many of the [largest and most affordable insurance companies](https://www.valuepenguin.com/largest-auto-insurance-companies "Largest Auto Insurance Companies"). And you can often buy coverage on your phone, so you don't even need a computer to do it.
The only downsides to same-day insurance are that you won't have much time to shop around and you might miss out on a small [buy-in-advance discount](https://www.valuepenguin.com/car-insurance-discounts#early "Car Insurance Discounts").

The insurance company will use the info you provide, plus info from third parties, like your credit score, to [set your rates](https://www.valuepenguin.com/auto-insurance "Compare Car Insurance Quotes Online") and provide you with a car insurance quote.
But in some circumstances, an insurance company won't be able to provide you with insurance coverage right away. This might be because the company is missing key info about you that it needs to set [your rates](https://www.valuepenguin.com/average-cost-of-insurance "Average Cost of Car Insurance").
There could also be something about your current situation that means the insurance company needs more time to set your rate or approve your coverage. You may be looking for coverage for a rare or high-value car that isn't in the insurance company's price database. Or a [poor driving history](https://www.valuepenguin.com/best-cheap-car-insurance-bad-driving-record "Best Cheap Car Insurance for Those With a Bad Driving Record") may mean an insurance company doesn't offer you a quote at all.
