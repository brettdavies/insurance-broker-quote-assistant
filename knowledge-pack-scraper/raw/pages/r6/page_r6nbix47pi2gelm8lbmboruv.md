## What Is Property Damage Liability (PDL)?
Property Damage Liability (PDL) is a type of automobile insurance coverage specifically designed to pay for repairs or replacement when you are responsible for causing damage to someone else‚Äôs property. Generally, this includes damage to another vehicle, but it can also cover a range of other properties, from fences to storefronts.
However, what property damage liability **does NOT** cover is damage to your own car. If you‚Äôre at fault in an accident, you‚Äôll need your own collision coverage to repair your vehicle.
PDL is not optional for drivers in Texas. It‚Äôs a required part of the minimum state liability insurance, meaning you must have it to legally drive.
## Understanding the Legal Framework for PDL in Texas
Every state sets its own rules regarding auto insurance requirements, and Texas is no exception. Here‚Äôs how PDL fits into the Lone Star State‚Äôs regulations.
### What Does Texas Law Say About PDL?
Texas mandates that all drivers meet its **30/60/25 liability coverage rule**. The ‚Äú25‚Äù in this equation refers to property damage liability. Specifically, Texas drivers must carry at least **$25,000 in PDL coverage**. This ensures you can compensate others for property damage caused in an accident that you‚Äôre deemed responsible for.
Failing to meet this requirement can lead to hefty penalties, including fines, license suspension, and having your vehicle impounded. Additionally, being caught without insurance can make you personally liable for all damages caused in an accident, leaving you at financial risk.

Texas law enforcement can verify your insurance status during traffic stops, vehicle registration, and after an accident. Carrying valid proof of insurance in your vehicle at all times is essential. ### Who is Covered Under PDL Policies?
Property Damage Liability doesn‚Äôt just cover you, the policyholder; it may extend to others in certain situations. Depending on your insurance plan, PDL might also cover designated drivers operating your car, provided they have your permission.

**What‚Äôs Covered by PDL?**

  * **Vehicle Repairs:** Covers the cost to fix the other driver‚Äôs car.
  * **Property Repairs:** Pays for damages to non-vehicular property, such as fences, buildings, mailboxes, or street signs.
  * **Replacement Costs:** Should the damage render the other party‚Äôs property beyond repair, PDL can help cover its replacement.
