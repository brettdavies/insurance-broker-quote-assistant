Each filing status has its own set of[ tax brackets](https://blog.turbotax.intuit.com/tax-planning-2/what-is-a-tax-bracket-24007/), so refer to the charts below to see how your specific status impacts your rates.
**Tax rate** | **Single or Married Filing Separately** |  **Married Filing Jointly** **or Qualifying Surviving Spouse** | **Head of Household**
---|---|---|---
1%  | Up to $10,756  | Up to $21,512  | Up to $21,527
2%  | $10,757 to $25,499  | $21,513 to $50,998  | $21,528 to $51,000
4%  | $25,500 to $40,245  | $50,999 to $80,490  | $51,001 to $65,744
6%  | $40,246 to $55,866  | $80,491 to $111,732  | $65,745 to $81,364
8%  | $55,867 to $70,606  | $111,733 to $141,212  | $81,365 to $96,107
9.3%  | $70,607 to $360,659  | $141,213 to $721,318  | $96,108 to $490,493
10.3%  | $360,660 to $432,787  | $721,319 to $865,574  | $490,494 to $588,593
11.3%  | $432,788 to $721,314  | $865,575 to $1,442,628  | $588,594 to $980,987
12.3%  | $721,315 and over  | $1,442,629 and over  | $980,988 and over
**_Source:_**[_California Franchise Tax Board (CFTB)_](https://www.ftb.ca.gov/forms/2024/2024-540-tax-rate-schedules.pdf)
**Note:** If your taxable income is $100,000 or less, use the tax table listed on the[ CFTB website](https://www.ftb.ca.gov/file/personal/tax-calculator-tables-rates.asp) to calculate your taxes owed.
**Residency status** |  **Definition** |  **How California taxes income**
---|---|---
Resident  | You live in California permanently, or you’re based in California but are temporarily living outside the state.  | You’re taxed on all income, no matter where it’s earned.
Part-year resident  | You lived in California for part of the year and lived elsewhere for the rest.  | You’re taxed on income earned while you lived in California and any income from California sources while you were away.
Nonresident  | You don’t live in California but earned money from California sources (such as a job performed within the state, property, or business located within the state).  | You’re taxed on income from California sources.
**Tax credit** |  **Description** |  **Amount**
---|---|---
[**California Earned Income Tax Credit (CalEITC)** ](https://www.ftb.ca.gov/file/personal/credits/california-earned-income-tax-credit.html) | Helps low-income individuals and families with income under $31,950.  | Up to $3,644
[**Child Adoption Costs Credit** ](https://www.ftb.ca.gov/file/personal/credits/child-adoption-costs-credit.html) | Covers 50% of the costs of adopting a qualified child.  | Up to $2,500 per child in a tax year
[**Child and Dependent Care Expenses Credit**](https://www.ftb.ca.gov/file/personal/credits/child-and-dependent-care-expenses-credit.html) | Covers costs if you paid someone to care for a child or dependent.  | Up to $3,000 for one person, $6,000 for two or more
[**College Access Tax Credit (CATC)** ](https://www.ftb.ca.gov/file/personal/credits/college-access-tax-credit.html) | Encourages donations to the College Access Tax Credit Fund.  | Up to 50% of your contribution
[**Dependent Parent Credit** ](https://www.ftb.ca.gov/file/personal/credits/dependent-parent-credit.html) | For taxpayers supporting a dependent parent. Your parent doesn’t have to live with you.  | Up to $592
[**Foster Youth Tax Credit (FYTC)**](https://www.ftb.ca.gov/file/personal/credits/foster-youth-tax-credit.html) | Offers financial support for current or former foster youth.  | Up to $1,154 per eligible individual
[**Joint Custody Head of Household** ](https://www.ftb.ca.gov/file/personal/credits/joint-custody-hoh-credit.html) | For taxpayers sharing custody of a child but not living together.  | Up to $592
[**Nonrefundable Renter’s Credit** ](https://www.ftb.ca.gov/file/personal/credits/nonrefundable-renters-credit.html) | Available for renters with incomes below a certain threshold who paid rent for at least 6 months during the year.  | Up to $60 (single) or $120 (married)
[**Other State Tax Credit** ](https://www.ftb.ca.gov/file/personal/credits/other-state-tax-credit.html) | Provides relief for taxes paid to another state so you aren’t paying taxes twice.  | Varies based on other state taxes
[**Prior Year Alternative Minimum Tax (AMT)** ](https://www.ftb.ca.gov/file/personal/credits/prior-year-alternative-minimum-tax.html) | Reduces tax owed if you paid AMT in prior years.  | Varies based on AMT paid
[**Senior Head of Household Credit** ](https://www.ftb.ca.gov/file/personal/credits/senior-hoh-credit.html) | For people 65 and over acting as head of household with dependent income.  | Up to $1,806
[**Young Child Tax Credit (YCTC)** ](https://www.ftb.ca.gov/file/personal/credits/young-child-tax-credit.html) | Additional support for families with young children.  | Up to $1,154 per return
_For the complete list of California’s tax credits and deductions, visit the_[](https://tax.colorado.gov/income-tax-credits)[ _California Franchise Tax Board website_](https://www.ftb.ca.gov/file/personal/index.html) _._
