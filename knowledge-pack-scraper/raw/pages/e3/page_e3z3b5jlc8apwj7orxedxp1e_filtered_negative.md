* As of January 1, 2025, California requires minimum auto liability limits of $30,000 per person for injury or death, $60,000 per accident for all people, and $15,000 for property damage.
* Policies that renew on or after January 1, 2025 must meet the new minimums to avoid fines, license issues, or being underinsured in liability claims.
*     *       *         * **$15,000** for injuries or the death of one person in an accident.
*     *       *         * **$30,000** for injuries or deaths of multiple people in a single accident (shared among those injured).
*     *       *         * **$5,000** for property damage, such as repairs to someone else’s vehicle or other property.
*     *       *         * **$30,000** for one person’s injuries or death.
*     *       *         * **$60,000** total for multiple people injured or killed in one accident.
*     *       *         * **$15,000** for property damage.
1.     1.       1.         1. **Bodily Injury or Death for One Person**: The minimum coverage will increase by $20,000, bringing the total to **$50,000**.
1.         1. **Bodily Injury or Death for All Persons in an Accident**: The minimum coverage will increase by $40,000, bringing the total to **$100,000**.
1.         1. **Property Damage**: The minimum coverage will increase by $10,000, bringing the total to **$25,000**.
*     *       *         * **Optional Coverage:** Drivers can decline UM/UIM coverage in writing.
*     *       *         * **Impact on Coverage Limits:** Your UM/UIM policy limits will not automatically increase to match the new liability requirements. If you wish to align your UM/UIM limits with the updated liability minimums, you must contact your insurer to request an adjustment or purchase new coverage.
