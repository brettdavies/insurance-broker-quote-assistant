## Is RV insurance required in Utah?

If you drive a motorhome, [RV liability coverage](https://www.progressive.com/answers/rv-liability-coverage/) is required by Utah law and can pay for damage or injuries you cause on the road. If you need more coverage than the state minimum, you can increase your liability limits to protect your assets.

| Coverage | What it covers | Amount |
| --- | --- | --- |
| [Bodily injury liability coverage (BI)](https://www.progressive.com/answers/bodily-injury-liability/) | What it coversInjuries you cause to another while driving your motorhome | Amount$25,000 per person   $65,000 per accident |
| [Property damage liability coverage (PD)](https://www.progressive.com/answers/property-damage-liability/) | What it coversDamage you cause to another vehicle or property while driving your motorhome | Amount$15,000 per accident |
| [Personal injury protection (PIP)](https://www.progressive.com/auto/insurance-coverages/personal-injury-protection/) | What it coversMedical and funeral expenses, survivor compensation, and work loss compensation, regardless of who is at fault |

The state of Utah also mandates [uninsured/underinsured motorist bodily injury coverage](https://www.progressive.com/answers/uninsured-motorist-insurance/), but you may reject or lower this coverage with the submission of a signed form.

## Do you have to insure a camper in Utah?

Utah requires that you carry auto liability coverage for the vehicle that pulls your travel trailer. Your [auto liability coverage](https://www.progressive.com/answers/auto-liability-insurance/), however, won't cover damage to your travel trailer, also referred to as a camper. There are coverage options available for those who want further protection. Learn more about [travel trailer insurance](https://www.progressive.com/rv-insurance/travel-trailer-insurance/).

### Comprehensive and collision coverage

If you're financing or leasing your motorhome or travel trailer, it's likely that your lender requires [RV comprehensive and collision coverage](https://www.progressive.com/answers/rv-collision-comprehensive-coverage/). Even if you own your RV outright, comprehensive and collision coverages could be a worthwhile option. These coverages protect against damage to your RV and may cover up to the actual cash value of your RV if your motorhome or trailer is damaged, totaled, or stolen.

Comprehensive covers events out of your control, including [RV theft](https://www.progressive.com/answers/does-rv-insurance-cover-theft/), vandalism, fire, hitting an animal, and weather-related issues. Collision covers your RV if you collide with another vehicle, tree, fence, guardrail, or any object.

## What if I live in my RV full time?

If your RV is your permanent residence, you may need additional coverage that a recreational RV policy doesn't offer. However, a [full-time RV insurance policy](https://www.progressive.com/answers/full-time-rv-insurance/) may offer protection when your RV is your primary home with the following coverages:

* **Personal liability**: Liability coverage on a full-timers policy can protect against liability claims if you're responsible for someone else's injuries or damages, even when your RV is parked
* **Medical payments**: If someone hurts themselves in or around your RV, medical payments coverage may pay for their medical bills
* **Loss assessment**: This coverage can pay for fees or charges from your RV association, up to $5,000

## Fast facts for Utah RVers
