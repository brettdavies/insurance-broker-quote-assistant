## Average Cost of Home Insurance in Illinois by Coverage Amount
In Illinois, the average annual homeowners insurance premium for policies with $250K in dwelling coverage, $125K in personal property coverage and $200K liability coverage is $2,119, which is less than the [national average](https://www.moneygeek.com/insurance/homeowners/average-cost-of-home-insurance/) of $2,423.
When determining [how much coverage you need](https://www.moneygeek.com/insurance/homeowners/how-much-home-insurance-do-you-need/), make sure your policy covers the full cost to rebuild your home and replace your belongings after a total loss. A policy with $100K in dwelling coverage costs about $1,363 per year on average. If you need $500K in dwelling coverage for a larger home, expect to pay around $3,275 annually.

## FAQ: Homeowners Insurance in Illinois
### On average, what is the cheapest insurer in Illinois?
Auto-Owners offers the lowest average premium in Illinois at about $1,219 per year. You can find more affordable options among [cheap national providers](https://www.moneygeek.com/www.moneygeek.com/insurance/homeowners/cheap-homeowners-insurance/).
### Is tornado coverage included in Illinois homeowners insurance?
Yes. Tornadoes are considered windstorm damage, which is typically covered by Illinois homeowners insurance. If you’re also concerned about [other natural disasters](https://www.moneygeek.com/insurance/homeowners/does-homeowners-insurance-cover-natural-disasters/#when-natural-disasters-are-covered), ask your insurer about additional coverage options or endorsements.
### What is the biggest factor that impacts the price of homeowners insurance in Illinois?
Your credit rating plays the largest role in determining homeowners insurance costs in Illinois. Premiums can range from about $1,213 for excellent credit to as high as $9,452 for poor credit.
### How much can I save bundling my home and auto insurance in Illinois?
[Bundling in Illinois](https://www.moneygeek.com/insurance/auto/best-home-auto-bundle-in-illinois/) can help homeowners save an average of $240 to $627 per year on combined premiums.
### Is flood coverage included in Illinois homeowners insurance?
No. Standard home insurance policies don’t include flood protection. You’ll need a separate policy through the National Flood Insurance Program, a private insurer, or as an endorsement from certain home insurance companies.
