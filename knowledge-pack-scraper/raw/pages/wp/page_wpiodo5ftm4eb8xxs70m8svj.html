Also known as no-fault insurance, PIP coverage is mandatory for all drivers in New York. It ensures that if youâre injured in an auto accident, your insurance pays for basic economic losses, regardless of who was at fault.

PIP pays for:

  • Essential services (such as household help, up to $25 per day)
  • Death benefits (up to $2,000 in case of death due to the accident)

New York State law requires all auto insurance policies to include a minimum of $50,000 per person in PIP coverage.

  • The policyholder, who is either you or the insured driver
  • Passengers in your insured motor vehicle
  • Pedestrians injured by your vehicle

This $50,000 coverage is the maximum standard PIP limit in NY. Drivers can purchase Additional PIP (APIP) coverage for higher limits. Unlike PIP, MVAIC is not automatic. You must meet certain criteria and file the correct paperwork within strict deadlines to qualify for benefits.

  • A bicyclist struck by a stolen or unregistered vehicle
  • A passenger in an uninsured vehicle
