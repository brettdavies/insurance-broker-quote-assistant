## What Amica home insurance covers

These types of home insurance coverage generally come standard:

* ### Dwelling

  [Dwelling coverage](https://www.nerdwallet.com/article/insurance/dwelling-coverage-homeowners) pays to repair or rebuild the structure of your home after a covered event. It also covers attached structures, such as porches.

  **Typical amount**: Enough to rebuild your home.
* ### Other structures

  [Other structures coverage](https://www.nerdwallet.com/article/insurance/other-structures-coverage) pays to repair or rebuild stand-alone structures on your property, such as a fence or shed.

  **Typical amount**: 10% of dwelling coverage.
* ### Personal property

  [Personal property coverage](https://www.nerdwallet.com/article/insurance/personal-property-insurance) pays to repair or replace stolen or damaged belongings. Personal property coverage comes in one of two options: [actual cash value or replacement cost](https://www.nerdwallet.com/article/insurance/actual-cash-value-vs-replacement-cost).

  **Typical amount:** 50-70% of dwelling coverage.
* ### Loss of use

  [Loss of use coverage](https://www.nerdwallet.com/article/insurance/loss-of-use-coverage), sometimes known as additional living expenses coverage, helps pay temporary living expenses while your home is being repaired. This can cover hotel stays, home rentals and meals.

  **Typical amount**: 20% of dwelling coverage.
* ### Personal liability

  [Personal liability coverage](https://www.nerdwallet.com/article/insurance/personal-liability-insurance-homeowners) pays if you injure someone or cause property damage unintentionally or through neglect.

  **Typical amount**: $100,000 to $500,000.
* ### Medical payments

  [Medical payments coverage](https://www.nerdwallet.com/article/insurance/medical-payments-coverage-homeowners) pays to treat someone injured on your property, regardless of who‚Äôs at fault. It also pays if you, a family member or a pet injures someone away from your home.

  **Typical amount**: $1,000 to $5,000.

For more details, see [What Does Homeowners Insurance Cover?](https://www.nerdwallet.com/article/insurance/understanding-homeowners-insurance)

### Optional Amica home insurance coverage

Amica has several types of coverage you can add to a standard policy, including:

**Dwelling replacement** that pays up to 30% above your coverage limit to rebuild or repair your home.

**Personal property replacement** to cover the cost of replacing personal items with brand-new versions.

[**Water backup/sump overflow**](https://www.nerdwallet.com/article/insurance/water-backup-coverage) to pay for damage from backed-up drains or failing sump pumps.

**Special computer coverage** that pays for a broader variety of damage to computers and smart devices.

[**Identity fraud expense**](https://www.nerdwallet.com/article/insurance/identity-theft-insurance) for credit monitoring and costs associated with restoring a stolen identity.

**Home business coverage** for equipment and liability concerns that arise when running a business from your house.

Amica offers an upgraded homeowners policy called Platinum Choice. It comes with more coverage for liability, medical payments and valuable items such as jewelry and computers. It also includes some of the add-ons listed above, such as dwelling replacement and water backup coverage.

Did you know...

Most homeowners insurance policies offer limited theft coverage for valuables such as jewelry. If you have an expensive engagement ring or a valuable collection, you may want to consider buying separate [jewelry insurance](https://www.nerdwallet.com/article/insurance/jewelry-insurance).

In some states, Amica also offers dividend policies. These offer the same coverage as traditional policies but require a higher premium. In return, dividend policies can refund money to you at the end of your policy period if the company has performed well. The amount is typically 5% to 20% of the policy's annual premium.

## How to file a claim with Amica

Amica offers several ways to file a claim: over the phone, through its website or using its app. Amica recommends taking pictures of any damage before you start making repairs. You‚Äôll also want to prevent further damage if possible, such as by tarping your roof.

Here are some key things to remember when filing a home insurance claim:

**Document the damage.** Take photos and create an inventory of damaged items. Provide details like labels and model numbers if possible, especially for more expensive items. Once you've documented the damage, you can make temporary repairs if it's safe to do so.

**File your claim as soon as possible.** This is especially important if the damage was caused by a storm, as it likely means other homes in the area were also affected.

**Save your receipts.** You may end up spending money on temporary repairs or living expenses before getting a payment from your insurance company. Keep all receipts so you can submit them for reimbursement later.

For more information, see [how to file a home insurance claim](https://www.nerdwallet.com/article/insurance/home-damage-claims).

### Why is Amica quoting me higher rates than other companies?

Every insurance company has a different formula for calculating premiums. That means the cheapest option for your neighbor might not be the cheapest one for you. Also, insurers often have slightly different estimates for how much it will cost to rebuild your home. For example, a company that thinks it‚Äôll cost $400,000 to rebuild your home may charge a higher premium than one that sets the replacement cost at $360,000.

When comparing [home insurance quotes](https://www.nerdwallet.com/insurance/homeowners/home-insurance-quotes), check how much coverage is included in each policy and which deductibles apply. You may also want to weigh each company‚Äôs track record of consumer complaints. (You can find this information in NerdWallet‚Äôs [home insurance reviews](https://www.nerdwallet.com/article/insurance/homeowners-insurance-reviews).)

### Why does my dwelling coverage from Amica not match what my home is worth?

The dwelling coverage limit on a homeowners policy is the maximum amount your insurance company will pay to rebuild your home if it‚Äôs destroyed. This amount may not match the [value of your home](https://www.nerdwallet.com/article/mortgages/how-to-determine-home-value) as determined by an appraiser for selling purposes. That‚Äôs because the appraised value includes the land your house sits on, which isn‚Äôt a factor in your rebuilding cost.
