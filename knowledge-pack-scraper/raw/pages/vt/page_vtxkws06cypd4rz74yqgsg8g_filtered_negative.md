Umbrella insurance helps protect your assets from unexpected liabilities. It typically costs a few hundred dollars per year for every $1 million in coverage. For example, someone with a home, two cars, and two drivers will typically pay less than $1 per day for coverage for a $1 million policy.[[1]](https://www.compare.com/homeowners-insurance/coverage/umbrella-insurance/average-costs#cite-1)
Umbrella insurance offers additional financial protection on top of your primary insurance, such as [homeowners insurance](https://www.compare.com/homeowners-insurance) or [auto insurance](https://www.compare.com/auto-insurance). Think of it as a backup plan — it’s not your first line of defense. Your umbrella coverage comes into play once you exceed your existing insurance policy limits.[[2]](https://www.compare.com/homeowners-insurance/coverage/umbrella-insurance/average-costs#cite-2)
