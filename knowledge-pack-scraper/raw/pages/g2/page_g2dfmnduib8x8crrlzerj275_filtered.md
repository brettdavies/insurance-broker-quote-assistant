## What Does State Farm Homeowners Insurance Cover?

Every State Farm policy includes the six core types of homeowners insurance coverage that safeguard your property and personal assets. Review the list below to see exactly [what home insurance covers](https://www.moneygeek.com/insurance/homeowners/what-does-homeowners-insurance-cover/) under a typical State Farm policy.

![house icon](https://res.cloudinary.com/moneygeek/image/upload/v1585171420/assets/components/icon/house_f1rmru.svg)

**Dwelling Coverage**

[Dwelling coverage](https://www.moneygeek.com/insurance/homeowners/what-is-dwelling-coverage/) insures your home’s main structure, including attached features like walls, floors and roofs, against damage from covered events. It provides the funds needed to repair or rebuild your home after a loss.

![garage icon](https://res.cloudinary.com/moneygeek/image/upload/v1585171420/assets/components/icon/garage_ttngpd.svg)

**Other Structures Coverage**

[Other structures coverage](https://www.moneygeek.com/insurance/homeowners/does-homeowners-insurance-cover-other-structures/) safeguards detached structures on your property, such as garages, fences and sheds. It pays for repairs or replacement when these are damaged by a covered peril.

![bookshelves icon](https://res.cloudinary.com/moneygeek/image/upload/v1585171420/assets/components/icon/bookshelves_nsuojr.svg)

**Personal Property Coverage**

[Personal property coverage](https://www.moneygeek.com/insurance/homeowners/what-is-personal-property-coverage-how-much-do-you-need/) protects your belongings, from clothes to appliances, against theft or damage. It covers your items both at home and when they’re temporarily outside your property.

![scale icon](https://res.cloudinary.com/moneygeek/image/upload/v1585171420/assets/components/icon/scale_dhas7a.svg)

**Personal Liability Coverage**

[Personal liability coverage](https://www.moneygeek.com/insurance/homeowners/personal-liability-homeowners-insurance/) helps cover costs if you accidentally cause injury or property damage to someone else. It also pays for legal defense and court-related expenses.

![hospital icon](https://res.cloudinary.com/moneygeek/image/upload/v1585171420/assets/components/icon/hospital_l5afnp.svg)

**Medical Payments Coverage**

Medical payments coverage provides limited reimbursement for medical expenses if someone is hurt on your property. It’s designed for small, no-fault claims to help guests recover quickly.

![hotel icon](https://res.cloudinary.com/moneygeek/image/upload/v1585171420/assets/components/icon/hotel_tab8pv.svg)

**Loss of Use Coverage**

[Loss of use coverage](https://www.moneygeek.com/insurance/homeowners/homeowners-insurance-loss-of-use/) pays for living expenses like temporary housing and meals when your home is under repair after a covered loss. It ensures you have a place to stay until your home is safe to return to.

### Optional Home Insurance Coverages from State Farm

State Farm offers five additional coverages, also known as endorsements, that homeowners can add to strengthen their protection. An endorsement is an optional add-on that extends your policy’s limits or provides coverage for risks not included in standard home insurance.

Review the table below to learn about each endorsement and what it covers. Note that availability varies by state, so check with your local agent to see which options apply to you.

| Coverage | Description |
| --- | --- |
| Water Backup | Covers water damage caused by backed-up sewers or drains that isn’t included in standard home insurance. |
| Home Systems Protection | Helps repair or replace essential systems and appliances that break down due to mechanical or electrical failure. |
| Identity Theft | Provides coverage, for an annual fee of $25, to help pay for expenses and recovery costs related to identity fraud. |
| Scheduled Personal Property | Offers replacement cost coverage for high-value belongings such as jewelry, sports equipment and medical devices. |
| Umbrella Insurance | Adds extra liability protection beyond standard policy limits for personal injury or property damage claims. |

### What Does State Farm’s Home Insurance Not Cover?

While State Farm’s homeowners insurance provides broad protection, it excludes certain types of risks. Understanding these exclusions helps homeowners know when extra coverage may be necessary.

![flood icon](https://res.cloudinary.com/moneygeek/image/upload/v1585171420/assets/components/icon/flood_kua1hg.svg)

**Floods and Earthquakes**

Standard home insurance doesn’t pay for flood or earthquake damage. You’ll need separate flood insurance or other coverage to stay protected.

![injury icon](https://res.cloudinary.com/moneygeek/image/upload/v1585171420/assets/components/icon/injury_ykcx1b.svg)

**Intentional or Self-Inflicted Damage**

Damage you cause on purpose or through neglect isn’t covered. Insurers exclude these losses because they result from preventable actions.

![business icon](https://res.cloudinary.com/moneygeek/image/upload/v1585171420/assets/components/icon/business_bshihe.svg)

**Business Use of the Home**

If you operate a business or rent part of your home, related losses or liability claims aren’t included. Separate commercial or landlord coverage is needed for those activities.

3. 3

   Compare Policy Options

   Look at different coverage levels and deductible amounts to see what works best for your situation. Consider any optional endorsements that could strengthen your protection.
4. 4

   Apply for Discounts

   Ask about potential savings before finalizing your quote. Discounts for impact-resistant roofing or protective home devices can make your premium more manageable.
5. 5

   Review and Finalize Your Policy

   Go over your coverage limits, exclusions and deductibles before signing. Once you’re satisfied, complete the purchase to activate your homeowners insurance policy.

### How to File a Homeowners Insurance Claim with State Farm

[Filing a home insurance claim](https://www.moneygeek.com/insurance/homeowners/how-does-a-homeowners-insurance-claim-work/) with State Farm is simple and can be done online, through the mobile app or by contacting your local agent. Acting quickly after a loss helps you start repairs sooner and get reimbursed for covered damages.

1. 1

   Report the Claim

   Notify State Farm as soon as possible after the incident by filing a claim online, through the app or by calling your agent. Provide key details such as the type of damage, when it occurred and any immediate repairs made to prevent further loss.
2. 2

   Document the Damage

   Take clear photos or videos of all affected areas and damaged property. Keep receipts for emergency repairs or temporary living expenses to support your claim.
3. 3

   Schedule an Inspection

   A claims adjuster will contact you to inspect the damage and assess the extent of the loss. During the inspection, share any documentation or estimates you’ve gathered to help with the evaluation.
4. 4

   Review the Settlement and Repair Process

   Once the inspection is complete, State Farm will provide a settlement estimate outlining what’s covered. Review the details carefully, ask questions if needed and proceed with repairs using your claim payment.

### State Farm Home Insurance: FAQ

Below are answers to frequently asked questions about State Farm home insurance:

### Is State Farm home insurance good?

Yes. With a MoneyGeek score of 4.5 out of 5, State Farm ranks among the leading insurers for its balance of affordability, customer satisfaction and coverage options. It offers both standard protections and valuable add-ons like water backup and identity theft coverage.

### How much is home insurance with State Farm?

The average cost for a State Farm policy with $250,000 in dwelling coverage is $2,454 per year. Rates vary depending on your location, home features, credit score and the amount of coverage you choose.

### Does State Farm offer discounts on homeowners insurance?

Yes. State Farm offers four main discounts, including savings for installing home security devices, using impact-resistant roofing, meeting wind mitigation standards and taking wildfire prevention measures in eligible areas. Availability depends on your state.

### What optional coverages can you add to a State Farm home insurance policy?

Homeowners can enhance their protection with options such as water backup, home systems protection, personal articles coverage, identity theft restoration and umbrella insurance. These add-ons give you extra peace of mind beyond the standard policy.

### What does State Farm home insurance not cover?

State Farm’s standard homeowners policy doesn’t cover floods, earthquakes, intentional damage or losses linked to business use of the home. Separate flood or commercial coverage would be needed for those risks.
