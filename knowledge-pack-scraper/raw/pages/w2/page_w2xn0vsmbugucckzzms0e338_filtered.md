## Finding car insurance when you've been denied coverage

If you‚Äôve been denied auto insurance, you‚Äôre not alone. Some companies ‚Äî even [non-standard insurers](/auto-insurance/insurance-guide/standard-insurance-vs-non-standard-insurance/) ‚Äî may refuse coverage if your driving record includes serious violations or multiple incidents.

In these cases, **assigned risk insurance may be your only option**. It‚Äôs a last-resort program for drivers with poor records who can‚Äôt get coverage elsewhere.[[1]](#footnote-1)

To qualify, you‚Äôll need to show that you‚Äôve applied for insurance several times and been denied due to your record. An insurance agent can then file a report with your state, which may approve you for the assigned risk pool.

**Your best bet for saving money**¬†is to¬†shop around, compare quotes, and find¬†companies that work with high-risk drivers.

## What driving factors make you a high-risk driver?

When an insurance company calculates your premium for your [auto insurance policy](https://www.thezebra.com/auto-insurance/policies/), they assess driving and non-driving factors to determine how much risk you pose.

First, insurers typically look at the number of violations or citations you've received. Each infraction typically results in a rate hike, but the type of violation and quantity push you further into high-risk territory. If you have one or more of the following points on your record, you may be considered high risk:¬†

* [At-fault accidents](https://www.thezebra.com/auto-insurance/accidents/)
* [Speeding tickets](https://www.thezebra.com/auto-insurance/tickets-violations/)
* Reckless driving charges
* Street racing charges
* [DUIs/DUIs](https://www.thezebra.com/auto-insurance/tickets-violations/car-insurance-after-dui/)

### Your financial history

Even if you‚Äôve [never had an accident](https://www.thezebra.com/auto-insurance/driver/other-factors/best-car-insurance-good-drivers/), your finances can affect your insurance rates.

* Insurers often use **credit-based insurance scores** to predict how likely you are to file a claim.
* If you‚Äôve had a [**lapse in coverage**](https://www.thezebra.com/auto-insurance/coverage/car-insurance-after-lapsed-coverage/) or a low credit score, you might be seen as a higher risk.

***Tip:*** *Try to maintain continuous coverage and check if* [*your state limits the use of credit*](https://www.thezebra.com/resources/research/credit-insurance-rates/) *in pricing.*

![location]()

### Where you live and drive

[Your ZIP code](https://www.thezebra.com/auto-insurance/driver/other-factors/) affects more than just your mail ‚Äî it shapes your insurance price, too.

* Living in a **high-traffic, high-claim** area usually means higher rates.
* If you use your car for [**rideshare**](https://www.thezebra.com/auto-insurance/driver/profession/rideshare-insurance/) **driving or long commutes**, insurers may also charge more to reflect that added risk.

***Tip:*** *Be sure your policy matches how you actually use your car ‚Äî* [*commuter vs. pleasure*](https://www.thezebra.com/auto-insurance/policies/pleasure-use-vs-commuter-auto-insurance/) *use matters.*

![driving experience]()

### Your age and experience

New drivers are more likely to file claims ‚Äî and insurers know it.

* [**Teenagers**](https://www.thezebra.com/auto-insurance/driver/age/cheap-car-insurance-teens/) and drivers with limited experience **typically pay more.**
* **Some states also factor in your** [**age**](https://www.thezebra.com/auto-insurance/driver/age/) directly when calculating your rate.

***Tip:*** *Good student* [*discounts*](https://www.thezebra.com/auto-insurance/discounts/) *and safe-driving programs can help younger drivers save.*

### Your car and driving record

Both the type of car, and the way you drive it, matter.

* [**High-performance**](https://www.thezebra.com/auto-insurance/vehicles/sports-car-insurance/) **or luxury vehicles** usually cost more to insure.
* If you have **points from tickets or violations**, expect those to raise your rate, too.

***Tip:*** *Most* [*points*](https://www.thezebra.com/auto-insurance/tickets-violations/how-do-license-points-affect-auto-insurance/) *stay on your record for a few years, depending on your state ‚Äî but shopping around can still lower your rate.*

## High-risk car insurance FAQs

How much is high-risk car insurance?
Chevron down icon

High-risk drivers will pay more for car insurance. However, the increase in your premiums will vary [depending on a number of variables](https://www.thezebra.com/auto-insurance/driver/other-factors/). For example, a DUI/DWI conviction can raise your premiums by around 96%, which adds up to an extra $1,680 per year. On the other hand, a speeding ticket could increase the annual cost by an average of $431.

Which company has the cheapest car insurance for high-risk drivers?
Chevron down icon

This depends on your situation, but among the major carriers, [USAA](https://www.thezebra.com/insurance-companies/usaa-reviews-coverage-options-and-ratings/) tends to provide the cheapest coverage for drivers in a number of high-risk categories, such as at-fault accidents and speeding tickets. However, as USAA is only available to those in the military (or their immediate family members), other carriers offering lower rates include GEICO, Nationwide and State Farm. Chances are a major carrier will not offer the cheapest rate, so it might be worth looking into a carrier that specializes in high-risk insurance.

What if I can‚Äôt find auto insurance coverage?
Chevron down icon

If you are denied coverage from a standard carrier, your best bet is to look for what‚Äôs known as a [non-standard carrier](https://www.thezebra.com/auto-insurance/insurance-guide/standard-insurance-vs-non-standard-insurance/). These insurance companies specialize in insuring drivers who don‚Äôt have the cleanest driving records. While you may forego some of the perks offered by standard carriers like GEICO or State Farm, you will be more likely to find coverage. However, if your driving record is such that even non-standard carriers are wary of taking you, most states have what is known as a high-risk pool. Your state can mandate that insurance companies provide auto coverage to these drivers, but beware, this coverage is not cheap.
