## Arkansas SR-22 Insurance Requirements

An SR-22, also called an SR-22 bond or certificate of financial responsibility, isn't insurance but a form your insurer files to confirm you meet Arkansas's Financial Responsibility law for license reinstatement. To comply with Arkansas laws, you must carry these [minimum liability limits](https://www.moneygeek.com/insurance/auto/state-minimum-car-insurance-requirements/):

* $25,000 personal injury liability per person
* $50,000 personal injury liability per accident
* $25,000 property damage liability per accident

In the state, SR-22 certificates are usually required for three years. It can be extended to 5 years or more, depending on the violation and if there is noncompliance with requirements or repeated offenses.

![mglogo icon](https://res.cloudinary.com/moneygeek/image/upload/v1585171420/assets/components/icon/mg-logo_wxc8wu.svg)

TYPES OF SR-22 FILINGS

Arkansas drivers required to file SR-22 insurance have several choices. A traditional SR-22 Owners Certificate provides coverage only when driving your own vehicle, while [non-owner SR-22 insurance](https://www.moneygeek.com/insurance/auto/non-owner-sr-22-insurance/) in Arkansas (SR-22 Operators Certificate) covers you when driving vehicles belonging to others.

If you need both, Arkansas offers a comprehensive solution through the SR-22 Operators-Owner Certificate. This combined filing protects you whether driving your own car or someone else's vehicle.

### Other Arkansas SR-22 Insurance Laws and Regulations

Arkansas drivers need to understand various insurance laws and regulations beyond just filing an SR-22 to meet the state's minimum coverage requirements.

![money2 icon](https://res.cloudinary.com/moneygeek/image/upload/v1585171420/assets/components/icon/money_c8wtrb.svg)

SR-22 Filing and Reinstatement Fees

SR-22 filing fees in Arkansas range from $25 to $50, while license reinstatement fees cost between $100 and $150, based on the violation type. Arkansas offers payment plans for fees totaling more than $500, helping drivers manage these costs more easily.

![uninsured icon](https://res.cloudinary.com/moneygeek/image/upload/v1585171420/assets/components/icon/uninsured_jck1df.svg)

Coverage Lapses and Cancellations

If your SR-22 insurance lapses or gets canceled, your insurance company must notify the Arkansas Office of Motor Vehicle immediately. Your license will be suspended again until you file a new SR-22 and pay additional reinstatement fees.

![clock icon](https://res.cloudinary.com/moneygeek/image/upload/v1585171420/assets/components/icon/clock_klyche.svg)

SR-22 Requirement Duration

Arkansas requires SR-22 insurance for three years, but this extends to 5 years for serious violations involving injuries, death, or major property damage.

For non-driving violations, the requirements are:

+ **First offense:** one-year SR-22 requirement
+ **Second offense within five years:** two-year suspension with continued SR-22 requirement
+ **Third offense within five years:** three-year suspension with continued SR-22 requirement

![insurance2 icon](https://res.cloudinary.com/moneygeek/image/upload/v1585171420/assets/components/icon/insurance_2_iixd7m.svg)

SR-22 Filing Process

To file an SR-22 in Arkansas, contact an insurance company licensed in the state and purchase a policy meeting Arkansas minimum requirements. Your insurance company files the SR-22 electronically with the Arkansas Office of Motor Vehicle, with processing typically completed within 72 hours.

WHAT TO DO IF PROVIDERS DENY YOU COVERAGE

Arkansas drivers who have been denied car insurance coverage still have options for finding a policy. While Arkansas doesn't operate a high-risk insurance program, the Arkansas Insurance Department helps connect drivers with private insurers who specialize in covering high-risk drivers.
