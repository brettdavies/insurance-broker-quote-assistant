## Minimum car insurance requirements in Mississippi

Mississippi car insurance policies must include liability insurance. If you ever cause an accident, this coverage pays for others‚Äô injuries or property damage, up to your insurance limit.

The minimum limits you can select are:

* $25,000 bodily injury liability per person.
* $50,000 bodily injury liability per accident.
* $25,000 property damage liability per accident.

In lieu of car insurance, you may post a bond or make a cash or security deposit equal to the amounts above.

Most drivers can benefit from buying more than the legal minimum, as a crash could easily result in injury or repair bills higher than the amounts listed above. You may also want to consider common add-ons such as collision, comprehensive and uninsured motorist protection.

Check out NerdWallet‚Äôs guide to [car insurance requirements by state](https://www.nerdwallet.com/article/insurance/minimum-car-insurance-requirements) for more help understanding coverage options for your Mississippi auto policy.
