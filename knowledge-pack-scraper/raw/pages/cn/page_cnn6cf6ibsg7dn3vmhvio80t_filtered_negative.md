## State Farm Renters Insurance Coverage
Standard State Farm [renters insurance policy coverage](https://www.usnews.com/insurance/renters-insurance/what-does-renters-insurance-cover) includes:
#### Personal Property
This coverage pays for damages to personal items not attached to the home (e.g., clothing, furniture, electronics) if they are damaged or stolen by a covered event.
#### Personal Liability Coverage
This covers you if someone claims you caused their bodily injury or property damage. It includes up to $500 of coverage if your pet causes damage to the property you rent. It will cover legal settlements for damages and may pay defense and court costs.
#### Loss of Use
This helps pay your additional living expenses if your home is uninhabitable after a covered loss. It may cover expenses such as food and hotel stays.
#### Medical Payments to Others
Also known as guest medical payments, this provides limited coverage for injuries that others incur on your property.
## Additional State Farm Renters Insurance Coverage
Here are some additional coverage options you can add to your policy.
* **Back-Up Sewer or Drain:** This pays for damages from water that backs up from sewers or drains.
* **Earthquake:** This covers damages that occur from tremors in your area.
* **Cyber Event:** State Farm offers cyber extortion coverage and cyberattack coverage. These pay expenses related to a loss, such as data recovery, system restoration, and professional assistance from an expert, plus related response costs from extortion.
* **Identity Restoration:** This provides case management and expense reimbursement as a direct result of identity fraud. It provides identity restoration case managers to respond to identity fraud as well.
* **Fraud Loss:** This helps you recover if you’re a victim of fraud. It offers limited coverage for credit and debit fraud, check forgery, and counterfeit cash. It also provides coverage for financial losses from a variety of frauds, such as identity fraud.
### Other Coverage
Policies may also provide payment for credit or bank card forgery, debris removal, locks and remote devices, refrigerated products, power interruption, temporary repairs, shrubs and landscaping, fire department service charges, and more.
## How Much Does State Farm Renters Insurance Cost?

## State Farm Renters Insurance Discounts
State Farm offers several discounts to help you save on your policy premium:
* **Home Security:** You may receive a home security discount if you install certain home security devices, such as a smoke detector or fire alarm.
* **Multi-Policy:** Bundling your renters insurance policy with another State Farm insurance policy may help you save on your premium.
* **Claims Free:** You may qualify if you haven’t filed a claim in a set period, typically three to five years.
## How Do I Buy a Renters Insurance Policy From State Farm?
You can begin the process of [purchasing a renters insurance policy](https://www.usnews.com/insurance/renters-insurance/how-to-buy-renters-insurance) by obtaining a quote online. The website states that the quote process should take less than 10 minutes.
Be prepared to share details about:
* **Yourself:** Name, date of birth, contact information
* **Rental property:** Address, property type (e.g., single-family home, condo, townhouse)
* **Insurance history:** Claims, most recent insurance provider, last date of coverage
* **Property safety features:** Burglar or fire alarm, sprinkler system, distance to the nearest fire station
Once you have a quote, you’ll work with a State Farm agent to customize and secure your policy. With 19,000 agents located across the United States, there’s plenty of support available.
## How Do I File a State Farm Renters Insurance Claim?
If your property is damaged during a covered event, you can [file a claim](https://www.usnews.com/insurance/renters-insurance/how-to-file-a-renters-insurance-claim) for reimbursement.
1. **File your claim:** You can file a claim online, via the mobile app, or by phone at 800-732-5246. An agent will confirm the claim and provide you with a point of contact in case you have questions.
2. **Be available during the investigation:** You should be available to answer questions about your claim and provide additional details as needed. This will help to avoid processing delays. During the investigation, your claim handler will determine whether you’re owed a settlement.
3. **Receive your settlement:** You can specify whether you prefer the settlement payment by check or direct deposit.
