The [average cost](https://www.valuepenguin.com/average-cost-of-insurance "Average Cost of Car Insurance") of full coverage car insurance in Alabama is $196 per month, but where you live in the state can impact the price you pay. Areas with narrow streets, poorly maintained roads or higher crime rates tend to have higher prices for car insurance.
Drivers must have a certain amount of [liability car insurance](https://www.valuepenguin.com/cheap-liability-only-car-insurance "Cheapest Liability-Only Car Insurance") in Alabama to drive legally and avoid [penalties](https://www.valuepenguin.com/auto-insurance/alabama/penalties-driving-without-insurance "Penalties for Driving without Insurance in Alabama"). The requirement is sometimes written as  25/50/25.

**In addition, liability-only insurance doesn't include[collision and comprehensive coverage](https://www.valuepenguin.com/collision-comprehensive-car-insurance "Collision vs. Comprehensive Car Insurance").** These coverages pay for most types of damage to your car, regardless of whose fault it is.

This is partially because Alabama has higher auto theft rates than the U.S. average, according to the [FBI](https://cde.ucr.cjis.gov/LATEST/webapp/#/pages/explorer/crime/crime-trend "FBI: Crime Data Explorer").
