## What is the mandatory liability insurance in Alabama?
Drivers must have a certain amount of [liability car insurance](https://www.valuepenguin.com/cheap-liability-only-car-insurance "Cheapest Liability-Only Car Insurance") in Alabama to drive legally and avoid [penalties](https://www.valuepenguin.com/auto-insurance/alabama/penalties-driving-without-insurance "Penalties for Driving without Insurance in Alabama"). The requirement is sometimes written as  25/50/25.
  * **[Bodily injury liability (BI)](https://www.valuepenguin.com/bodily-injury-car-insurance-coverage "Bodily Injury Car Insurance Coverage: How Does It Work?"):** $25,000 per person and $50,000 per accident 
  * **[Property damage liability (PD)](https://www.valuepenguin.com/property-damage-liability-car-insurance "What Is Property Damage Liability Car Insurance?"):** $25,000 per accident 


**Most people should get more coverage than the minimum requirement if they can afford it.**
That's because the liability limits required in Alabama may not be high enough to cover the cost of a serious accident. For example, if you hit an expensive car, it's unlikely that $25,000 of property damage liability will be enough to buy the other driver a new vehicle.
**In addition, liability-only insurance doesn't include[collision and comprehensive coverage](https://www.valuepenguin.com/collision-comprehensive-car-insurance "Collision vs. Comprehensive Car Insurance").** These coverages pay for most types of damage to your car, regardless of whose fault it is.
Comprehensive coverage is the only way to [protect your car against damage from natural disasters](https://aldoi.gov/Preparedness/WeatherDisasterPreparedness.aspx "Alabama Department of Insurance: Weather Disaster Preparedness"). This includes tornado damage, flooding from hurricanes and hail damage, all of which are common in Alabama.
If you have a car loan or lease, your lender probably requires you to have collision and comprehensive coverage. You should also consider these coverages if your car is less than 8 years old or worth more than $5,000.
