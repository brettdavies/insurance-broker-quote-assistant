## Average Cost of Home Insurance in Washington by Coverage Amount

In Washington, homeowners pay an annual premium of $1,410 for policies with $250K Dwelling / $125K Personal Property / $200K Liability coverage. This cost is less than the [national average](https://www.moneygeek.com/insurance/homeowners/average-cost-of-home-insurance/).

When deciding [how much coverage you need](https://www.moneygeek.com/insurance/homeowners/how-much-home-insurance-do-you-need/), consider the total cost to rebuild your home and replace your belongings. For a policy offering $100K in dwelling coverage, the annual premium is $867. However, if you own a larger home requiring $500K in dwelling coverage, the premium increases to $2,451 annually. These figures demonstrate the direct correlation between coverage amounts and premium costs.

## Average Cost of Home Insurance in Washington by Construction and Roof Type

The type of roof on your home affects insurance premiums. Composition roofing, a common choice, often leads to an average premium that's slightly lower than more expensive materials. Shake-treated roofs, while aesthetically pleasing, can raise insurance costs due to their maintenance needs and vulnerability to severe weather.

Construction materials also influene premium pricing. Homes featuring superior construction materials, which are more resistant to perils like fire, can mean favorable insurance rates. Conversely, standard frame construction, while widespread, comes with higher premiums due to its commonality and risk profile.

## Average Cost of Home Insurance in Washington by Claims History

In Washington, homeowners who have maintained a claim-free status for at least five years can expect an average annual premium of $1,410 for their homeowners insurance. Those with two claims in the same period have an even steeper hike, with an average annual premium of $1,908, marking a $498 uptick from the claim-free rate.

## FAQ: Homeowners Insurance in Washington

### On average, what is the cheapest insurer in Washington?

Allstate offers the lowest average premium at $741. There are other [cheap homeowners insurance](https:/www.moneygeek.com/insurance/homeowners/cheap-homeowners-insurance/) options available.

### Is flooding in Washington covered by homeowners insurance?

Flooding is not typically covered under [natural disaster coverage](https://www.moneygeek.com/insurance/homeowners/does-homeowners-insurance-cover-natural-disasters/). Separate flood insurance can be purchased through the National Flood Insurance Program or a private flood insurer, and some home insurers offer it as an endorsement.

### What is the biggest factor that impacts the price of homeowners insurance in Washington?

The insurance provider is the biggest factor, with premiums ranging from $741 to $2633.

### In Washington, how much, on average, can I save bundling my homeowners insurance with auto?

You can save between $104 and $365 by [bundling](https://www.moneygeek.com/insurance/auto/best-home-auto-bundle-in-washington/).
