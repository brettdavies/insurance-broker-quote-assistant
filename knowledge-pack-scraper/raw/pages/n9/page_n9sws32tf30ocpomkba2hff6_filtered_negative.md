# Insurer vs. Insured
Insurance is a legal contract between an insurer and the insured, providing financial protection against risks. The insurer, typically a firm, assumes financial risks in exchange for premiums, while the insured receives coverage. Key responsibilities of the insurer include risk assessment, premium collection, and claims processing.
The insured must provide accurate information, pay premiums, and follow policy procedures. Understanding these roles is crucial for a smooth claims process and risk management. Transparency and adherence to policy terms benefit both parties in the agreement.
