## FAQs

### What makes car insurance more expensive in Texas compared to other states?

Texas ranks as one of the most expensive state for car insurance, primarily because nearly 85% of the state's population lives in densely populated urban areas like Houston, Dallas, and San Antonio where traffic congestion and accident rates are significantly higher. Additionally, Texas experiences diverse weather hazards including hurricanes along the Gulf Coast, hailstorms in the northern regions, and flooding, all of which increase claim frequency and repair costs for insurers operating in the state.

### Does Texas require Personal Injury Protection (PIP) coverage on my policy?

Texas law requires insurance companies to offer [Personal Injury Protection coverage](https://financebuzz.com/what-is-pip-car-insurance) to all drivers, but you are not required to purchase it. If you choose to decline PIP coverage, you must sign a written waiver with your insurance company. PIP covers medical expenses, lost wages, and funeral costs for you and your passengers regardless of who caused the accident, making it valuable protection in Texas's at-fault insurance system where uninsured motorist rates remain a concern.

### Can Texas insurance companies raise my rates before the state approves the increase?

Yes, Texas has a unique regulation that allows insurance companies to implement rate increases immediately, even before the Texas Department of Insurance completes its review and approval process. This means Texas drivers may see premium hikes take effect faster than in other states. The state monitors loss ratios to ensure companies aren't charging excessive rates, but this regulatory structure can result in more frequent rate adjustments for policyholders.
