## Massive DVD-sized hail pelts Texas

Massive hailstones fell across portions of Texas on Sunday as severe thunderstorms raced across the area, including DVD-sized hail that fell in Afton. FOX Weather Meteorologist Kendall Smith has the latest on May 26, 2025.

**OKLAHOMA CITY** – [Memorial Day](https://www.foxweather.com/category/holidays) weekend plans from the Plains to the [Southeast](https://www.foxweather.com/category/southeast) were put in jeopardy on Sunday as a slow-moving storm system pummeled communities with massive [hail](https://www.foxweather.com/category/hail), damaging [wind](https://www.foxweather.com/category/wind) gusts and even some reports of [tornadoes](https://www.foxweather.com/category/tornadoes).

[Flooding](https://www.foxweather.com/category/flood) was also an issue, as rounds of torrential rain overwhelmed storm drains, rivers and streams.

[Hail larger than grapefruits](https://www.foxweather.com/learn/hail-size-differences-severe-weather-hailstorm) fell in [Texas](https://www.foxweather.com/category/texas) on Sunday evening. Colt Forney with Atmospheric Chaos said the 5.5-inch and 6-inch giant hailstones fell in [Afton](https://www.foxweather.com/local-weather/texas/afton).

Pictures show him comparing one of the hailstones to a [baseball](https://www.foxweather.com/category/sports) and another to a baseball hat.

There were numerous reports of large hail in the Afton area, ranging from 4 to 6 inches.

There was also a report of 3-inch hail in Goldsby, [Oklahoma](https://www.foxweather.com/category/oklahoma).

## Watch: Lightning strikes Arkansas radio tower

A video shared by the Washington County Sheriff's Office in Arkansas shows the moment a bolt of lightning struck a radio tower on Sunday, May 25, 2025.

Tens of millions of people from Texas and Oklahoma through the [Mississippi](https://www.foxweather.com/category/mississippi) Valley and Southeast were at risk of severe [thunderstorms](https://www.foxweather.com/category/thunderstorms) on Sunday, and some of those areas were hit hard.

A [video](https://www.foxweather.com/video) recorded in [Arkansas](https://www.foxweather.com/category/arkansas) shows the moment a bolt of [lightning](https://www.foxweather.com/category/lightning) struck a radio tower in [Fayetteville](https://www.foxweather.com/local-weather/arkansas/fayetteville) on Sunday.

Winds were also whipping during the storms, with an 81-mph wind gust reported in [Amherst](https://www.foxweather.com/local-weather/texas/amherst), Texas. That’s as strong as a [Category 1 hurricane](https://www.foxweather.com/learn/tropical-cyclone-one-explainer-season).

Some communities in Texas also reported tornadoes. According to NOAA’s Storm Prediction Center (SPC), possible tornadoes were reported in Afton, Amherst, Stamford and Floyd.

## Watch: Texas storm chaser, son capture video of possible tornadoes

A storm chaser and his son captured incredible video of two possible tornadoes in Texas on Sunday as severe weather tore across portions of the Lone Star State.

An incredible video captured by a storm chaser and his son showed images of what appeared to be two tornadoes spinning across the landscape in the Stamford area on Sunday.

[The area was under a Tornado Warning](https://x.com/NWStornado/status/1926800436050158027) at the time the video was taken.

## Watch: Drone video shows flooding in Ozark, Missouri

A dramatic drone video recorded in Ozark, Missouri, shows flooding that occurred after torrential rain and thunderstorms moved over the area on Saturday, May 24, 2025.

The heavy rain also led to reports of water rescues and flooding in [Missouri](https://www.foxweather.com/category/missouri).

A [drone](https://www.foxweather.com/category/drones) video recorded in [Ozark](https://www.foxweather.com/local-weather/missouri/ozark), Missouri, shows neighborhoods surrounded by water after flooding due to the relentless [precipitation](https://www.foxweather.com/category/precipitation).

Water can also be seen getting dangerously close to the bottom of a bridge while the flooding was ongoing.

Sunday’s threat came after [parts of the same regions were slammed with severe thunderstorms and flooding](https://www.foxweather.com/weather-news/memorial-day-forecast-us-rain-flood) on Saturday, with damage to buildings reported in [Oklahoma](https://www.foxweather.com/category/oklahoma), water rescues in [Missouri](https://www.foxweather.com/category/missouri) and storms tearing across communities in [Florida](https://www.foxweather.com/category/florida).
