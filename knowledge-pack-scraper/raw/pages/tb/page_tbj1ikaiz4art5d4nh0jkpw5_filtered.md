## Is auto insurance required in California?

Car insurance is required by law in California. [The state legal minimum has been increased for 2025](https://www.insurance.com/auto-insurance/states-with-minimum-car-insurance-requirement-changes/). The new requirements are:

* $30,000 per person for bodily injury

* $60,000 per incident for bodily injury

* $15,000 per incident for property damage

## How much is car insurance in California?

The average cost of [full coverage](/auto-insurance/coverage/full-coverage.html) car insurance in California is **$2,416** per year. But these rates can differ depending on factors like your age, the coverage you choose, your driving record and the type of car you drive.

For example, younger drivers usually pay more for car insurance, and people with bad driving records do, too.

California drivers who choose to carry only the minimum car insurance will pay $551 on average. But this doesn't include [collision or comprehensive coverage](/auto-insurance/coverage/comprehensive-and-collision-auto-insurance.html), so if you cause an accident, you'd have to pay for damage to your own car yourself.

## California car insurance laws

Certain driver groups in California are subject to specific laws.

### California car insurance laws for high-risk drivers

The insurance requirements for high-risk drivers in California are not different, but you will be required to file an SR-22 if convicted of certain traffic violations. An SR-22 is a document filed by your insurance company that indicates you have the legally required insurance.

You may need an SR-22 after:

* A license suspension

* Driving without insurance

* A DUI

* Multiple tickets

### California car insurance laws for teen drivers

California follows a graduated licensing program that eases teen drivers into the responsibilities of driving with a series of stages, each with restrictions.

1. Learner's permit. Driving is only permitted with a driver 25 years old or older in the car, no alcohol is permitted in the vehicle and the permit must be held for at least six months.

2. Provisional License. No passengers under 20 for the first 12 months unless an adult is present. No driving between 12 p.m. and 5 a.m. for the first 12 months with exceptions. No alcohol in the vehicle.

3. Full License. Must be 18, at which time restrictions are lifted, although alcohol is still not permitted in the vehicle.

### California car insurance laws for senior drivers

There are no insurance laws that apply to senior drivers, but drivers over 70 must apply to renew their license in person every **five years**.

## How to compare car insurance companies in California

To compare car insurance in California and find the best fit for your needs, follow these steps:

1. Review your current policy. Note what coverage you are carrying and decide if you want to make any changes.

2. Request quotes at your desired coverage levels. Make sure you are requesting the same coverage levels on each quote. If you're looking to change your coverage, ask your current insurance company to provide a quote with those limits as well.

3. Review the quotes and note any added benefits. Some [car insurance companies](https://www.insurance.com/auto-insurance/carriers.aspx) include extras at no additional charge. Is [roadside assistance](https://www.insurance.com/auto-insurance/auto-insurance-basics/can-roadside-assistance-sabotage-your-car-insurance.aspx) included? Are there other perks like a diminishing deductible program?

4. Research the car insurance companies you're considering. [Cheap car insurance](https://www.insurance.com/auto-insurance/cheapest-car-insurance-rates-by-state) isn't everything. Make sure you are also looking at each insurance provider's reputation to make sure you find not just the cheapest car insurance but also the [best car insurance companies in California](https://www.insurance.com/auto-insurance/cheapest-car-insurance-in-california.html).

## Methodology

Insurance.com commissioned car insurance rates from Quadrant Data Systems in late 2023 for all 50 states and Washington, D.C. Base rates were fielded based on a 40-year-old driver with a clean record and good credit, driving a 2023 Honda Accord LX.
