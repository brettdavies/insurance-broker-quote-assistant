## Is auto insurance required in California? 
Car insurance is required by law in California. [The state legal minimum has been increased for 2025](https://www.insurance.com/auto-insurance/states-with-minimum-car-insurance-requirement-changes/). The new requirements are: 
  * $30,000 per person for bodily injury 


  * $60,000 per incident for bodily injury 


  * $15,000 per incident for property damage

## California car insurance laws 
Certain driver groups in California are subject to specific laws. 
### California car insurance laws for high-risk drivers 
The insurance requirements for high-risk drivers in California are not different, but you will be required to file an SR-22 if convicted of certain traffic violations. An SR-22 is a document filed by your insurance company that indicates you have the legally required insurance. 
You may need an SR-22 after: 
  * A license suspension 


  * Driving without insurance 


  * A DUI 


  * Multiple tickets 


### California car insurance laws for teen drivers 
California follows a graduated licensing program that eases teen drivers into the responsibilities of driving with a series of stages, each with restrictions. 
  1. Learner's permit. Driving is only permitted with a driver 25 years old or older in the car, no alcohol is permitted in the vehicle and the permit must be held for at least six months. 


  1. Provisional License. No passengers under 20 for the first 12 months unless an adult is present. No driving between 12 p.m. and 5 a.m. for the first 12 months with exceptions. No alcohol in the vehicle. 


  1. Full License. Must be 18, at which time restrictions are lifted, although alcohol is still not permitted in the vehicle. 


### California car insurance laws for senior drivers 
There are no insurance laws that apply to senior drivers, but drivers over 70 must apply to renew their license in person every **five years**.

## Methodology
Insurance.com commissioned car insurance rates from Quadrant Data Systems in late 2023 for all 50 states and Washington, D.C. Base rates were fielded based on a 40-year-old driver with a clean record and good credit, driving a 2023 Honda Accord LX. Coverage levels include state minimum, 50/100/50 liability only, and full coverage at 100/300/100 liability limits with $500 deductibles.
