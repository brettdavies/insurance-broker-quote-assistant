State Farm is so expensive because car insurance is expensive in general, due to rising costs for insurers. But at $718 per year, the average State Farm car insurance policy is actually cheaper than coverage from most competitors. In fact, State Farm is one of the [cheapest car insurance companies](https://wallethub.com/cheap-car-insurance/) nationally.

Even though State Farm offers cheaper-than-average rates, its premiums can get expensive for drivers with certain characteristics. For example, teenagers pay an average of $1,803 per year for State Farm insurance, compared to $718 per year for older drivers. Additionally, drivers who recently had an at-fault accident pay an average of $1,140 per year for State Farm coverage, which is 59% more than drivers with a clean record.

## Factors That Can Make State Farm Insurance More Expensive

* Being a young or inexperienced driver
* Purchasing a [full coverage](https://wallethub.com/edu/ci/full-coverage-car-insurance/10505) policy versus minimum coverage
* Causing an accident or being convicted of a moving violation
* Living in a high-risk ZIP code
* Driving an expensive vehicle
* Having a poor [credit-based insurance score](https://wallethub.com/edu/ci/auto-insurance-score/39224)

If you're having trouble affording your State Farm insurance, there are ways for you to lower your premium. For example, you can take advantage of [State Farm discounts](/edu/ci/state-farm-discounts/90977) or reduce your coverage.

In some cases, it might be better to [switch insurers](https://wallethub.com/edu/ci/how-to-switch-car-insurance-companies/13553) altogether. It's usually a good idea to [compare quotes](https://wallethub.com/edu/ci/compare-car-insurance/93611) from at least three different insurers to see if you can find a better deal elsewhere. Each insurer uses its own algorithm to calculate premiums, so even if State Farmis charging you a high premium, you may be able to find a cheaper policy with another insurance company.
