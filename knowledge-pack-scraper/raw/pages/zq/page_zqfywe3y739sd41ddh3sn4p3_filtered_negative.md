* SR-22 for Suspended Driver's License
![A police officer who pulled over a driver](https://www.statefarm.com/content/dam/sf-library/en-us/secure/legacy/simple-insights/144-suspended-drivers-license-wide.jpg)
Written by [State Farm® Editorial Team ](https://www.statefarm.com/simple-insights/editorial-team-and-process)
An SR-22 certifies that an individual has vehicle insurance that meets the state’s minimum [liability](https://www.statefarm.com/simple-insights/residence/what-is-individual-liability-insurance-and-what-does-it-cover) requirements and is needed to maintain driving privileges. The Certificate of Financial Responsibility is sometimes known as SR-22 insurance, but it’s important to clarify that it’s not a type of auto insurance. It is a certificate filed with the state to confirm coverage is maintained.
* [DUIs, DWIs](https://www.statefarm.com/simple-insights/auto-and-vehicles/the-real-consequences-of-drunk-driving) or other serious [moving violations](https://www.statefarm.com/simple-insights/auto-and-vehicles/the-real-costs-of-a-non-moving-or-moving-violation)
The state will notify you if an SR-22 is required. You will need to contact your insurance company, and they may file the SR-22 form with the state’s DMV. If your insurance provider does not assist with SR-22 filings, you likely will need to find a new provider. An SR-22 fee may be charged and may differ by state. The filing fee usually costs around $25. Additionally, there may be an increase to your [auto insurance premium cost](https://www.statefarm.com/simple-insights/auto-and-vehicles/industry-wide-rise-in-auto-insurance-rates).
[Contact your insurance agent](https://www.statefarm.com/agent/) for more SR-22 information, and to determine the SR-22 and liability insurance requirements in your state. Your insurance agent may be able to assist you in obtaining [auto insurance](https://www.statefarm.com/simple-insights/auto-and-vehicles/high-risk-auto-insurance) and the SR-22 filing through an insurance provider in your area.
