If the at-fault party does not have insurance, you could be responsible for paying for these expenses [out-of-pocket](https://www.mathyslaw.com/). Uninsured motorist coverage steps in to pay some or all of these damages if the at-fault driver is uninsured or can’t be identified.
To make an uninsured motorist claim, you must notify your insurance provider of the accident. A claims adjuster is assigned to your claim. They may ask for additional information about the accident to show that another party was at fault, such as a copy of the [accident report](https://www.mathyslaw.com/blog/2022/12/faqs-car-accident-reports-in-illinois/) and any photos of the accident scene. You work with your own insurance carrier throughout this process.
