## What are Wisconsin's minimum auto insurance requirements?

Drivers in Wisconsin are required to have [auto liability coverage](https://www.progressive.com/answers/auto-liability-insurance/) ([BI liability](https://www.progressive.com/answers/bodily-injury-liability/) and [PD liability](https://www.progressive.com/answers/property-damage-liability/)), and [uninsured/underinsured motorist (UM/UIM)](https://www.progressive.com/answers/uninsured-motorist-insurance/) bodily injury coverage at the following minimum limits:

### Coverage

| Coverage | What it covers | Minimum amount |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Bodily injury liability | What it covers Injuries you cause to others in an accident | Minimum amount $25,000 per person $50,000 per accident |
| Property damage liability | What it covers Damage you cause to other people's property in an accident | Minimum amount $10,000 per accident |
| UM/UIM bodily injury | What it covers Your medical expenses an uninsured or underinsured driver causes | Minimum amount $25,000 per person $50,000 per accident |

While these are Wisconsin's minimum required car [insurance limits](https://www.progressive.com/answers/insurance-limits/), higher coverage amounts may better protect your family's financial wellbeing. Learn [how much car insurance you may need](https://www.progressive.com/answers/how-much-car-insurance-do-i-need/) based on your circumstances and preferences.

### Is Wisconsin a no-fault state?

No. Wisconsin is an at-fault state, which means the party responsible for an accident must cover the injuries of the other driver and their passengers. Learn more about [what happens if you're at fault in an accident](https://www.progressive.com/answers/at-fault-vs-no-fault-accidents/).

## Additional car insurance coverages to consider

If you're leasing or financing a vehicle, most lenders require you to carry [comprehensive auto coverage](https://www.progressive.com/answers/comprehensive-car-insurance/) and [collision coverage](https://www.progressive.com/answers/auto-collision-coverage/) in addition to Wisconsin's state-required coverages. Comprehensive and collision pay to repair or replace your car regardless of fault, if it's in an accident, gets stolen, or is otherwise damaged due to a covered accident. It also makes sense to add these physical damage coverages if you're not sure you can afford a new car or major repairs after an accident.

## Wisconsin driving resources

From Ashland to Kenosha, get to know Wisconsin's driving laws and resources. These resources apply to Racine, Appleton, and everywhere in between. Local municipalities may have additional laws and resources.

* **[Wisconsin Department of Transportation DMV](https://wisconsindot.gov/pages/online-srvcs/external/dmv.aspx)**: Find out how to renew your license plate, get title and plates, renew your driver's license, get a non-driver ID, and access many other Wisconsin driver services.
* **[Wisconsin DoT safe driving tips](https://wisconsindot.gov/Pages/safety/education/safedriving.aspx)**: Wisconsin safety officials want to remind you to buckle up and put your phone down, get tips for what to do if you're involved in a crash, and the importance of yielding for first responders by obeying the "Move Over" law and more.
* **[Wisconsin driver information](https://wisconsindot.gov/Pages/online-srvcs/external/bds-landing.aspx)**: Get information about how to check or reinstate your Wisconsin driving privileges, get your REAL ID, schedule a road test, and several other online driver services.
