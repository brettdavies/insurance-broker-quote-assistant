[**Liability insurance**](https://insurify.com/car-insurance/coverage/liability-insurance/) may kick in after a covered accident. It can cover the other party’s property damage and medical expenses.
[**PIP**](https://insurify.com/car-insurance/coverage/pip-insurance/) and [**medical payments coverage**](https://insurify.com/car-insurance/knowledge/medical-payments-coverage/) can pay for medical bills your drivers and their passengers might face. It applies regardless of fault.
[**Collision insurance**](https://insurify.com/car-insurance/coverage/collision-insurance/) can pay to repair or replace your vehicle (or your employee’s) after a covered accident, regardless of who caused the accident.
[**Comprehensive insurance**](https://insurify.com/car-insurance/coverage/comprehensive-insurance/) might come in handy if your business vehicle sustains damage from something other than a collision, like theft, vandalism, or severe weather.
[**UM/UIM coverage**](https://insurify.com/car-insurance/coverage/uninsured-motorist-coverage/) may come into play if you or a worker has an accident with a driver who doesn’t have enough or any insurance. It may pay for medical bills, lost wages, and pain and suffering.
Through State Farm’s 24/7 [**roadside assistance**](https://insurify.com/car-insurance/knowledge/what-is-roadside-assistance/), you and your employees can return to work after an emergency situation on the road. This policy covers flat tire replacements, fuel delivery, towing, and more.
If one of your business vehicles is in the repair shop following a covered claim, [**rental car reimbursement**](https://insurify.com/car-insurance/vehicle/rental-car-insurance/) can reimburse you for a rental car. It can help keep your business running.
