Car insurance companies base premiums on multiple [**rating factors**](https://insurify.com/car-insurance/knowledge/factors-that-affect-rates/), including individual driver profiles — to estimate your risk level — your age, ZIP code, vehicle type, [**driving record**](https://insurify.com/car-insurance/driver/driving-record/clean-driving-record/), and more. These are some common factors insurers consider when setting rates.
More experienced drivers pay lower car insurance rates than young drivers. The crash rate for [**teen drivers**](https://insurify.com/car-insurance/driver/age/teenagers/) aged 16–19 is nearly four times the rate for drivers 20 and older, according to the Insurance Institute for Highway Safety (IIHS).[[2]](https://insurify.com/car-insurance/why-your-car-insurance-costs-are-so-high/#cite-2) Crash rates are lowest for people in their 60s, and that’s typically when you get the cheapest rates.

The annual national average for [**full-coverage car insurance**](https://insurify.com/car-insurance/coverage/full-coverage/) is $2,126, but people in some states pay up to 67% more than that. New York, Nevada, and Florida are the three most expensive states to buy car insurance, according to the latest [**Insurify auto insurance report**](https://insurify.com/car-insurance/report/).
Factors like population density, regional increases in severe accidents, vehicle theft, vandalism, and damaging weather events can lead to rising rates. Rampant insurance fraud contributed to soaring insurance rates in Florida in 2023.[[3]](https://insurify.com/car-insurance/why-your-car-insurance-costs-are-so-high/#cite-3)
Your driving record is one of the most critical factors in determining your car insurance rates. If you have a [**clean driving record**](https://insurify.com/car-insurance/driver/driving-record/clean-driving-record/), you’ll likely pay significantly less than drivers with traffic violations or accidents on their records. Car insurance companies tend to charge more if your driving habits indicate you could be a high-risk driver.
[**Drivers with a DUI**](https://insurify.com/car-insurance/driver/driving-record/dui-insurance/) pay the highest insurance rates, on average. COUNTRY Financial, the cheapest insurer for drivers with a DUI, charges $59 per month for liability coverage. By comparison, the company charges drivers with a clean record just $40 per month for liability insurance.
Drivers with [**at-fault accidents**](https://insurify.com/car-insurance/driver/driving-record/after-accident/) experience rate increases of about 26%, according to Insurify data. Depending on your insurance company, you could face a premium surcharge every year when your policy renews, for three to five years. Some insurers offer [**accident forgiveness**](https://insurify.com/car-insurance/driver/driving-record/accident-forgiveness/) coverage for your first car accident.
[**Speeding tickets**](https://insurify.com/car-insurance/driver/driving-record/speeding-ticket/) can also increase your premium, but how much of a spike you’ll see depends on your overall record, location, and insurance company.
