5 Best Mobile Home Insurance in Florida (2025 Guide for Manufactured Homes)

Get quotes from verified agents easily.

Emily Carter

Updated on: November 6, 2025

  • For Insureds

Florida is a paradise, boasting sunny skies, beautiful beaches, and a laid-back lifestyle. With manufactured homes becoming increasingly popular, especially over the last year, it’s essential to find the Best Mobile Home Insurance in Florida 2025 to protect your investment. In fact, Florida ranked No. 2 among the states in new manufactured homes in 2022.

Manufactured homes (often called mobile homes) are factory-built residences constructed under federal HUD standards. Unlike traditional site-built homes, these structures can be more vulnerable to extreme weather, especially in states like Florida, where windstorms, tropical systems, and heavy rainfall are common occurrences.

Finding solid coverage for mobile and manufactured homes isn’t always straightforward. Policies vary, exclusions are common, and premiums can skyrocket in high-risk zones.

In this guide, we’re cutting through the confusion. We’re talking about the best home insurance options for mobile and manufactured homes in Florida, who offers them, what they cover, and what to watch out for.

Why Mobile/Manufactured Homeowners in Florida Need Specific Insurance ?

Florida isn’t your average state when it comes to home insurance. With the Atlantic hurricane season running from June through November, insurers are cautious. Standard homeowners’ insurance policies often exclude windstorm or flood coverage unless specifically added. For mobile and manufactured homes, this risk increases. That’s why it’s crucial to understand how to reduce your mobile home insurance cost in Florida

Reasons you need targeted coverage:

  • Increased risk from hurricanes and high winds
  • Higher fire susceptibility (due to construction materials)
  • Ineligibility for traditional homeowners’ insurance policies
  • State-specific regulations and insurance pools (like Citizens)

Without the right protection, one storm can wipe out your home and financial security.

Best mobile home insurance companies in Florida 2025

1. Foremost (a Farmers Company) 

Rating: A+ (AM Best Rating)
Claim approval time: 1-2 weeks

Why it stands out: Foremost is widely regarded as the #1 choice for mobile home insurance, built specifically for this market. It offers strong coverage customization, including extended replacement cost, debris removal, and optional flood protection.

Pros And Cons

Pros

  • Covers older HUD-built homes
  • J.D. Power satisfaction score of 868/1,000
  • Covers both occupied and unoccupied homes
  • Optional replacement cost coverage
  • Convenient online quote tool makes it easy to estimate your policy in minutes
  • A-rated by AM Best, BBB, and Super Money for financial strength

Cons

  • No instant online quotes-agent-assisted only
  • Relatively high price.
  • Relatively high price.

2. State Farm 

Rating: A++ (AM Best Rating)
Claim approval time: 1-2 weeks

Why it stands out: State Farm is a major national insurer with customizable mobile home coverage, including replacement-cost options, earthquake endorsements, and strong agent-based support.

Pros And Cons

Pros

  • Highly rated (Impurify IQ score: 9.3/10; J.D. Power: 842)
  • Choice of deductibles and add-ons (cyber, earthquake)
  • Optional inflation protection
  • Multi-policy discounts
  • Good reputation for claims processing

Cons

  • No online mobile-home policy quotes
  • Limited mobile-home-specific discounts

3. Citizens Property Insurance

Rating: A (AM Best Rating)
Claim Approval Time: 24-72 hrs

Why it stands out: As Florida’s state-backed insurer of last resort, Citizens Property Insurance is ideal if you’ve been denied by private carriers or live in a high-risk hurricane area.

Eligibility: Only available if declined by two private insurers

Pros And Cons

Pros

  • Guaranteed coverage when other companies decline
  • Strong reinsurance support and statewide footprint
  • Available statewide, including high-risk zones
  • Compliant with Florida’s strict insurance regulations

Cons

  • Limited policy features, higher premiums, regulatory rates

4. American Modern Insurance

Rating: A+ (AM Best Rating)
Claim Approval Time: 0-6 days 

Why it stands out: American Modern offers flexible mobile home insurance for rental, occasional-use, and vacant homes. While online quotes aren’t available, agents can provide estimates. In case of total loss, you’ll receive the agreed-upon stated value of your home.

Pros And Cons

Pros

  • Custom-built policies for Florida mobile homes
  • Discounts for wind proofing and tie-down systems
  • Provides a fixed payout amount in the event of a total loss

Cons

  • Limited to Florida-no nationwide network
  • Home must meet modern anchoring/build standards
  • You have to work with an agent to obtain a quote

5. American Family

Rating: A (AM Best)
Claim Approval Time: 1-2 days

Why it stands out:

American Family may not be as heavily advertised as some big-name insurers, but it still holds a solid spot among the top eight home insurance providers in the U.S. by market share. One of its standout features is the variety of discounts offered, which can help lower your monthly insurance costs.

Another unique perk is its diminishing deductible program. When you start your policy, your deductible drops by $100 automatically, so a $1,000 deductible becomes $900 on day one. Each year you renew, it continues to decrease, as long as you stay within your policy’s limit.

Pros And Cons

Pros

  • Plenty of ways to qualify for discounts
  • Handy mobile app lets you track your claims step by step
  • Optional coverages available to customize your policy

Cons

  • Some customers have experienced slow claim processing

Cost of Mobile Home Insurance in Florida

Understanding the cost of insuring your mobile or manufactured home in Florida is key to budgeting effectively. Here’s a breakdown of what you’re likely to pay and how you can save money.

1. Average Premiums by Home Type & Coverage

  • Typical Range: $700–$1,500 per year, or about $58–$125 per month, depending on factors like age, value, location, and coverage limits.
  • With Wind Coverage: Average premiums with windstorm/hurricane protection range from $1,200 to $1,400 per year.
  • Without Wind Coverage: More budget-friendly options exist around $300–$500 per year, though this means no protection for storm-related damage.

2. Why Costs Are Rising

Florida homeowners are facing significant rate increases due to:

  • Frequent hurricane claims and extreme weather: More storms lead to more losses, and carriers pass rising costs to consumers.
  • Construction inflation: Replacement costs have risen sharply – tariffs on building materials can add $400 to premiums.
  • Insurance industry stress: Florida leads the nation in average home insurance costs, with turbulent local markets and carrier exits driving premiums up
  • Reinsurance market shifts: Insurers pay more to reinsurers, and those costs filter down to policyholders.

How to find The Best Mobile Home Insurance Company for You?

Selecting the right insurance policy for your mobile or manufactured home in Florida requires careful evaluation. Here’s how to make an informed decision as if you were working with an experienced agent:

1. Check Provider Ratings

Start with financial and customer service credibility:

  • AM Best: Look for ratings A- or higher, a key sign of claims-paying ability.
  • Better Business Bureau (BBB): Filter for insurers with a low complaint-to-policy ratio.
  • NAIC complaint index: A score below 1.0 suggests better-than-average complaint performance.

2. Compare Multiple Quotes

  • Compare at least three insurers: Foremost, State Farm, Progressive, and Citizens.
  • Use online marketplaces, independent agents, or insurer websites.
  • Pay attention not only to price, but differences in coverage limits, deductibles, and endorsements.

3. Ask About Hurricane Deductibles

Florida policies often have separate hurricane deductibles:

  • These can be high, usually 2%–5% of dwelling coverage.
  • Always confirm whether your deductible is “all-peril” or hurricane-specific to avoid surprise costs after a storm.

4. Choose Replacement Cost Over Actual Cash Value

  • Actual Cash Value (ACV) reimburses the depreciated value of items—cheaper upfront, but could leave you underinsured.
  • Replacement Cost Coverage (RCC) covers rebuilding or replacing with new materials, more expensive but much safer in major storms.

How to Buy Mobile Home Insurance Online in Florida (Simplified)

Buying mobile home insurance online in Florida is quick and easy when you follow these steps:

  1. Know What You Need: Estimate how much coverage you need for your home and belongings to ensure you’re fully protected.
  2. Get Online Quotes: Most Florida insurers let you request quotes online. Use comparison tools to check prices and coverage side by side.
  3. Complete the application: Once you’ve chosen the right coverage and price, complete the online application. Some providers may require a quick follow-up with an agent.

Final Thoughts

If you live in a mobile or manufactured home in Florida, home insurance isn’t just another bill; it’s your safety net. With the right provider, clear coverage, and proactive planning, you can protect your home, your belongings, and your peace of mind.

Foremost, American Modern, Assurant, and Citizens stand out as the most reliable names in the game. But every home is different. Compare quotes, ask questions, and never settle for the bare minimum.

Your home may be on wheels, but your protection shouldn’t be. Anchor your future with coverage that’s built to last.

Frequently Asked Questions

  • Is Getting Mobile Home Insurance in Florida Difficult?

    Getting coverage can be tricky, especially for older homes or those near the coast. While big insurers like State Farm and Geico don’t offer it in Florida, smaller providers usually fill the gap.

  • Average Mobile Home Insurance Cost in Florida (2025)?

    In Florida, mobile home insurance typically ranges from $894 to $1,349 per year, depending on factors like location, coverage limits, and home condition.

  • Why Might a Mobile Home Be Hard to Insure?

    There’s no exact rule, but certain factors can make getting insurance difficult. Older homes, especially those built before 1976, with outdated wiring or plumbing, often face challenges. Also, if you’ve filed multiple claims before, insurers might be reluctant to offer coverage.
