Who Is Considered A High-Risk Driver?

A high-risk driver is someone considered by auto insurance companies to have a greater likelihood of being involved in an accident or filing a claim. There are several ways a driver can be classified as high-risk, often resulting in higher premiums.

Common reasons include speeding tickets, which indicate risky behavior, and distracted driving, such as texting or using a phone while driving. Both suggest a lack of attention to the road, increasing the likelihood of accidents. Reckless driving, which includes dangerous actions like running red lights or excessive speeding, further raises the risk profile.

One of the most severe offenses is Driving Under the Influence (DUI). A DUI conviction significantly increases a driverâ€™s risk rating, as it shows a disregard for road safety and legal regulations. Additionally, teen drivers and those with bad credit can fall into the high-risk category. Teenagers are inexperienced, while drivers with bad credit are statistically more likely to file claims. All of these factors lead to higher insurance rates and fewer coverage options for high-risk drivers.
