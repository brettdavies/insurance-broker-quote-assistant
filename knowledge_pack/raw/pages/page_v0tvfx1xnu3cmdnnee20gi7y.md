## Car insurance and earthquake damage
Your car is only covered for earthquake damage if you have [**comprehensive coverage**](https://www.thezebra.com/auto-insurance/insurance-guide/comprehensive-insurance/). This part of your auto policy pays for damage from events beyond your control, like natural disasters, falling objects, or theft. However, **comprehensive isnâ€™t included automatically**.
Insurers can make exceptions for rare or catastrophic events, especially in high-risk areas, so always check your policy details to be sure.
* * *
## Can you add coverage right before an earthquake?
Unfortunately, no. **You canâ€™t wait until the shaking starts to get protection,** and insurers are prepared for that. Hereâ€™s why:
  * **Binding restrictions:** When a disaster is looming, insurance companies may temporarily stop allowing new policies or coverage changes. This â€œpauseâ€ is called a binding restriction, and it prevents last-minute updates until the risk has passed.
  * **No warning:** Unlike hurricanes or snowstorms, earthquakes strike without notice. The USGS confirms thereâ€™s no reliable way to predict them, so thereâ€™s no forecast window to act on.[[1]](https://www.thezebra.com/auto-insurance/coverage/natural-disasters/does-car-insurance-cover-earthquake-damage/#footnote-1)


**Bottom line:** If you want your car covered for earthquake damage, youâ€™ll need to add comprehensive coverage in advance.
#### Am I covered everywhere?
Earthquake damage coverage is included in the "weather-related incidents" bucket. Unless your policy has a unique stipulation, this coverage should be consistent across all major car insurance companies, in every US state. For example, if you move from [Texas](https://www.thezebra.com/auto-insurance/texas-car-insurance/) to [California](https://www.thezebra.com/auto-insurance/california-car-insurance/), where earthquakes occur more often, you would maintain earthquake coverage from your comprehensive insurance.

## Earthquakes and car insurance: FAQs
Does car insurance go up for natural disasters? Chevron down icon
It can. Anytime you make a claim, your premium could increase at the next renewal. Insurers also consider the overall risk in a given area, and frequent natural disasters can drive up rates over time.
Is earthquake excluded from insurance? Chevron down icon
With car insurance, if you have comprehensive coverage, earthquake damage is usually covered under comprehensive coverage. However, homeowners insurance is different and requires a specific policy endorsement.
Is my car covered for natural disasters? Chevron down icon
If you have comprehensive coverage, this optional part of your auto policy pays for damage caused by events outside your control, like earthquakes, wildfires, hail, or flooding. Itâ€™s not included in basic liability coverage, so you must add it separately.
Is your car covered by a renters or homeowners policy? Chevron down icon
No. Renters and homeowners insurance cover your personal belongings and your home, but not your car. To protect your vehicle from damage (including from natural disasters), youâ€™ll need auto insurance with comprehensive coverage.
