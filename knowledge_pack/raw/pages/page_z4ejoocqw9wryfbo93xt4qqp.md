##  Geico vs. State Farm: Coverage Options
State Farm ranks No. 2 in our study of the[ best renters insurance companies](https://www.usnews.com/insurance/renters-insurance) and takes the first spot in our ranking of the[ cheapest renters insurance companies](https://www.usnews.com/insurance/renters-insurance/cheapest-renters-insurance-companies). However, Geico isn't rated in either study.
According to our study, State Farm's average monthly premium is $13.04, while Geico's website advertises an average rate starting at $12 per month. Both insurers offer a multi-policy discount, but Geico also offers a military discount. State Farm, on the other hand, offers a discount for home alarm protection and being claims-free.
Both insurers offer standard renters insurance policies that include [coverage](https://www.usnews.com/insurance/renters-insurance/what-does-renters-insurance-cover) for personal property, liability, additional living expenses, and guest medical expenses.
Additionally, Geico offers optional coverages that may include identity theft protection, replacement cost coverage, earthquake coverage, and extra coverage for high-value items such as jewelry. State Farm offers additional coverage, including earthquake coverage, cyber event coverage, identity restoration, sewer or drain backup coverage, and fraud loss coverage.
State Farm writes its own renters insurance policies, while Geico sells policies through third-party companies. However, both companies offer mobile apps that allow customers to pay bills and file claims. Geico is available in all 50 states and Washington, D.C., while State Farm is available in 47 states and Washington, D.C., excluding California, Massachusetts, and Rhode Island.
### Do Geico and State Farm Cover Bedbugs?
Neither company covers bedbug damage or infestation in a standard renters insurance policy. Most insurers don't cover bedbugs or pest infestations because they are considered preventable with standard home maintenance.
### Do Geico and State Farm Cover Water Damage?
Both insurers cover accidental water damage caused by internal sources, such as broken or frozen plumbing, malfunctioning appliances, or water that seeps in from neighboring units. However, neither company covers water damage caused by flooding.
### Do Geico and State Farm Cover Flood Damage?
Renters insurance typically doesn't cover flood damage, and neither does Geico or State Farm. Flood damage is generally defined as water that comes from outside or underground sources.
### Do Geico and State Farm Cover Mold Damage?
Mold damage isn't covered by either company unless it occurs due to a covered peril, such as a burst pipe. Mold damage is considered preventable with standard maintenance.
### Do Geico and State Farm Cover Property Damage?
A standard renters insurance policy covers property damage caused by a covered peril, up to your policy limits. Personal liability is also included if you accidentally damage someone else's property.
### Do Geico and State Farm Cover Fire Damage?
Geico and State Farm both include fire damage as a covered peril. This includes damage or loss of personal property caused by fire or smoke, as well as additional living expenses if you're displaced from your home.
### Do Geico and State Farm Cover Car Theft?
Car theft isn't covered by a standard renters insurance policy, even if it occurs on the rental property. However, both companies cover personal belongings stolen from your vehicle, regardless of its location.
### Do Geico and State Farm Cover Moving Costs?
Neither company covers moving costs. However, if you're forced to temporarily relocate due to a covered peril, the additional living expenses portion of your policy may help cover moving costs.
### Do Geico and State Farm Cover Temporary Housing?
Both Geico and State Farm cover temporary housing caused by a covered peril. This is covered under the additional living expenses portion of your policy and will help pay for restaurant and hotel bills if you're displaced from your home.
### Do Geico and State Farm Offer Renters Insurance Bundling?
Also known as a multi-policy discount, insurance bundling is a common way to help you save on your premium. Both insurers offer discounts for [bundling your renters insurance policy](https://www.usnews.com/insurance/renters-insurance/how-to-bundle-renters-and-auto-insurance) with another insurance policy.
