Car insurance is affordable in Washington. The average car insurance rate in [Washington](https://www.thezebra.com/auto-insurance/washington-car-insurance/) is $1,160 per year — 18.7% less than the [national average](https://www.thezebra.com/auto-insurance/how-to-shop/average-auto-insurance/).

##  Average Auto Insurance Prices in Washington by Age
It's a common phenomenon: as you [age](https://www.thezebra.com/auto-insurance/driver/age/), your auto insurance prices shift. Generally, car insurance is more expensive for young drivers, as car insurers see inexperienced motorists as riskier.
In Washington, auto insurance costs $6,301 each year for a [16-year-old](https://www.thezebra.com/auto-insurance/driver/age/car-insurance-16-year-olds/) driver, but just $999 per year for a driver in their [50s](https://www.thezebra.com/auto-insurance/discounts/senior-citizen-discount/).
###### WASHINGTON CAR INSURANCE RATES BY AGE BRACKET

18 | $4,157.56  
19 | $2,716.21  
20s | $1,511.22  
30s | $1,112.68  
40s | $1,057.87  
50s | $998.70  
60s | $1,024.50  
70s | $1,283.54  
If you're shopping for car insurance for a teen driver, Washington is the 27th cheapest state in which to buy auto insurance for a 16-year-old.

##  Average Car Insurance Rates in Washington by Credit
Car insurers reference credit history as a prediction of a potential customer's insurability. Historical data indicate motorists who carry exceptional credit are more dependable auto insurance customers: they less frequently file claims covered by insurance companies.

##  Average Car Insurance Costs for Bad Drivers in Washington
If you've been found at fault in a car accident or convicted of an [on-road offense](https://www.thezebra.com/auto-insurance/driver/other-factors/cheap-car-insurance-high-risk-drivers/), your auto insurance costs are going to climb. The more violations you amass, the more expensive your auto insurance gets.
In Washington, your first minor at-fault collision results in a penalty of $326.07 per year in auto insurance premiums. A DUI — thought of as one of the most severe driving violations — will raise your annual car insurance costs in Washington by an average of $520.40.

##  Median Auto Insurance Prices by Coverage Amount in Washington
How much you pay each month for auto insurance is dependent on the [tier of coverage](https://www.thezebra.com/auto-insurance/coverage/) you elect to carry. Liability-only coverage generally costs less, while lower-deductible comprehensive coverage costs more. In Washington, the cost gap between liability-only coverage and full coverage with a $500 deductible is $507.
