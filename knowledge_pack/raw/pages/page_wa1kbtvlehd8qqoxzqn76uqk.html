Compare Cheap Auto Insurance in New York

Progressive and NYCM have some of the cheapest rates in New York.

Get quotes from providers in your area

Find out if you're overpaying for auto insurance.
Aliza Vigderman
Senior Writer & Editor
Maya Afilalo

The average cost of full coverage auto insurance in New York state is $2,279, which is similar to the national average of $2,399. However, the average cost of minimum coverage is $1,343, more than double the national average of $635. This is partly due to the state’s no-fault insurance laws, which require drivers to file claims with their own insurer, regardless of fault.

Insurance costs tend to be higher in no-fault states, but some companies are cheaper than others. We’ve rounded up the most affordable options in the Empire State.

Overview

  • The average cost of full coverage auto insurance in New York is $2,279 annually. Minimum coverage averages $1,343 annually.
  • Progressive is cheapest in several categories, including full and minimum coverage and for drivers with a speeding ticket or DUI.
  • Regional insurer New York Central Mutual (NYCM) also has cheap rates, including for drivers with low credit, an at-fault accident, and households with teen drivers.
  • New York City, being densely populated, has higher-than-average premiums, with full coverage insurance costs of $3,675 annually.

Cheapest Car Insurance in New York for Full Coverage: Progressive

Progressive offers the most affordable full coverage auto insurance in New York at $1,288 annually ($107 per month). It’s a top choice for drivers seeking the lowest premiums without sacrificing essential coverage. Progressive is also known for competitive pricing across different driving scenarios, such as after a driver’s speeding ticket or DUI.

NYCM ($1,447 annually) and USAA ($1,652 annually) also offer cheap rates, with higher ratings for customer satisfaction. Note that USAA is only available to military members, veterans, and their families.

How to Find the Cheapest Rates in New York

Here are some tips to ensure you find the most affordable auto insurance in New York:

  • Compare Quotes: Always get quotes from multiple providers to see which offers the best rates for your specific situation. Get quotes from at least three providers for the same coverage.
  • Ask About Discounts: Many providers offer discounts for safe driving, bundling home and auto insurance, and having anti-theft devices. Speak to a representative from your provider and make sure all discounts are being applied.
  • Consider Increasing Your Deductible: A higher deductible can lower your premium, but be sure it fits within your budget in case of a claim. In general, a $1,000 deductible is a good middle ground, although ultimately, the decision will depend upon your personal finances.
  • Drive Safely: Participating in telematics programs or staying accident-free can lead to lower premiums over time. Maintaining a clean driving record not only helps with your insurance rates but also promotes overall safety on the road.

Minimum Car Insurance Requirements in New York

New York requires the following minimum auto insurance coverage for all drivers:

  • Bodily injury liability: $25,000 per person/$50,000 per accident
  • Property damage liability: $10,000 per accident
  • Uninsured/underinsured motorist: $25,000 per person/$50,000 per accident
  • Personal injury protection: $50,000 per person

Your liability coverage must be in effect until your vehicle registration expires, even if you don’t use your vehicle. It also must be New York State insurance coverage.4

Frequently Asked Questions

To lower your car insurance in New York, start by comparing quotes from multiple providers. Insurance rates can vary significantly, so getting several quotes will help you find the best deal for your coverage needs. Additionally, consider raising your deductible: This can lower your monthly premium. Just be sure you’re comfortable with the higher out-of-pocket costs in the event that you need to file a claim. Another way to reduce costs is by asking your insurance provider about available discounts, such as those for safe driving, bundling policies, installing anti-theft devices, or even for taking a defensive driving course. Finally, consider adjusting your coverage to reflect your current needs. For example, if your car is older, fully paid off, and has a lower value, you might drop collision and/or comprehensive coverage.

  • In New York, you cannot go without insurance on a registered vehicle for any length of time without facing potential penalties. However, the severity of the consequences depends on the duration of the lapse:
  • 1-90 days: You can pay a civil penalty to avoid license and registration suspension. The daily rates are:
    • $8 per day for days 1-30
    • $10 per day for days 31-60
    • $12 per day for days 61-90
  • 91 days or more: Your license and registration will be automatically suspended for a period equal to the duration of the lapse. You’ll also need to pay a $50 suspension termination fee to reinstate your license.
