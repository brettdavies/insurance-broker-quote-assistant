Progressive Car Insurance Summary

  • Average Yearly Premium:$1,820
  • Difference from National Average: -$192
  • Collision Protection: Included
  • Liability Protection: Available
  • Multi-Policy Savings: Offered
  • Multiple Vehicle Discount: Eligible
  • Safe Driver Reward: Applicable
  • Customer Review Rating: 4.17
  • Digital Quote & Purchase Option: Accessible Online
  • Coverage Reach: All 50 States plus Washington, D.C

Progressive’s average premium falls below the national benchmark, and the insurer earned strong overall ratings from customers in our recent survey.

Respondents rated the company positively across key performance areas, including customer service, claims handling, and the likelihood of recommending Progressive or renewing their policy.

Although Progressive offers fewer optional coverages than some competitors, it provides unique choices that aren’t commonly available elsewhere, such as rideshare protection and coverage for custom vehicle equipment.

Additionally, Progressive stands out with a wider range of discounts compared to most insurers in our review, giving policyholders more opportunities to save

Is Progressive Insurance a Good Car Insurance?

Progressive got good reviews for how it handles claims. Most people in our survey said they were happy with the company’s customer service. One person said, “They resolved the claim promptly,” and another mentioned, “I was kept well informed of the status of my claim.”

We also checked the NAIC Complaint Index and found that Progressive gets fewer complaints than the average insurance company.

Progressive offers standard auto insurance coverages like liability, collision, and comprehensive. It also provides optional features such as roadside assistance, rental car reimbursement, and accident forgiveness.

The company’s usage-based program, Snapshot, lets drivers earn personalized rates based on their driving behavior.

Founded in 1937 as Progressive Mutual Insurance Company, the Ohio-based insurer now serves customers across all 50 states and Washington, D.C.

Policies can be purchased online, over the phone, or through a local insurance agent.

How Much Does Progressive Car Insurance Cost

Progressive’s average yearly premium is $1,820, which is $192 less than the national average of $2,012 for a 12-month policy. Its rates fall around the middle compared to other major insurers in our review.

For most driver profiles, such as young adults or those with a speeding ticket, Progressive’s average rates tend to be below the national average.

Like many auto insurance providers, Progressive offers a range of discounts to help reduce premiums.

It has more discount options than most companies in our 10-insurer comparison, including savings for young drivers through its teen driver, good student, and distant student discounts.

Keep in mind that our review uses sample rates for comparison. Actual premiums vary based on factors like your location, coverage options, and the type of vehicle you drive, so your individual costs may differ.

Progressive Insurance Rates for Teen Drivers

Progressive’s sample yearly rates for teenage drivers are higher than the national average, $2,569 more for females and $2,558 more for males. This difference exists because insurers generally see teens as higher-risk drivers due to their limited experience and greater likelihood of accidents.

  • National Average: $6,475

Progressive Rates for Young Adult Drivers

Car insurance premiums typically decrease as drivers age, as long as they keep a clean driving record. In our review, rates for young adults, single 25-year-old drivers, are much lower than those for teens.

Progressive’s average rates for this group are also slightly below the national average.

25-Year-Old Female:

  • Progressive: $2,305
  • National Average: $2,309

25-Year-Old Male:

  • Progressive: $2,332
  • National Average: $2,459

Progressive Rates for Adult Drivers

In our analysis, adult drivers were represented by married 40-year-olds. This group generally pays lower premiums than young adults, and much less than teen drivers.

Progressive’s average annual rate for 40-year-old women is $183 below the national average, while rates for men are $240 lower than average.

40-Year-Old Female:

  • Progressive: $1,710
  • National Average: $1,893

40-Year-Old Male:

  • Progressive: $1,682
  • National Average: $1,922

Progressive Rates for Senior Drivers

For married 60-year-olds, the senior group in our rate comparison, Progressive’s average premiums are lower than the national average.

Female drivers at this age pay about $294 less, while male drivers pay around $304 less than the national average. Overall, senior drivers enjoy the lowest average rates of any age group in our study.

60-Year-Old Female:

  • Progressive: $1,430
  • National Average: $1,724

60-Year-Old Male:

  • Progressive: $1,461
  • National Average: $1,765

How Much is Minimum Coverage with Progressive?

Most states require drivers to carry a minimum level of car insurance to stay legal on the road. This usually includes liability coverage and, depending on the state, a few additional protections.

Our research shows that the average cost of minimum coverage nationwide is $780 per year, while Progressive’s average rate is slightly lower at $771.

However, meeting only the state’s minimum requirements may not be enough. It’s important to consider how much coverage you truly need to ensure proper financial protection.

Minimum Coverage

  • Progressive: $771
  • National Average: $780

How Much is Full Coverage with Progressive?

Full coverage car insurance generally includes your state’s required minimum coverage along with collision and comprehensive insurance, both commonly required for financed or leased vehicles.

Progressive’s average annual rate for full coverage is about $150 less than the national average.

Full Coverage

  • Progressive: $2,233
  • National Average: $2,386

Minimum coverage only meets state-required liability and other mandatory protections.

Full coverage, on the other hand, includes the following:

  • $100,000/$300,000 bodily injury liability,
  • $100,000 property damage liability,
  • comprehensive and collision with a $500 deductible,
  • $100,000/$300,000 uninsured/underinsured motorist coverage (UIM),
  • Any additional state-required protection.

It’s worth noting that some states don’t permit insurers to use gender when setting car insurance rates, and others limit the use of credit information when determining premiums.

Progressive Car Insurance Discounts

Progressive provides a variety of discounts to help lower your car insurance premiums if you qualify. Some of the most common savings opportunities include:

  • Multi-policy discount
  • Multi-car discount
  • Continuous insurance discount
  • Snapshot (safe driver) discount
  • Teen driver discount
  • Good student discount
  • Distant student discount
  • Homeowner discount
  • Online quote discount
  • Sign online discount
  • Paperless discount
  • Pay in full discount
  • Automatic payment discount

These discounts can add up, making it easier to reduce your overall insurance costs while maintaining strong coverage.
