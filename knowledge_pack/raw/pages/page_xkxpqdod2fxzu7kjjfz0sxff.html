    If you’re unable to purchase homeowners insurance in the state, consider applying to the California FAIR Plan, the state’s insurer of last resort. It provides basic fire insurance for high-risk properties. To qualify for the FAIR Plan, you must have been rejected by at least two insurers. Keep in mind that FAIR plans are typically more expensive than policies in the standard market.

    What Is a FAIR Home Insurance Plan?

    Earthquakes

    A standard homeowners insurance policy doesn’t cover damage caused by earthquakes. If you live in an area prone to this disaster, consider purchasing an earthquake insurance policy from a private insurer or the California Earthquake Authority.

    Flooding

    Flood damage isn’t covered by a standard homeowners insurance policy. If you live in a flood-prone area, consider purchasing flood insurance from a private insurer or the National Flood Insurance Program (NFIP).

    Wildfires

    Damage caused by wildfires is typically covered by a standard homeowners insurance policy. However, coverage varies by insurer, so it’s essential to review your policy details carefully.

    The California Department of Insurance regulates the insurance industry in the state. Its goal is to protect consumers from excessive and unfair insurance rates and enforce state insurance laws. Californians can file complaints and review agent and broker information along with insurance laws and regulations through the department’s website.

    Homeowners insurance isn’t legally mandated in the state, but most mortgage lenders will require you to show proof of a policy. Although not legally required, homeowners insurance is a wise investment to protect your assets if disaster strikes.

    A standard homeowners insurance policy in California protects your home from specific perils, such as wind, lightning, fire, hail, theft, and vandalism. A typical policy covers your dwelling, other structures, personal property, liability, medical payments to others, and loss of use.

    Natural disasters, specifically wildfires, are causing home insurance companies to drop customers in the state. The 2025 Los Angeles fires resulted in an initial loss estimate of $40 billion. Due to massive losses, the inability to include reinsurance costs in premiums, and the inability to increase rates without prior approval, insurers are nonrenewing policies and leaving the state.

    The cost of homeowners insurance is expensive in the state due to an increase in natural disaster risks, namely wildfires. Massive losses have led to increased premiums, as insurers prepare for the possibility of paying out large claims.

    According to the Insurance Information Institute, to save money on your homeowners insurance, you can increase your deductible, bundle your home and auto policies, and prepare your home against natural disasters, such as reinforcing your roof.

        To build our ratings, we researched 16 homeowners insurance companies and insurance agencies and analyzed data from a consumer survey with 1,125 responses, product features collected from company websites, and data on insurance costs provided by Quadrant Information Services. U.S. News worked with Quadrant to analyze a report of publicly available insurance rates using 34,595 ZIP codes across all 50 states and Washington, D.C. for the companies in our rating of the best homeowners insurance companies that operate in that state. The insurance rates in our study are based on averages of sample rates for a 38-year-old and 56-year-old married applicant household with a 720 FICO credit score. The rates were obtained for an HO3 policy covering a wood-frame house with 10% Other Structures Coverage, 20% Loss of Use Coverage, 50% Personal Property Coverage, and a guest medical limit of $5,000.
