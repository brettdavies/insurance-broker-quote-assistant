Introduction to Car Insurance in New York

New York has strict car insurance laws to protect drivers and pedestrians. The state requires minimum coverage levels, including $25,000/$50,000 for bodily injury and $10,000 for property damage. Personal Injury Protection (PIP) must also reach $50,000.

Since 2023, spousal liability rules add another layer. Policies must now cover spouses unless waived in writing. This change reflects New York’s focus on family protection.

Urban areas like Brooklyn see higher premiums due to traffic density. Suburban zones like Medford often pay less. Claims frequency drops in rural parts of Long Island.

Uninsured motorist (UM) coverage is critical here. Over 6% of New York drivers lack insurance. UM safeguards against accidents with these motorists.

Knowing these rules helps drivers tailor policies. Balancing state minimums with personal needs ensures both compliance and safety.
