## Is State Farm a good company?
State Farm is great if you’re looking for affordable insurance from one of the [largest insurance companies](https://www.lendingtree.com/car-insurance/largest-car-insurance-companies/) in the U.S. Its car, home and renters insurance rates are among the cheapest available. 
State Farm also offers a good selection of coverage and discount options, though not as many as some other top insurance companies.
Customers seem happy with State Farm, too, based on the company’s J.D. Power satisfaction and NAIC complaint ratings, though some competitors fare better. In LendingTree’s review of insurance companies, State Farm received a rating of 4.5 out of 5.0 for auto insurance and 4.2 out of 5.0 for home insurance.

Car insurance from State Farm costs $124 a month, on average, if you buy full coverage. If you buy only liability coverage from State Farm, the average cost is about $50 a month.
These average rates make State Farm the cheapest car insurance company that serves all customers nationwide.

Its [average rate for full coverage insurance](https://www.lendingtree.com/car-insurance/cheap-full-coverage-car-insurance/) is 25% cheaper than the national average of $164 a month.

### How much is State Farm for drivers with a ticket, accident or DUI?
Among top insurers, State Farm has the **cheapest car insurance for most drivers with a speeding ticket or at-fault accident** on their records.
However, its rates for drivers with a DUI (driving under the influence) conviction are among the highest for nationwide companies.
  * If you have a speeding ticket on your record, State Farm car insurance costs about $135 a month.
  * Drivers with an at-fault accident on their record pay $154 a month for car insurance from State Farm.
  * State Farm’s average rate for drivers with a DUI is $367 a month.

### How much is State Farm insurance for teens?
State Farm car insurance for teen drivers costs around **$151 a month for liability coverage**. Teens pay an average of $344 a month for full-coverage car insurance with State Farm.
These make State Farm the cheapest large [car insurance company for most teen drivers](https://www.lendingtree.com/car-insurance/cheap-car-insurance-for-teens/), for both liability and full coverage.
State Farm’s average liability car insurance rate for 18-year-olds is 26% lower than the national average. The company’s average full-coverage rate for 18-year-olds is 29% lower than the national average.

### What does State Farm car insurance cover?
State Farm car insurance provides all of the usual coverage options, along with rideshare insurance and travel expense coverage.
A standard State Farm car insurance policy comes with these coverages:
  * Liability
  * Collision
  * Comprehensive
  * Uninsured motorist
  * Medical payments and/or personal injury protection


State Farm also offers these additional car insurance coverage options:
  * [Rideshare insurance](https://www.lendingtree.com/car-insurance/rideshare-insurance/) for drivers who use a personal vehicle for rideshare companies like Uber and Lyft
  * Travel expense coverage for vehicles damaged more than 50 miles from home


Unlike many other car insurance companies, State Farm **doesn’t offer** accident forgiveness, gap insurance or mechanical breakdown coverage.

### What does State Farm home insurance cover?
State Farm offers all of the usual home insurance coverage and upgrade options.
Its standard home insurance policies include liability and guest medical coverage. They also include loss of use, which covers temporary living expenses after a covered disaster.
With State Farm, you can upgrade your home insurance policy to include replacement cost coverage for your belongings. This means you’ll get higher payouts for stolen or damaged items.

## What does State Farm renters insurance cover?
State Farm’s standard renters insurance policy covers your belongings, additional living expenses, personal liability and medical payments to others who are injured on your property.
You can also buy add-on coverage for a State Farm renters insurance policy, including:
  * Pet medical insurance
  * Personal liability umbrella coverage
  * Identity restoration coverage
  * Earthquake insurance


## Methodology
LendingTree obtains rates from insurance company filings reported to Quadrant Information Services.
Car insurance rates are based on an analysis of quotes for sample drivers from every ZIP code. Home insurance rates are based on quotes for home insurance in all 50 states plus Washington, D.C.
Our sample driver for car insurance quotes is a 30-year-old man with good credit and no tickets, accidents or DUIs. The sample vehicle is a 2015 Honda Civic EX. Our sample teen driver is an 18-year-old male with a clean driving record.
Full-coverage car insurance policies include:
  * Bodily injury liability: $50,000 per person, $100,000 per accident
  * Property damage liability: $25,000
  * Uninsured motorist: $50,000 per person, $100,000 per accident, where required by law
  * Personal injury protection: Minimum limits, where required by law
  * Collision: $500 deductible
  * Comprehensive: $500 deductible


Homeowners policies include:
  * Dwelling coverage: $400,000
  * Other structures: $40,000
  * Personal property: $200,000
  * Personal liability: $100,000
  * Medical payments: $5,000
  * Deductible: $1,000


Renters policies include:
  * Personal property coverage: $20,000
  * Deductible: $500
  * Personal liability: $100,000
  * Guest medical protection: $1,000
  * Loss of use: $9,000
