Established on March 10, 1937, in Cleveland, Ohio, by Joseph Lewis and Jack Green, Progressive Mutual Insurance Company sought to transform auto insurance. As pioneers of the first drive-in claims location, the company emphasized innovation.

In 1956, Progressive Casualty Insurance Company was founded as the inaugural nonstandard insurance underwriter, unveiling the Safe Driver Plan. By 1964, Progressive introduced a payment plan for nonstandard auto policies in Ohio and broadened coverage to include motorcycle insurance policies, establishing benchmarks for an evolving insurance industry.

In 1971, Progressive turned into a stock insurance company with an initial public offering (IPO). That same year, the company entered its commercial insurance business as well as began offering coverage for RVs.

In 1974, Progressive moved to its current headquarters in Mayfield Village, Ohio, where it would continue to set industry standards for decades. This includes:

  • Being the first auto company to publish its loss reserve report.
  • Streamlining data through the Progressive On-line Transaction Enquiry and Update System (PROTEUS).
  • Creating immediate claim response for 24/7 service for policyholders along with the option of purchasing and comparing coverage over the phone.
  • Progressive launched the first auto insurance website which evolved into real-time quotes and enrollments. This would be expanded over time to other product lines.
  • Several technological innovations including apps, the popular Snapshot discount, and integration with Google Home among proprietary tools to help policyholders.

 

Today, the company needs no introduction. Progressive is the third-largest property and casualty insurance company in the United States known for its extensive product offerings along with illustrious resources and a quirky marketing campaign.

Progressive’s Policy Options

There isn’t much that the Progressive Corporation doesn’t cover when it comes to protecting against property and casualty losses. If you’re looking for an extensive product listing with personalized coverage, Progressive has you covered:

Progressive Auto Insurance

Whether you’re looking to just stay legal with liability coverage or enroll in full coverage to better protect your vehicle, auto insurance from Progressive has it all. You can even find other policies such as gap insurance, roadside assistance, classic car coverage, and more for a highly personalized approach.

Progressive Home Insurance

Home insurance covers your property and your finances. There are different property types and Progressive has many options to meet your needs. You can even shop among different policy types and riders/endorsements to customize your protection.

Progressive Renters Insurance

Renters insurance by Progressive is the perfect solution for anyone looking to protect their personal belongings from loss. You can customize your coverage to protect against losses due to liability and coverage that can help you with eating and lodging should your home become uninhabitable after a major loss.

Progressive Motorcycle Insurance

From ATVs to motorcycles, there are several different types of bikes available. You deserve coverage that fits your needs and Progressive motorcycle insurance delivers. There are discounts for association members, personalized coverage options, roadside assistance, and more available.

Progressive Boat Insurance

More than 1 million watercraft are protected by Progressive ranging from fishing boats to extensive sailboats and everything in between. Towing, salvage, fuel spill, and other coverages are available in addition to protecting your watercraft.

Progressive RV Insurance

Not only is Progressive RV insurance affordable, but it’s flexible for any type of recreational vehicle. This includes Class A, B, and C motorhomes, campers, and even cargo and horse trailers. Coverage in case of emergencies is also available.

Progressive Commercial Insurance

Progressive commercial auto insurance has been a mainstay since 1971, however, the company has expanded into other business coverages since. The list is extensive, which is a good thing. No matter what type of business you have, the industry you’re involved in, or losses you need to protect against, Progressive business insurance has a policy for your company.

Progressive Umbrella Insurance

If you have a home, rent a home, or own an automobile, you could find these assets and your finances in jeopardy in the event of a lawsuit. Progressive umbrella insurance protects you through supplemental coverage against major losses that other policies may not have high enough limits for.

Does Progressive Have a Grace Period?

Yes, Progressive has a grace period that changes for drivers from state to state. In Texas, you’ll have 7 to 30 days if you’ve missed a payment or if you are transferring coverage to a new vehicle.

However, you should note that this may result in higher premiums later, you’ll need to pay a late fee, and you could be non-renewed once your policy expires should it become a habit rather than an exception.
