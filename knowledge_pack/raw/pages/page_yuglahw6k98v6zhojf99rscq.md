### Does California Allow Non-Owner Car Insurance?
California permits non-owner car insurance for those who drive but don't own a car. If you must file an SR-22, you can still purchase this affordable coverage to meet state minimum requirements.
![carInsurance icon](https://res.cloudinary.com/moneygeek/image/upload/v1585171420/assets/components/icon/car_insurance_aud02m.svg)
CALIFORNIA MINIMUM INSURANCE REQUIREMENTS
[California's minimum coverage requirements](https://www.moneygeek.com/insurance/auto/state-minimum-car-insurance-requirements/) apply to non-owner car insurance and include:
  * $15,000 in bodily injury liability per person
  * $30,000 in bodily injury liability per accident
  * $5,000 in property damage liability per accident


Sometimes, the state minimums might not fully cover your needs. Consider [how much coverage you need](https://www.moneygeek.com/insurance/auto/how-much-car-insurance-do-i-need/) before purchasing a policy.
### Should You Get Non-Owner Car Insurance in California?
California drivers who frequently drive but don’t own a vehicle can opt for non-owner car insurance. It covers injuries or property damage you cause and is ideal for renters or car-sharing users. This affordable option meets state minimum coverage requirements.
![insurance2 icon](https://res.cloudinary.com/moneygeek/image/upload/v1585171420/assets/components/icon/insurance_2_iixd7m.svg)

WHAT IS SR-22 INSURANCE?
An [SR-22](https://www.moneygeek.com/insurance/auto/sr22-car-insurance-california/) is a form that insurance companies send to your state's DMV to show you have the required minimum coverage. Often called a Certificate of Financial Responsibility, the SR-22 is necessary for some drivers to keep or regain their driving privileges after incidents like a DUI or driving without insurance.
