# Financial goals by age: a decade-by-decade approach
Written by [State Farm® Editorial Team ](https://www.statefarm.com/simple-insights/editorial-team-and-process)
Help meet your financial goals and stay on track for retirement with these age-based saving tips. 
The specifics of your [financial plan](https://www.statefarm.com/simple-insights/financial/follow-these-steps-to-achieve-financial-wellness) and goals may not resemble anyone else's. Maybe you want to trek the globe after you retire while your neighbor plans to dive into full-time volunteer work. Regardless, some shared steps can help get you on track for your future.
A common financial regret for many people is not [saving early enough for retirement](https://www.statefarm.com/simple-insights/retirement/retirement-advice-for-millennials-and-gen-x). Here's a breakdown of some money goals by age.
## Financial goals for your 20s  

  * Establish a consistent income and [good spending habits](https://www.statefarm.com/simple-insights/financial/the-psychology-of-spending-habits-and-mistakes).
  * Sustain (and live with) a [budget](https://www.statefarm.com/simple-insights/financial/budget-breakdown).
  * [Eliminate debt](https://www.statefarm.com/simple-insights/financial/how-to-get-out-of-debt) (credit cards or loans), beginning with debt that has the highest interest.
  * Enroll in [health insurance](https://www.statefarm.com/insurance/health).
  * Start investing in a 401(k) and remember to contribute enough to receive the [maximum employer match](https://www.statefarm.com/simple-insights/financial/how-employer-401k-matching-works).
  * Begin to develop an [emergency fund](https://www.statefarm.com/simple-insights/financial/build-an-emergency-fund) to help prepare for any unexpected financial situations.
  * Open savings accounts for big expenses and investment accounts for retirement.


**Retirement savings for your 20s:** Some experts recommend saving funds equivalent to one year of your annual salary or saving 15 to 25% of your pay as you receive it.
## Financial goals for your 30s
  * Fill your emergency fund with [three to six months' salary](https://www.statefarm.com/simple-insights/financial/building-an-emergency-fund-calculate-how-much-to-save).
  * Eliminate additional non-mortgage debt, including [student loans](https://www.statefarm.com/simple-insights/financial/should-you-refinance-student-loans).
  * Work on building up a [good credit score](https://www.statefarm.com/simple-insights/financial/what-is-a-good-credit-score) in the 700s or higher, if possible.
  * Create a [will](https://www.statefarm.com/simple-insights/family/what-is-a-will-and-why-do-you-need-one) and get [life insurance](https://www.statefarm.com/insurance/life).
  * Maximize the match on [employer-sponsored retirement plans](https://www.statefarm.com/simple-insights/financial/how-employer-401k-matching-works).
  * Put [20% down on a home](https://www.statefarm.com/simple-insights/residence/how-to-save-for-a-down-payment-on-a-house).
  * Build kids' [education savings funds](https://www.statefarm.com/simple-insights/college/how-a-529-plan-works).


**Retirement savings by age 30:** Some experts recommend having two to three times your annual salary saved.
## Financial goals for your 40s
  * Maximize personal contributions to [retirement accounts](https://www.statefarm.com/simple-insights/retirement/steps-you-can-take-now-to-save-for-retirement)[,](https://www.statefarm.com/simple-insights/retirement/steps-you-can-take-now-to-save-for-retirement) [such as Roth and Traditional IRAs](https://www.statefarm.com/finances/retirement-plans-iras/individual-retirement-accounts).
  * Increase income (negotiate a raise or add an income stream).
  * Consider additional investments to help diversify your portfolio.
  * Pay extra on your mortgage, and [consider paying it off early](https://www.statefarm.com/simple-insights/residence/pay-off-mortgage-early).
  * Discuss your [parents' finances](https://www.statefarm.com/simple-insights/financial/how-to-talk-about-money-with-aging-parents) and [estate plans](https://www.statefarm.com/simple-insights/estate/does-everyone-need-estate-planning).
  * Map out [college payment options](https://www.statefarm.com/simple-insights/college/give-the-gift-of-education) with your kids (without touching retirement savings).


**Retirement savings by age 40:** Some experts recommend making extra contributions and having six times your annual salary saved.
## Financial goals for your 50s
  * Take advantage of [catch-up contributions](https://www.irs.gov/retirement-plans/plan-participant-employee/retirement-topics-catch-up-contributions).
  * Get [long-term care](https://www.statefarm.com/simple-insights/health-insurance/dont-wait-too-long-to-get-longterm-care-coverage) insurance.
  * Check on your investment portfolio regularly, looking for ways to maximize value.
  * Look into [financial advisors](https://www.statefarm.com/simple-insights/financial/when-do-i-need-a-financial-advisor) who can help you manage your situation.
  * Get educated on Medicare, Social Security and other retirement benefits.
  * Increase your contributions to your 401(k) plan now that you can use the over 50 tax advantage.
  * Put aside medical savings and maximize your [health savings account](https://www.statefarm.com/simple-insights/health-insurance/key-health-savings-account-benefits).
  * Revisit your will.


**Retirement savings by age 50:** Some experts recommend having seven to eight times your annual salary saved.

# A decade-by-decade approach
## Financial goals: Your 20s
  * Enroll in health insurance.
  * Start investing in a 401(k).
  * Sustain and live within a budget.
  * Eliminate debt (credit cards or loans).
  * Begin to develop an emergency fund.
  * Open savings accounts and investment accounts.
  * Establish consistent income and good spending habits.


**Retirement savings for your 20s** : Some experts recommend saving funds equivalent to one year of your annual salary or saving **15-25%** of your pay as you receive it.
## Financial goals: Your 30s
  * Put 20% down on a home.
  * Create a will and get life insurance.
  * Build kids’ education savings funds.
  * Build a good credit score in the 700s.
  * Eliminate additional non-mortgage debt.
  * Fill your emergency fund with 3-6 months’ salary.
  * Maximize match on employer-sponsored retirement plans. 


**Retirement savings by 30** : Some experts recommend saving **2-3x** your annual salary. 
## Financial goals: Your 40s
  * Increase income.
  * Map out college payment options with your kids.
  * Discuss your parents’ finances and estate plans.
  * Maximize contributions to Roth and Traditional IRAs.
  * Consider additional investments to diversify your portfolio.
  * Pay extra on your mortgage and consider paying it off early.


**Retirement savings by 40** : Some experts recommend saving **6x** your annual salary or making extra catch-up contributions.
## Financial goals: Your 50s
  * Revisit your will.
  * Look into financial advisors.
  * Get long-term care insurance.
  * Maximize your health savings account.
  * Take advantage of catch-up contributions.
  * Check on your investment portfolio regularly.
  * Increase 401(k) plan contributions using the over 50 tax advantage.
  * Get educated on Medicare, Social Security & other retirement benefits.


**Retirement savings by 50** : Some experts recommend saving **7-8x** your annual salary.
## Financial goals: Your 60s
  * Enroll in Medicare.
  * Finalize an estate plan.
  * Finish paying off all remaining debt.
  * Decide when to take Social Security.
  * Plan out a retirement income strategy.
  * Make retirement plans and tweak contributions.
  * Think about downsizing or selling off unneeded assets.


**Retirement savings by 60** : Some experts recommend having **10x** your annual salary saved. 
## Important at all ages
Planning is important at each stage of life and age. Talk with a financial professional who can help you adjust goals and strategies to your specific lifestyle.
## Financial goals for your 60s
  * Finish [paying off all remaining debt](https://www.statefarm.com/simple-insights/financial/how-to-get-out-of-debt), including your mortgage.
  * Make retirement plans and tweak contributions based on what you can afford.
  * Plan out a [retirement income strategy](https://www.statefarm.com/simple-insights/retirement/retirement-income-plan).
  * Decide when to take Social Security and enroll in Medicare.
  * Think about [downsizing](https://www.statefarm.com/simple-insights/financial/calculate-ways-to-save-money) or selling off unneeded assets.
  * Finalize an estate plan.


**Retirement savings by age 60:** Some experts recommend having 10 times your annual salary saved.

Planning is important at each stage of life and age.
