How much is car insurance?

The average cost of car insurance is $2,101 a year, but youll likely pay more or less than that based on where you buy your policy.

State Farm charges an average rate of $1,608 a year, for example. This is the cheapest rate of the largest auto insurance companies we surveyed. You may get an even lower rate from regional or local car insurance companies like Erie and Country Financial.

USAA is cheaper if you or a family member have military ties. Its average rate is $1,320 a year.

With different companies offering such different rates, its important to compare car insurance quotes from several companies before you decide which one is the best for you.

Comparing quotes side by side is one of the best ways to get the cheapest car insurance rate for the coverage types and amounts you need.

Car insurance cost by state

The state you live and drive in can have a big impact on what you pay for car insurance

  • Wyoming drivers pay an average of $372 a year for minimum-coverage car insurance, for example, while Michigan drivers pay $1,597 a year.
  • In Maine, full-coverage car insurance costs $1,243 a year, on average. The average cost in Nevada is $3,439 a year.

Even within these states, though, what you pay for car insurance can be higher or lower than the average cost due to other rate factors.

Why is car insurance so cheap in some states and so expensive in others? 

Car insurance companies often charge higher or lower rates depending on things like:

  • How many claims are filed in a state or city
  • How much those claims cost them
  • Population density
  • Accident and crime rates
  • Likelihood of severe weather

A states car insurance requirements also impact the average price of a policy. Some states require a lot less coverage than others, and that can help drivers there get a cheaper rate than they would in other states.

Although theres nothing you can do about the factors listed above, you can counter them by driving safely, avoiding claims and comparing quotes from several companies at least once a year.

Car insurance cost by age

Younger drivers usually pay more for car insurance than older drivers with more experience on the road do. A lot more.

  • The average 18-year-old driver pays $2,673 a year for minimum car insurance.
  • These drivers pay an average of $6,359 a year for full-coverage car insurance.

Teen car insurance rates are more than triple the amount a typical 30-year-old driver pays for the same amount of coverage.

Young drivers pay the highest car insurance premiums of any age group. This is mostly because insurers see them as a high risk due to their lack of experience behind the wheel. Teen drivers usually see their rates go down as they age and keep a clean driving history.

Car insurance cost with a DUI

The average cost of full-coverage car insurance after a DUI (driving under the influence) is $3,891 a year. Thats an 85% increase from what a driver with a clean record pays for the same amount of car insurance.

Progressive is the best company for cheap car insurance with a DUI, charging an average of $2,652 a year. Its also the company with the smallest average rate increase after a DUI. Progressive charges drivers with clean records $2,076 a year, which means its DUI rate is just 28% more.

After teen drivers, car insurance companies consider drivers with a DUI on their record to be the riskiest to insure. This is why drivers with DUIs pay much higher rates for car insurance than drivers with clean records do.

Also, a DUI can stay on your insurance record for three to five years, and youll probably pay higher rates the whole time.

If your license is suspended after a DUI, youll probably have to file an SR-22 form with the state. The SR-22 proves that you carry the minimum amount of car insurance your state requires. Youll need this to reinstate your license.

Factors that impact your car insurance cost

What you pay for car insurance is based on several factors, including where you live (your ZIP code) and the make and model of the vehicle you drive.

Other factors that determine your car insurance rate include your:

  • Annual mileage
  • Driving record
  • Insurance claim history
  • Policy coverage limits and deductible
  • Age
  • Gender
  • Marital status
  • Credit history (in some states)

Car insurance companies weigh these rate factors differently when setting your quote. One company may see your age or claim history as being a greater risk than another company, for example, and charge you more as a result.

This is why car insurance quotes can differ so much between companies. Its also why comparing car insurance quotes from multiple companies can help you get the cheapest rate for the coverage you need.

How to lower your car insurance costs

Some of the best ways to lower your car insurance rate are to look for discounts, raise your deductible and compare quotes from several companies.

Get discounts

Qualifying for one or more car insurance discounts can save you a lot of money. Some of the most common discounts reward people who:

  • Bundle multiple insurance policies with one company
  • Insure more than one vehicle
  • Drive safely
  • Get good grades
  • Go paperless
  • Pay up front and in full

Increase your deductible

Raising your car insurance deductible lowers your overall rate. Since your deductible is what you pay out of pocket when you file a claim, make sure you can afford that amount before you change it.

Compare quotes

Comparing car insurance quotes from a number of companies side by side lets you see which one offers the best price for the coverage you need. Use the same info for each quote so you can make the most accurate comparison.

Frequently asked questions

The average cost of car insurance is $829 a year if you buy minimum coverage. For full-coverage car insurance, the average cost is $2,101 a year.

If you buy minimum coverage, the average cost of car insurance per month is $69. The average cost of full-coverage car insurance per month is $175.

Nevada is the most expensive state for full-coverage car insurance. Its drivers pay an average of $3,439 a year, or $287 a month. Florida has the next most expensive state at $272 a month.

Michigan has the highest minimum-coverage rate at $1,597 a year, or $133 a month. Nevada has the next highest rate at $130 a month.

Maine is the cheapest state for full-coverage car insurance. Its rates average $1,243 a year, or $103 a month. New Hampshire is next-cheapest at $107 a month.

Wyoming and Vermont have the cheapest minimum-coverage rates at $31 a month and $36 a month, respectively.

Methodology

LendingTree uses insurance rate data from Quadrant Information Services using publicly sourced insurance company filings. Rates are based on an analysis of hundreds of thousands of car insurance quotes for a typical driver. Prices are shown for comparative purposes only. Your own rates may be different.

Unless noted otherwise, quotes are for a full-coverage policy for a 30-year-old man with good credit and a clean driving record who drives a 2015 Honda Civic EX.

Minimum-liability policies provide liability coverage with the states required minimum limits.
Full-coverage policies include collision, comprehensive and liability coverage:

  • Bodily injury liability: $50,000 per person, $100,000 per accident
  • Property damage liability: $25,000
  • Uninsured / underinsured motorist bodily injury: $50,000 per person and $100,000 per accident
  • Personal injury protection: Minimum limits, where required by law
  • Collision: $500 deductible
  • Comprehensive: $500 deductible

Our team of insurance experts rated insurance companies based on several categories. These categories include average rates, discounts, coverage options, third-party customer service ratings and app/website experience. We weighted these categories based on what customers value in an insurance company.

For third-party customer service ratings, we included Complaint Index scores from the National Association of Insurance Commissioners (NAIC) and financial strength ratings from A.M. Best. NAIC Complaint Index scores are used to determine how satisfied customers are with their claims, while financial strength ratings from AM Best reflect the ability to pay out claims.
