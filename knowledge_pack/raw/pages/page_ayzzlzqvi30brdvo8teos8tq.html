SR-22 Insurance in Indiana: What Is It? How Much Does It Cost?

Written by Lindsay Bishop
Agent Reviewed
Updated Nov 4, 2025

An SR-22 in Indiana is a certificate that is required for drivers after a serious driving violation as proof of insurance to get back a suspended license.

Currently Insured?
It's free, simple and secure.

If you face a serious driving violation in the state of Indiana that results in a suspension, you may be required to get SR-22 coverage. This certificate serves as proof of insurance to help you get your license back.

Your insurance company will file the certificate on your behalf, which typically requires a one-time cost of about $50.

An insurance policy with an SR-22 in Indiana usually costs more than a standard car insurance policy. But the SR-22 filing itself doesn't generally raise the cost of car insurance much. The increased cost is usually due to the driving violation that caused you to need an SR-22 in the first place.

Cheap SR-22 insurance in Indiana

State Farm, Progressive and Auto-Owners offer the cheapest rates for SR-22 insurance in Indiana.

The only real cost associated with SR-22 insurance is the fee to file the form, which is usually between $15 and $50. However, SR-22 insurance costs more than a regular policy because drivers with tickets on their record are more likely to cause an accident in the future. This increased risk makes them more expensive to insure.

What is SR-22 insurance in Indiana, and when is it required?

An SR-22 certificate is a form your insurance company submits to the state insurance office to prove your policy meets the minimum coverage required by Indiana law.

Indiana's minimum liability car insurance coverage requirements are:

  • Bodily injury liability: $25,000 per person and $50,000 per accident
  • Property damage liability: $25,000 per accident
  • Uninsured motorist bodily injury liability: $50,000 per accident

Who needs SR-22 insurance in Indiana?

There are a number of reasons you may need SR-22 insurance. Some of the most common are:

  • Failure to pay for insurance
  • Reckless driving
  • Vehicular manslaughter
  • DUI
  • Drag racing
  • You are convicted of offenses that lead to driver's license suspension
  • You are convicted of insurance violations

If you are from another state, you still have to keep your SR-22 if you are driving in Indiana.

Indiana non-owner SR-22 insurance

Drivers who don't own a car but are working toward getting their license reinstated need a non-owner auto insurance policy. This type of policy protects you if you're driving a car that is the legal property of someone else.

Non-owner policies are typically less expensive than other SR-22 policies while still meeting the necessary legal requirements.

How to get an SR-22 in Indiana

To get SR-22 insurance in Indiana, you will need to take a few key steps.

Find an authorized insurance company. Not all companies provide SR-22 insurance. Start by calling your current company to see if they offer SR-22 insurance. If not, you may need to find a new company that can file the SR-22 form on your behalf.

File the SR-22 form and pay the fee. To get your driving privileges reinstated, your insurance company will need to file the SR-22 form electronically with the Indiana Bureau of Motor Vehicles (BMV). Your company will likely charge a flat fee between $15 and $50 to file your SR-22 certificate.

Get confirmation. After processing the SR-22 form, both the Indiana BMV and your insurance company should send you a letter confirming your insurance coverage and legal right to get back behind the wheel.

If the state of Indiana requires you to have SR-22 insurance coverage and you live out of state, you can submit an Out of State Residency Affidavit to waive your SR-22 requirement.

If you move back to Indiana before the end of your SR-22 requirement period, you must have proof of insurance coverage and file an SR-22 certificate.

How long is SR-22 insurance coverage required in Indiana?

The length of time you'll be required to have SR-22 insurance in Indiana depends on the reason you're required to file the SR-22.

  • If you failed to provide proof of insurance when requested by the Indiana BMV, you need to have SR-22 coverage for 180 consecutive days.
  • If your driving privileges are suspended, you must keep SR-22 insurance for three years following the reinstatement of your license.
  • After your third or subsequent suspension, you will be required to carry SR-22 insurance for five years.

Letting your SR-22 coverage lapse can result in lost driving privileges, reinstatement fees and higher auto insurance rates.

To avoid a coverage lapse, you should try to renew your policy early at least 30 days in advance while you're required to carry SR-22 insurance. Insurance companies will notify the Indiana BMV if you cancel or fail to renew your SR-22 policy.

Once you've had SR-22 insurance for the required period, contact the Indiana BMV to make sure your requirement is complete before you switch to a standard car insurance policy.

Frequently asked questions

How much is SR-22 insurance in Indiana?

The cost of SR-22 insurance in Indiana depends on the incident that caused you to need an SR-22. For example, minimum-coverage SR-22 insurance costs an average of $83 per month for an Indiana driver with a DUI. In comparison, a driver convicted of reckless driving can expect to pay $86 per month for the same coverage.

How long do you have to have SR-22 insurance in Indiana?

The amount of time you're required to have SR-22 insurance depends on the reason you need it. Drivers who have their license suspended need SR-22 insurance for three years after their license is reinstated. If you failed to provide proof of insurance to the Indiana BMV, you're required to have an SR-22 filing for 180 days.

What does an SR-22 cover in Indiana?

SR-22 insurance refers to a certificate your insurance company needs to file with the Indiana BMV to prove you have insurance coverage after a serious traffic ticket or violation. Your policy must have the minimum amount of coverage required in Indiana, which includes $25,000 of bodily injury liability coverage per person and $50,000 per accident, $25,000 of personal property liability per accident and $50,000 of uninsured motorist bodily injury liability coverage. However, you can get more coverage if you need it.

How do I get an SR-22 in Indiana?

To get an SR-22 policy in Indiana, you'll need to find an insurance company that offers SR-22 coverage, buy a policy and pay the SR-22 fee, which is usually between $15 to $50. Your insurance company will file your SR-22 certificate with the Indiana BMV to prove you have an insurance policy.

Who has the cheapest SR-22 insurance in Indiana?

State Farm, Progressive and Auto-Owners offer the cheapest SR-22 insurance for most drivers in Indiana. The cheapest company for you depends on the driving infraction that caused you to need an SR-22.

Who has the cheapest SR-22 insurance in Indianapolis?

Progressive has the cheapest SR-22 insurance for drivers with a DUI in Indianapolis. In comparison, a minimum-coverage policy from Progressive costs an average of $64 per month. That's 30% cheaper than the average in Indianapolis, which is $92 per month.
