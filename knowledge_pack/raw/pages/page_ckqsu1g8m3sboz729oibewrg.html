State Farm Pros and Cons

With so much information thrown your way about State Farm, we want to take a minute to recap the most important highlights of the company. The positives youll find at State Farm include:

  • Low Rates: State Farm is one of the most affordable auto insurance companies for drivers.
  • Local Agents: State Farm agents offer more personalized assistance to customers.
  • Discount Options: State Farm has several discounts with high saving potential.

However, there are a few limitations to be aware of. The downsides customers may experience with State Farm auto insurance include:

  • Financial Stability: State Farms lower rating could suggest financial issues in the future.
  • Add-On Coverages: State Farm doesnt sell modified car insurance.

For the most part, State Farms pros outweigh the cons that customers may experience, so buying car insurance from State Farm may be the right choice for most drivers.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does State Farm have a good reputation for paying claims?

Yes, State Farm has a good reputation for paying car insurance claims with a strong rating from J.D. Power.

Did State Farm get downgraded by AM Best?

Yes, State Farms financial rating was recently downgraded by A.M. Best.

Who gets State Farms best rates?

Good drivers in their 40s will have the best rates from State Farm (Read More: How to Lower Your Car Insurance Cost).

Who is State Farms biggest competitor?

Progressive and Geico are two of State Farms biggest competitors, as they have the next largest market shares.

Why does my State Farm bill keep going up?

Rate increases can be due to a number of factors, such as changes to your driving record or location risk changes. If your rates are getting expensive, compare rates with our free quote tool to find the cheapest coverage in your area.

Are State Farm rates negotiable?

Car insurance rates at State Farm arent negotiable, but you can apply for State Farm car insurance discounts to lower your rates.

Does State Farm sell gap insurance?

State Farm does sell gap insurance, but it is called payoff protector instead of gap at State Farm (Learn More: Is State Farm gap insurance worth it?).

Does State Farm charge to cancel a policy?

No, State Farm does not charge a cancellation fee.

Can I cancel a State Farm claim?

Yes, you can cancel your State Farm claim by contacting your agent or customer service.

Does State Farm refund if you cancel?

State Farm only refunds customers who cancel if they have prepaid for car insurance coverage. The exact cancellation and refund details can be found in your policy.

Make sure to line up another policy if you are keeping your car, as you need coverage to drive (Learn More: Why You Need Car Insurance).

Is State Farm pulling out of Florida?

No, State Farm Insurance is not pulling out of Florida.

State Farm, as with many insurance companies care about only 1 thing. Money. I think the best way to understand it is just to watch the Incredibles, and that scene with Bob at his job. Does it matter who is at fault? No. Does it matter what the circumstances were? No. Does it matter if anybody is being unethical? No. What matters is the cold, hard cash. You can pay thousands of dollars, have a fender bender with barely a scratch and OOOP, they had to pay money to fix it? Strike one. Soon enough, you are out with yesterday's garbage. Heaven forbid you have a child learning how to drive and something goes wrong. I can promise you it will haunt them for years to come. They say they only look at the past three years. That would be a lie. They say they have your back, and that would be a lie. Any possibility they have to pay anything, even if only partial fault, they can, and will, throw the sack at ya. With that said, it does have some lovely agents. I could rave about how awesome they are. Heck, an agent thought it would be ridiculous to sack somebody over what happened to me, but, you know, he appealed and the company still said "Whaaat, we must pay money even though the person has barely had issues? No way!" Amazing how they can hire good ethical employees, but can't be a good ethical business. You can spend way more in premiums than they have ever had to pay for anything, but who cares? They don't want to to ever have to do their job. Insurance is required by law. But what is the point of having insurance when at the snap of the fingers they can dig up something that happened when you were learning how to drive and compare it to a small fender bender in the future and say "We don't want that!" What is to feel safe about? The motto "we got your back... as long as you are the only one who has to pay us anything. Ever." Should be their slogan. You can't learn from mistakes, even if you never make them again, you can't have complications with a new license without it haunting your future. You can't be human, and sometimes make human error. Insurance is there to ensure you will be ok. The only thing state farm ensures is their path to your wallet, and a way to ditch if any of YOUR money ever has to come back to you because things happen.
