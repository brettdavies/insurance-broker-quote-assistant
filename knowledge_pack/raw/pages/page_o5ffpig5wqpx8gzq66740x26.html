High-Risk Auto Insurance Requirements

Car insurance is so expensive for high-risk drivers that it may be required for many reasons. One of the most significant purposes for high-risk insurance is to offset the risk of a driver filing an auto insurance claim.

But what are the specific reasons why you need high-risk auto insurance? Check out the table below to see the characteristics that define a high-risk driver.

Age indicates the driving experience of the insured, but location considers the risk of you filing claims based on the statistics of where you live. Gaps in coverage show how long youve been uninsured while owning a car.

Vehicle use is another way of showing how often you drive. Generally, cars dont get in accidents when theyre not moving. Therefore, the more you drive, the more youre at risk of an accident.

Although driving factors make sense, it may be confusing to understand why non-driving factors should affect your car insurance policy.

Even vehicle types can determine whether youre at high risk. Some vehicle types have higher accident frequencies, and some vehicle types generate expensive car insurance claims.

Therefore, your auto insurance company may drive up your rates to balance the risk.

The Impact of Bad Credit on Auto Insurance Rates

Bad driver car insurance can get worse if they have poor credit. High-risk car insurance companies issue more expensive rates to people with bad credit because it correlates to a high chance of filing claims.

Excellent credit does the opposite. Auto insurance companies correlate excellent and good credit with a low chance of filing a car insurance claim.

The Need for SR-22 Auto Insurance

What is SR-22 insurance? Whether you require an SR-22 hinges on your driving history, with insurance rates typically elevated for individuals with a poor driving record and often necessitating SR-22 certification.

Drivers who have too many accidents or severe traffic violations (DUI, reckless driving, or racing) may require an SR-22 form before their driving privileges are restored.

SR-22 certification is like a promise to your state that says youll carry the state minimum car insurance. Your state could suspend or revoke your drivers license for failing to meet state requirements for auto insurance.

If youre a high-risk driver in Indiana, youre required to get SR-50 certification. Meanwhile, Florida and Virginias high-risk drivers who commit serious driving offenses need double the minimum requirements through FR-44 certification. (For more information, read our Cheap FR-44 Insurance).

Frequently Asked Questions

How to get high-risk car insurance?

You can get high-risk insurance by shopping around with different providers, using online quote tools, or contacting insurance companies that specialize in high-risk policies.

What is considered a high-risk driver?

A high-risk driver is someone with multiple traffic violations, accidents, a DUI conviction, or poor credit, all of which make them more likely to file claims.

What is high-risk insurance?

High-risk insurance is a type of auto coverage designed for drivers who are deemed riskier to insure due to their driving history or other factors.

What makes you a high-risk driver?

A combination of traffic violations, at-fault accidents, DUI convictions, or a low credit score can classify you as a high-risk driver.

Where to get car insurance for high-risk drivers?

You can get car insurance for high-risk drivers from specialized companies or by comparing quotes from standard insurers that offer high-risk policies. To find the best rates, compare multiple insurance companies in your area using our free online quote tool above.

Who has the best car insurance for high-risk drivers in Michigan?

Several insurers offer competitive rates for high-risk drivers in Michigan, including State Farm, Progressive, and GEICO. Its best to compare quotes to find the right fit.

Who has the cheapest high-risk auto insurance?

Cheap high-risk auto insurance varies by location and individual driving records, but companies like GEICO, Progressive, and The General often offer affordable auto insurance for high-risk drivers.

Who will insure a high-risk driver?

Many national and specialized insurance companies, including Progressive, GEICO, and State Farm, offer car insurance for high-risk drivers.

Who requires high-risk insurance?

Drivers with serious traffic violations, DUI convictions, or multiple accidents typically require high-risk insurance to stay legally compliant.

Who is an at-risk driver?

An at-risk driver is someone whose driving behavior or history increases the likelihood of future accidents or claims, making them more expensive to insure.

Can multiple speeding tickets make me a high-risk driver?

Yes, it can. Too many speeding violations drive up car insurance rates by 15% or more.

Is Geico good for high-risk drivers?

Yes, it is. Geico is one of the cheapest companies in the United States, providing auto insurance for high-risk drivers. You may check our Geico auto insurance review for more details.

How long does high-risk auto insurance last?

High-risk car insurance can last at least three years. It takes a while to be considered a high-risk driver, and it will take some time to lower your risk and begin to save on your auto insurance again. You can explore car insurance discounts to help reduce your premiums during this period.

How long are you considered a high-risk driver?

You are typically considered a high-risk driver for three to five years, depending on your driving record and state laws.
