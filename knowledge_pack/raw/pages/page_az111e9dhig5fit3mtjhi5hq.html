Progressive and Allstate are two of America’s largest insurance companies, and for good reason. Both companies have strong reputations — Allstate ranks as the fourth-largest insurer by market share, while Progressive holds the second spot.[1] But the two insurers differ in several notable ways.

Allstate often delivers value with lower average rates and higher customer satisfaction scores. Progressive, on the other hand, stands out for its broader availability and superior digital tools for managing your policy.

Ultimately, the better choice depends on what matters most to you, whether that’s affordability, customer service, convenience, or something else entirely.

The article below explores the differences between Progressive and Allstate. We compare car insurance rates, coverage, and discounts to help you decide on the best company for you.

Key Takeaways

  • Allstate typically offers lower average rates than Progressive for both liability and full-coverage policies.

  • Progressive offers more comprehensive coverage options — including pet injury and mechanical breakdown coverage — than Allstate.

  • Both companies offer telematics programs, but unsafe driving behavior could increase your premiums.

Progressive Snapshot vs. Allstate Drivewise

Usage-based insurance programs monitor your driving habits to potentially lower your premiums based on safe driving behaviors. They’re ideal for drivers who avoid hard braking and late-night driving and maintain steady speeds.

Progressive’s Snapshot uses a mobile app to monitor factors like time of day, mileage, and braking. After each trip, you’ll receive personalized feedback, including a star rating, data summary, and driving tips to help improve your score. Safe drivers may earn significant discounts, but risky driving can lead to higher rates.

Allstate’s Drivewise also tracks your driving through its mobile app and rewards you for staying under 80 mph, avoiding sudden stops, and limiting late-night trips. New customers get a participation discount after taking at least one trip per policy period and may receive additional rewards after 50 trips.

Like Snapshot, Drivewise can increase your rates if it detects risky driving behaviors. It also includes crash detection and automatic assistance if you get into a serious accident.
