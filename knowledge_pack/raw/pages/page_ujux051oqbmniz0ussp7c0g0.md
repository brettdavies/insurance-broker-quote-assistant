Additionally, some insurance providers may even choose to cancel your policy altogether. However, Progressive takes a different approach and may still offer coverage to individuals with a DUI on their record.
But what exactly happens when a DUI affects your car insurance rates? Let’s dive deeper into the factors that determine the extent of the increase. One of the key factors is the state you reside in. Each state has its own regulations and guidelines when it comes to DUI offenses and how they impact insurance rates. Some states have stricter policies, resulting in higher rate hikes, while others may be more lenient.
Another factor that plays a role in determining the increase in insurance rates is your driving history. If you have a clean driving record prior to the DUI offense, the increase may be less severe compared to someone with a history of traffic violations. Insurance companies take into account your overall risk profile, and a DUI can significantly impact that.
Furthermore, the severity of the DUI offense itself can also affect your insurance rates. If it was a first-time offense with no accidents or injuries involved, the increase may be relatively lower compared to a DUI with multiple offenses or accidents. Insurance companies assess the level of risk you pose as a driver, and a more severe offense will likely result in a higher rate increase.
Progressive, as a car insurance provider, understands that everyone makes mistakes and believes in offering fair and competitive pricing to drivers who have had a DUI. While your rates may increase, they strive to take into consideration all the relevant factors to ensure you are not unfairly penalized.

Some insurance companies also offer programs or discounts to encourage responsible driving behavior and help drivers recover from a DUI.
In conclusion, a DUI can have a significant impact on your car insurance rates. Insurance companies view DUI offenses as a higher risk and may increase your premiums or even cancel your policy.
