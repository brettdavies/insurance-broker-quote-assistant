How to File a Progressive Insurance Claim

Home Blog Knowledge Base How to File a Progressive Insurance Claim

  • Sean Chalaki
  • August 30, 2025
  • Knowledge Base

Progressive is one of the largest auto insurance providers in the United States, with more than 27 million policies in force nationwide, and its claims process is built to serve millions of drivers across many states. With a company of this size, knowing where to start, what to expect, and how state laws in Texas, California, and Illinois affect your case can make a significant difference in how your claim moves forward.

Understanding Progressives Claims Process

Progressive differs from smaller regional carriers because of its national scale. The company maintains centralized call centers, online platforms, and mobile tools, which means much of the process is handled digitally. Once a claim is filed, Progressive assigns it to an adjuster who may be located anywhere in the country. While this allows the company to process a high volume of claims efficiently, it can also create delays and communication issues for policyholders. Before you begin, we strongly recommend contacting GoSuits for a free initial consultation to understand all of your rights and legal options. Taking this step prior to filing helps ensure that your claim is handled from a position of strength. Once you have done that, here is a step-by-step guide on how to file your claim with Progressive.

Step 1. Reporting a Claim with Progressive

The first step after an accident is reporting it to Progressive. You can do this by calling their 24/7 claims hotline at 1-800-776-4737 or by submitting a claim online through the Progressive Claims Portal. Claims can also be filed through the Progressive Mobile App, which allows you to upload accident details and photos directly.

When reporting, Progressive will ask for the policyholders name, policy number, the date and location of the accident, and the police report number if available. Once submitted, you should receive a confirmation and claim number. State acknowledgment requirements vary: 15 days in Texas, 40 days in California, and prompt response standards in Illinois.

Step 2. Claim Assignment and First Contact

After intake, Progressive assigns the file to an adjuster who becomes your main point of contact. Because Progressive centralizes its staff, the adjuster may not be based in your state. In some cases, adjusters handle files from multiple states at once, which can slow response times.

First contact may take several days, particularly in busy metropolitan areas such as DallasFort Worth, Los Angeles County, and Cook County, where claim volumes are extremely high. The longer the delay, the more important it becomes to have legal representation pressing for timely communication.

Step 3. Vehicle Inspection and Estimating

Progressive increasingly relies on photo estimating and artificial intelligence tools, often asking claimants to submit pictures through the Progressive Mobile App. While this can speed up approvals for minor repairs, AI-driven estimates have been criticized for undervaluing damage compared to in-person inspections.

Step 4. Repairs and Preferred Shops

Progressive operates Service Centers in major metropolitan areas to streamline vehicle inspections, repairs, and rental services. These centers are not mandatory; state laws protect your right to choose non-network repair shops, though exact addresses arent always publicly listed and can be found via progressive.com/claims or by calling their hotline.

Opting for a non-network shop can be beneficial, as they often uncover hidden damage overlooked in Progressives initial estimates, potentially affecting repair costs or leading to a total loss declaration.

Step 5. Medical and Injury Claims

Injury claims are often the most complex part of dealing with Progressive. Auto insurance covers both property damage and bodily injury, but medical coverage varies by state:

  • Texas: Policies commonly include Personal Injury Protection (PIP) or uninsured motorist coverage
  • California: Pure comparative fault rules apply, meaning even someone 99% at fault can seek damages for the remaining 1%
  • Illinois: Modified comparative negligence with a 51% bar prevents recovery if youre found more than half responsible

Because Progressive adjusters handle multiple states, they may not be fully familiar with each states nuances, leading to confusion over whether medical bills should be submitted under PIP, MedPay, or liability coverage.

Step 6. Communication and Claim Tracking

Progressive advertises its digital tools for keeping you informed through app or website status updates. In practice, many accident victims report inconsistent communication, particularly when liability is contested. Without advocacy, its easy for your file to sit without movement while you wait for repairs, medical reimbursement, or settlement offers.

Step 7. Settlement and Payment

The final stage involves settlement, which means paying for repairs or declaring total loss for property damage, and negotiating liability and damages for injury claims. Total loss cases are particularly contentious, as Progressives valuation systems often calculate lower market values than consumers expect.

Settlement payments may come by check or direct deposit, but lienholders such as medical providers or lenders may be paid first, which can surprise many claimants.

How to Submit Legal Claim Letters to Progressive Insurance

When sending a letter of representation or spoliation letter to Progressive Insurance, accuracy and proof of submission are critical. Unlike smaller carriers, Progressive does not publish a dedicated claims email for attorneys or consumers. Most claim activity is routed through the Progressive Claims Portal, the Progressive Mobile App, or their 24/7 claims hotline at 1-800-776-4737. These are the official intake channels recognized for initiating or tracking claims.

That said, some publicly available regulatory filings do list corporate contact details. For example, according to the Federal Motor Carrier Safety Administration and related filings, Progressive Casualty Insurance lists the following address, phone, and fax for registered carriers:

P.O. Box 94739, Cleveland, OH 44101

Phone: 800-444-4487

Fax: 440-603-4555

The best practice is to send legal claim letters through Progressives official claims channels, and if you mail physical correspondence, do so via certified mail or other trackable delivery methods so you can verify receipt. Always include the insureds name, policy number (if available), accident date and location, vehicle information, and police report number. Clearly state your legal representation and any spoliation requests, such as preserving the vehicle, photographs, or electronic crash data.

After submission, follow up with Progressive by calling the claims hotline to confirm receipt, obtain a claim number, and request the adjusters direct contact information. Given Progressives enormous claim volume across Texas, California, and Illinois, persistence is often necessary to prevent delays. Having an attorney involved early ensures your correspondence is routed correctly and that deadlines are enforced under your states insurance regulations.

State-Specific Progressive Claim Considerations

Texas

Texas law requires insurers to acknowledge claims within 15 days and decide within 15 business days of receiving necessary documentation. Minimum coverage requirements are 30/60/25, but many Texans carry additional PIP or UM/UIM coverage. Progressives regional service hubs in DallasFort Worth, Houston, San Antonio, and Austin handle significant claim volumes, and delays are frequent without persistent follow-up.

California

California operates under pure comparative fault rules and requires insurers to respond to claims within 40 days of receiving proof of claim. The Department of Insurance enforces strict fair claim settlement practices. Progressives significant presence in Los Angeles, San Diego, and the Bay Area processes substantial accident claims, while state law restricts insurers from forcing drivers into specific repair shops.

Illinois

Illinois requires minimum coverage of 25/50/20, with optional MedPay. The state follows modified comparative negligence, barring recovery if youre more than 51% at fault. Progressives claims are heavily concentrated in Chicago, Cook County, and surrounding suburbs, with the Illinois Department of Insurance providing oversight.

Common Mistakes to Avoid When Filing A Progressive Insurance Claim

Avoiding these critical errors can save you thousands of dollars and prevent your Progressive claim from being denied or undervalued:

  • Dont Give Recorded Statements Without Preparation: Progressive adjusters often request recorded statements within days of an accident. While youre required to cooperate, youre not required to give an immediate statement. Take time to gather facts, review your policy, and consider consulting an attorney from GoSuits. 
  • Never Sign Documents Without Understanding Them: Progressive may send settlement releases, medical authorizations, or repair agreements that contain binding language. In Texas, California, and Illinois, signing a full and final release ends your right to seek additional compensation, even if injuries worsen later. Read everything carefully or have an attorney review documents before signing.
  • Dont Delay Medical Treatment After an Accident: Waiting days or weeks to seek medical care gives Progressive ammunition to argue your injuries werent serious or werent caused by the accident. This is particularly important in Californias pure comparative fault system, where any delay can reduce your settlement. Seek prompt medical attention and follow all treatment recommendations.
  • Avoid Accepting the First Settlement Offer: Progressives initial offers are typically far below fair value, specifically for injury claims. The company expects negotiation and often increases offers significantly after pushback. Dont feel pressured to accept quickly without consulting an attorney first.

Warning Signs in Progressive Insurance Claims to Watch For

These warning signs indicate Progressive may be acting in bad faith or attempting to undervalue your claim:

  • Unreasonably Low Initial Offers: If Progressives first settlement offer seems shockingly low compared to your medical bills, lost wages, and property damage, it likely is. 
  • Pressure Tactics and Quick Settlement Demands Legitimate claims dont require rushed decisions. If Progressives adjuster is pressuring you to accept this offer today or claiming it will be withdrawn, this is a red flag.  
  • Full and Final Release Language Without Fair Compensation: Any settlement document containing full and final release language should be scrutinized carefully. This language prevents you from seeking additional compensation later, even if you discover new injuries or your condition worsens. Never sign these documents without consulting an attorney first or without understanding exactly what future rights youre giving up.
  • Requests for Excessive Medical Records or Personal Information: While Progressive has the right to investigate claims, requesting medical records from years before the accident or asking for unrelated personal information may indicate an attempt to find reasons to deny your claim. 
  • Denying Obvious Liability or Clear Coverage: If Progressive denies a claim where their insured clearly caused the accident, or claims your damages arent covered under circumstances that obviously fall within your policy terms, this suggests bad faith. Document these denials and consider legal action, as state insurance departments in all three states have penalties for such conduct.

FAQ

How do I file a claim with Progressive?

Call 1-800-776-4737 (24/7), file online at progressive.com/claims (Progressive Claims Portal), or use the Progressive Mobile App to submit details and photos.

What information do I need to report a claim?

Policyholder name, policy number, accident date, time, and location, vehicle information, and police report number (if available). Youll receive a confirmation and claim number after submission.

How quickly will Progressive acknowledge my claim?

By state: Texas generally within 15 days; California within 40 days of receiving proof of claim; Illinois follows prompt response standards.

When will an adjuster contact me?

After intake, a centralized adjuster is assigned. First contact can take several days, especially in high-volume areas like DallasFort Worth, Los Angeles County, and Cook County.

How does Progressive handle vehicle inspections and estimates?

Progressive often uses photo estimating and AI via the Mobile App. While fast for minor damage, these estimates may undervalue repairs compared to in-person inspections.

Do I have to use a Progressive Service Center or preferred shop?

No. You have the right in Texas, California, and Illinois to choose your own repair shop. Progressive Service Centers are optional conveniences.

Where can I find Progressive Service Centers?

Search progressive.com/claims or call the claims hotline for locations in major metro areas. They can coordinate inspections, repairs, and rentals.

Is there a benefit to using a non-network repair shop?

Often, yes. Independent shops may uncover hidden damage not captured in initial photo/AI estimates, which can change repair costs or lead to a total loss determination.

How are injury claims handled in Texas, California, and Illinois?

Texas policies commonly include PIP or UM/UIM; California uses pure comparative fault (you can recover even if mostly at fault); Illinois uses modified comparative negligence with a 51% bar (no recovery if more than 50% at fault). Coverage routing (PIP, MedPay, liability) can be confusing.

Should I give a recorded statement right away?

Not without preparation. You must cooperate, but you dont have to give an immediate recorded statement. Gather facts and consider consulting an attorney first.

Should I sign documents Progressive sends me without review?

No. Documents may include binding full and final release language. Signing can end your right to additional compensation. Read carefully or have an attorney review.

Does delaying medical treatment affect my claim?

Yes. Waiting to seek care can let the insurer argue your injuries are minor or unrelated. Get prompt treatment and follow medical recommendations.

Should I accept the first settlement offer?

Generally no. Initial offers, especially for injuries, are often low. Negotiation commonly increases the offer. Consider legal advice before accepting.

How are settlements and payments issued?

Property damage may be paid after repairs or as a total loss valuation (often lower than expected). Payments can be by check or direct deposit. Lienholders (e.g., medical providers, lenders) may be paid first.

What are warning signs of undervaluation or bad faith by Progressive?

Very low initial offers, pressure to settle quickly, full and final release language without fair compensation, excessive requests for unrelated medical records, and denials of obvious liability or clear coverage.

How do I submit legal claim letters (e.g., representation or spoliation) to Progressive?

Use official channels: the Claims Portal, Mobile App, or 1-800-776-4737. If mailing, send via trackable delivery to: P.O. Box 94739, Cleveland, OH 44101. Additional contacts cited in filings: Phone 800-444-4487, Fax 440-603-4555. Always include insureds name, policy number, accident details, vehicle info, and police report number.

How do I confirm Progressive received my legal letter or claim documents?

Follow up by calling the claims hotline to confirm receipt, obtain a claim number, and request the adjusters direct contact information.

How can I track my Progressive claim?

Use the Progressive app or website for status updates. Note that updates can be inconsistent; proactive follow-up calls may be necessary.

What state-specific timelines and rights should I know?

Texas: acknowledge within 15 days and decide within 15 business days after required documentation; California: respond within 40 days of proof of claim and cannot force you to use specific shops; Illinois: minimum coverage 25/50/20, modified comparative negligence (51% bar), and oversight by the Illinois Department of Insurance.

What if Progressive denies liability?

Liability disputes are common and can cause delays. Document everything and consider legal representation to protect your rights and move the claim forward.

How long does Progressive take to pay a claim?

It varies by state law and claim complexity. Injury claims often take weeks to months, especially when liability or damages are disputed.
