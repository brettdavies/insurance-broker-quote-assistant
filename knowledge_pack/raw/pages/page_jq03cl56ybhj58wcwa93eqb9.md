# Non-combustible vs Joisted Masonry – Understanding the Differences
![LH Team](https://secure.gravatar.com/avatar/b8a6a627372defbe01988048579d2a97a17718f7fea61bffce8f73c9e3513b78?s=40&d=mm&r=g)[LH Team](https://www.leidalandhart.com/author/seoadmin/ "Posts by LH Team")August 1, 2024December 18th, 20246 min read
Choosing the right construction type for your building project is crucial. Masonry non-combustible and joisted masonry are two common methods, each with its own set of advantages and disadvantages. This article will delve into the differences between these construction types, helping you make an informed decision for your next project.
## **What is Masonry Non-Combustible Construction?**
### **Definition and Description**
**Masonry non-combustible construction** uses materials that do not ignite or burn, such as concrete, brick, and stone. This method is known for its high fire resistance and durability. Common applications include commercial buildings, industrial facilities, and public structures.
### **Key Characteristics**
  * **Fire Resistance:** Non-combustible materials provide superior protection against fire, ensuring safety for occupants and protecting assets.
  * **Durability:** Buildings made with these materials tend to have a longer lifespan and can withstand harsh environmental conditions.
  * **Low Maintenance:** Requires minimal maintenance over time, reducing long-term costs and ensuring the structure remains sound for decades.


### **Materials Used**
Masonry non-combustible construction typically involves materials like:
  * **Concrete:** A versatile and durable material that offers excellent fire resistance.
  * **Brick:** Known for its aesthetic appeal and strength, brick is also fire-resistant and durable.
  * **Stone:** Offers a natural and robust option that is both fire-resistant and visually appealing.


### **Common Applications**
Masonry non-combustible construction is often used in:
  * **Commercial Buildings:** Office buildings, retail spaces, and shopping malls benefit from the durability and safety of non-combustible materials.
  * **Industrial Facilities:** Factories and warehouses where fire hazards are a concern.
  * **Public Structures:** Schools, hospitals, and government buildings that require long-lasting and safe construction methods.


## **What is Joisted Masonry Construction?**
### **Definition and Description**
**Joisted masonry construction** combines [masonry walls](https://www.leidalandhart.com/what-is-masonry-wall/ "Understanding Masonry Walls: Types, Construction Techniques, and Considerations") with wood or steel joists. This type of construction is often seen in residential buildings and smaller commercial structures.
![frame vs masonry construction](https://www.leidalandhart.com/wp-content/uploads/2024/09/frame-vs-masonry-construction-1024x683.jpg)
### **Key Characteristics**
  * **Structural Stability:** The combination of masonry walls and joists provides a solid framework that supports the structure effectively.
  * **Fire Risk:** Wooden joists can increase the risk of fire compared to non-combustible materials, although steel joists can mitigate this risk to some extent.
  * **Cost-Effectiveness:** Typically, less expensive and quicker to construct than masonry non-combustible buildings, making it an attractive option for many projects.


### **Materials Used**
Joisted masonry construction uses:
  * **Masonry Walls:** Brick or concrete blocks that provide structural support.
  * **Wood Joists:** Common in residential construction, wood joists are cost-effective but increase fire risk.
  * **Steel Joists:** Offer a more fire-resistant alternative to wood, providing additional safety.


### **Common Applications**
Joisted masonry construction is suitable for:
  * **Residential Buildings:** Homes and apartment complexes benefit from the cost-effectiveness and speed of [construction](https://www.leidalandhart.com/services/ "construction").
  * **Smaller Commercial Structures:** Small offices, retail stores, and restaurants where budget and construction time are significant considerations.

## **Comparison of Masonry Non-Combustible and Joisted Masonry**
### **Fire Resistance**
  * **Masonry Non-Combustible:** Offers high fire resistance due to the use of non-combustible materials, providing peace of mind for property owners.
  * **Joisted Masonry:** Has moderate fire resistance; the presence of wood joists can be a fire hazard, although [steel joists](https://masonryadvisorycouncil.org/wp-content/uploads/2021/03/Steel-Joist-to-Masonry-Connections.pdf "steel joists") can improve fire safety.


### **Durability and Longevity**
  * **Masonry Non-Combustible:** Generally more durable and long-lasting, with materials that can withstand environmental wear and tear.
  * **Joisted Masonry:** While durable, wooden elements may degrade over time due to factors like moisture and pests.


### **Cost and Construction Time**
  * **Masonry Non-Combustible:** Typically more expensive and time-consuming to build, reflecting the quality and durability of materials used.
  * **Joisted Masonry:** Often less expensive and quicker to construct, making it a practical choice for projects with tight budgets and timelines.


### **Insurance Implications**
  * **Masonry Non-Combustible:** May have lower insurance premiums due to reduced fire risk and enhanced durability.
  * **Joisted Masonry:** Potentially higher premiums because of the increased fire risk and potential for wood degradation.


## **Benefits and Drawbacks**
### **Masonry Non-Combustible**
  * **Benefits:** High fire resistance, durability, lower insurance costs, and minimal maintenance requirements.
  * **Drawbacks:** Higher construction costs and longer build times, which may not be suitable for all budgets or project timelines.


### **Joisted Masonry**
  * **Benefits:** Cost-effective, quicker construction, and suitable for a variety of smaller projects.
  * **Drawbacks:** Higher fire risk, potential for higher insurance costs, and maintenance issues with wood over time.


## **Suitable Use Cases**
**Masonry Non-Combustible** is ideal for commercial buildings, industrial facilities, and public structures that require high durability and fire resistance. **Joisted Masonry** is suitable for residential buildings and smaller commercial structures where [cost and construction](https://www.leidalandhart.com/soft-vs-hard-costs-in-construction/ "cost and construction") time are significant considerations.
### **Example Scenarios**
  * **Masonry Non-Combustible:** A new hospital or school requiring robust fire safety measures and long-term durability.
  * **Joisted Masonry:** A residential development or a small office building where budget constraints and faster construction are priorities.


## **Conclusion**
Choosing between masonry non-combustible and joisted masonry depends on your specific needs, budget, and project requirements. Each method has its strengths and weaknesses, making it crucial to evaluate these factors carefully.

### **What is the main difference between masonry non-combustible and joisted masonry?**

Masonry non-combustible construction is better for fire resistance due to the use of non-combustible materials.
### **How do construction costs compare between masonry non-combustible and joisted masonry?**
Masonry non-combustible construction is typically more expensive and time-consuming, whereas joisted masonry is generally less expensive and quicker to construct.
### **Are there any specific building codes that favor one type over the other?**
Building codes may vary by region and project type, but non-combustible construction often meets stricter fire safety standards.
### **What should property owners consider when choosing between these construction types?**
Property owners should consider factors such as budget, construction time, fire resistance, durability, and insurance implications.
### **What is the difference between joisted masonry and non-combustible?**
Joisted masonry combines masonry walls with wood or steel joists, whereas non-combustible construction uses materials that do not ignite or burn.

The two major types are masonry non-combustible and steel frame construction.
### **What is the difference between combustible and non-combustible buildings?**
Combustible buildings use materials that can ignite and burn, while non-combustible buildings use materials that do not ignite or burn.
### **Is masonry a combustible material?**
No, masonry materials like brick, stone, and concrete are non-combustible.
