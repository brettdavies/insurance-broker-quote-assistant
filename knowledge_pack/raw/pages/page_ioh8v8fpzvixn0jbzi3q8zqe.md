## What are Alaska's minimum auto insurance requirements?
Drivers in Alaska are legally required to have [auto liability coverage](https://www.progressive.com/answers/auto-liability-insurance/) ([BI liability](https://www.progressive.com/answers/bodily-injury-liability/) and [PD liability](https://www.progressive.com/answers/property-damage-liability/)), at the following minimum car insurance limits:
### Coverage
Coverage |  What it covers |  Minimum amount  
---|---|---  
Bodily injury liability  |  What it covers Injuries you cause to others in an accident |  Minimum amount $50,000 per person  
$100,000 per accident  
Property damage liability  |  What it covers Damage you cause to other people's property in an accident |  Minimum amount $25,000 per accident  
Alaska also requires insurers to offer [uninsured/underinsured (UM/UIM)](https://www.progressive.com/answers/uninsured-motorist-insurance/) bodily injury and property damage coverage at the following limits, though you may reject or lower these coverages via a signed form if you do not want them.
### Coverage
Coverage |  What it covers |  Minimum amount  
---|---|---  
UM/UIM bodily injury  |  What it covers Your medical expenses an uninsured or underinsured driver causes |  Minimum amount $50,000 per person  
$100,000 per accident  
UM/UIM property damage  |  What it covers Damage to your property an uninsured or underinsured driver causes |  Minimum amount $25,000 per accident  
While these are Alaska's minimum required car [insurance limits](https://www.progressive.com/answers/insurance-limits/), higher coverage amounts may better protect your family's financial wellbeing. Learn [how much car insurance you may need](https://www.progressive.com/answers/how-much-car-insurance-do-i-need/) based on your circumstances and preferences.
### Is Alaska a no-fault state?
No. Alaska is an at-fault state, which means the party responsible for an accident must cover the injuries of the other driver and their passengers. Learn about [what happens if you're at fault in an accident](https://www.progressive.com/answers/at-fault-vs-no-fault-accidents/).

## Additional car insurance coverages to consider
If you're leasing or financing a vehicle, most lenders require you to carry [comprehensive auto coverage](https://www.progressive.com/answers/comprehensive-car-insurance/) and [collision coverage](https://www.progressive.com/answers/auto-collision-coverage/) in addition to Alaska's state-required coverages. Comprehensive and collision pay to repair or replace your car regardless of fault, if it's in an accident, gets stolen, or is otherwise damaged due to a covered incident. It also makes sense to add these physical damage coverages if you're not sure you can afford a new car or major repairs after an accident.
