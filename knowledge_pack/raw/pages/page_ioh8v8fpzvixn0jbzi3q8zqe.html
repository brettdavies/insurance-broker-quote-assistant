What are Alaska's minimum auto insurance requirements?

Drivers in Alaska are legally required to have auto liability coverage (BI liability and PD liability), at the following minimum car insurance limits:

Is Alaska a no-fault state?

No. Alaska is an at-fault state, which means the party responsible for an accident must cover the injuries of the other driver and their passengers. Learn about what happens if you're at fault in an accident.

Additional car insurance coverages to consider

If you're leasing or financing a vehicle, most lenders require you to carry comprehensive auto coverage and collision coverage in addition to Alaska's state-required coverages. Comprehensive and collision pay to repair or replace your car regardless of fault, if it's in an accident, gets stolen, or is otherwise damaged due to a covered incident. It also makes sense to add these physical damage coverages if you're not sure you can afford a new car or major repairs after an accident.
