Real Life Scenario

A real-life scenario highlights the importance of umbrella insurance. An RLI policyholder hired a friend to replace a dock on his lake house, but an accident occurred while removing trees with a backhoe.

The policyholder's homeowners' insurance paid out to its maximum limits, but there was still a balance of $800,000 owed for medical expenses. This is a stark reminder that even with comprehensive insurance coverage, unexpected expenses can still arise.

The RLI Personal Umbrella policy stepped in and paid the remaining $800,000, avoiding any out-of-pocket expenses for the policyholder. This is exactly what umbrella insurance is designed to do provide an extra layer of protection in the event of a catastrophic claim.

Choosing the Right Policy

When deciding on an umbrella insurance policy, consider your total net worth to ensure you're adequately covered. This includes assets like vehicles, property, retirement accounts, and investments.

You should also think about your future income, as your umbrella policy should cover the value of all your assets, including any potential future earnings. This is especially important if someone sues you for damages, as your future wages might be garnished.

To find the best umbrella insurance, ask questions like, "What excess coverage does the policy provide for claims involving uninsured or under-insured drivers?" This will help you understand the policy's limitations and ensure you're getting the right protection for your assets.

Understanding Umbrella Insurance

An umbrella policy kicks in to provide more coverage when your primary insurance reaches its payout limits, giving you extra protection for your home, car, boat, or recreational vehicle.

Umbrella insurance covers excess liability costs after your auto, home, or other insurance limits have been exhausted. It can also cover things not covered under your other liability policies, such as libel or slander.

Most auto insurance policies only provide up to $500,000 in liability coverage. This is a good reason to consider an umbrella policy if you have more than $500,000 in assets.

Even if you never file an umbrella claim, the low cost of coverage is usually worth the added financial protection. This is because umbrella policies provide liability coverage beyond the limits of another insurance policy.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does a $1 million dollar umbrella policy cost?

A $1 million umbrella policy typically costs around $383 per year, making it a relatively affordable way to boost your liability coverage. This cost can vary depending on your individual circumstances and insurance provider.

Is an umbrella policy with insurance worth it?

An umbrella policy with insurance can provide valuable protection for high net worth individuals against costly lawsuits and financial losses. For just a few hundred dollars a year, it can safeguard your assets and provide peace of mind.

At what net worth should you have an umbrella policy?

If your net worth exceeds $500,000, consider an umbrella policy to fully protect your assets. This is because standard policies typically cap liability coverage at $500,000.

Does AAA offer an umbrella policy?

Yes, AAA offers an umbrella policy that provides additional protection beyond standard auto and home insurance policies. This coverage helps safeguard your assets from unexpected large claims and lawsuits.
