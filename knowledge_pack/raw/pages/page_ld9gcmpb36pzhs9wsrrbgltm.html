Esurance vs. Progressive: compare auto quotes

Car insurance quotes from Progressive and Esurance are both cheaper than the national average. However, the best choice for you depends on the type of policy you're shopping for, your driving history and your age.

Esurance vs. Progressive auto insurance coverage options

Progressive has a slight advantage when it comes to coverage options. It offers a few additional benefits that Esurance doesn't: accident forgiveness, custom equipment coverage and a deductible savings bank.

Both Esurance and Progressive offer full-coverage car insurance policies that include common coverages required or recommended in most states. In addition, both insurers offer extras like gap insurance, roadside assistance and rental car reimbursement.

In addition, Progressive offers rideshare insurance in most states, whereas Esurance's rideshare coverage is only currently available in California.

Accident forgiveness

Progressive offers two types of accident forgiveness:

  1. Small accident forgiveness is automatically included when you sign up for a policy with Progressive in most states. If you cause an accident and the total claim payout is less than $500, your rates won't go up.
  2. Large accident forgiveness is available to drivers who have been insured with Progressive for five years and remain accident- and violation-free for three consecutive years. Large accident forgiveness is included as a perk of the company's loyalty program and ensures that your rate won't go up, even if you cause an accident with a claim payout over $500.

Esurance does not offer accident forgiveness.

Progressive vs. Esurance: availability by state

Progressive, which is available in all 50 states and the District of Columbia, underwrites policies under several subsidiary names, including Progressive Casualty, Progressive Choice, Progressive Direct and more.

Esurance, which offers car insurance in 43 states, is owned by the large national insurer Allstate.

The seven states where you can get Progressive, but not Esurance, are Alaska, Delaware, Hawaii, Montana, New Hampshire, Vermont and Wyoming.

Progressive receives fewer complaints than other insurers of a similar size, according to the National Association of Insurance Commissioners (NAIC). The opposite is true for Esurance, which receives 76% more complaints than expected.

Both companies earned excellent financial stability ratings from A.M. Best, which means that they have the ability to pay out claims, even in tough economic times.

However, neither Progressive nor Esurance earned high ratings from our editors based on their coverage options, customer service reviews and the overall value offered.
