#  What is the new law on roof replacement in Florida 
By [Zach Brehm](https://brehmroofing.com/author/zbrehm/) | January 29, 2025
![](https://brehmroofing.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/01/blog-03-1024x574.jpg)
Florida roofing practices are undergoing significant changes with the updated roof replacement rule. This new law aims to provide relief to homeowners facing costly insurance claims and the pressure to replace their entire roofs due to minor damage. This shift in Florida roofing regulations aims to simplify the repair process and reduce financial strain while ensuring homes remain safe and compliant with current building codes.
## Understanding the Roof Replacement Law
The new Florida roof replacement law encompasses significant changes to roofing regulations, impacting homeowners and businesses alike. Under this legislation, ensuring the durability and compliance of the entire roof system is crucial. It dictates that any damaged portion requiring replacement must involve the rest of the roof if it exceeds a certain threshold. Compliance with this law is essential for insurance claims, as non-adherence could result in coverage issues during the next hurricane season in Ocala, FL.
### Key Highlights of the Legislation
The new law on roof replacement in Florida introduces significant changes to ensure compliance with Florida’s building code. This legislation mandates full roof replacement rather than repairs for roofs over a certain age, promoting durability during hurricane season. Homeowners must now file insurance claims for the entire roof system, not just the damaged portion, to prevent insurance fraud. These stringent regulations aim to enhance the quality of roofing industry practices and protect Florida homeowners from potential insurance crises.

## Frequently Asked Questions
### Can I choose any roofing material under the new regulations?
While the new Florida law focuses on the extent of replacement, the choice of roofing material is still subject to the existing building code and local regulations. Homeowners are encouraged to discuss suitable roofing material options with their contractor to ensure compliance.
### How old can your roof be in Florida for insurance?
Roofing regulations, as of 2023, prevent insurers from denying coverage solely based on the age of a roof if it’s less than 15 years old. However, the age of a roof can still influence insurance premiums and the need for inspections, especially as it approaches or surpasses this age.
