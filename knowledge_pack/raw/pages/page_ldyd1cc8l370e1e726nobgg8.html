Texas Car Insurance Requirements


Most of us donât get into our cars expecting to get into a car accident, but itâs always a good idea to be prepared. Brownsville, as in any other Texas city, requires you to purchase car insurance before driving. In this article, we will explain the Texas car insurance requirements, car insurance minimum requirements,  liability insurance, minimum insurance requirements for leased vehicles, penalties for driving without insurance, no-fault vs. at-fault, and the best and least expensive places to purchase car insurance in Texas.

Texas Car Insurance Laws


Texas car insurance laws are designed to ensure that all drivers carry adequate coverage in case of accidents. Hereâs a summary of the key requirements and rules regarding car insurance in Texas: 


    

  • Liability Insurance: Texas requires drivers to carry liability insurance, which covers damage and injuries you cause to others in an accident. The stateâs minimum coverage limits are $30,000 per person and $60,000 per accident. The Property Damage Requirement is $25,000 per accident. These are the minimums required by law, but you can choose to buy higher coverage for better protection if youâd like to.
    • 

  • Proof of Insurance: Drivers must carry proof of insurance (usually a physical or digital card) and provide it when requested by law enforcement or when registering their vehicle. Insurance must be active at the time of driving, and you can face penalties if caught driving without coverage.
    • 

  • Uninsured/Underinsured  Motorist Coverage (optional but recommended): Uninsured Motorist Coverage helps pay for injuries and damages caused by a driver who doesnât have insurance. Underinsured Motorist Coverage applies if the at-fault driverâs insurance is not enough to cover the full costs of your injuries or property damage. These coverages are optional in Texas, but they are strongly recommended for added protection. 
    • 

  • Collision and Comprehensive Coverage (optional): Collision insurance covers damage to your own vehicle from an accident, regardless of fault. Comprehensive insurance covers damages to your vehicle from non-collision events such as theft, vandalism, or natural disasters. These are typically required for leased or financed vehicles, but are optional for owners of vehicles.
    • 

  • Insurance for Teen Drivers: Texas law requires that all drivers, including teenagers, meet the same minimum insurance requirements. Teen drivers are usually covered under their parentsâ policies until they have their own, but parents must ensure they meet the stateâs minimum liability coverage for all vehicles driven by their teens.
    • 



Liability Insurance In Texas


Liability insurance is a requirement for drivers in Texas. This means if you are in a car accident and it is determined that you are at fault, you will be responsible for paying for repairs to the other personâs vehicle and/or their medical costs associated with bodily injuries that resulted from the accident. The stateâs minimum liability coverage requirements are:


    

  • Bodily Injury Liability: $30,000 per person for injuries, or $60,000 per accident for all injuries
    • 

  • Property Damage Liability: $25,000 per accident for damage to someone elseâs property
    • 



These minimums apply to all drivers in Texas. However, itâs often recommended to carry higher limits to better protect yourself financially in the event of an accident, as the minimums may not be sufficient to cover all costs in serious accidents.


Liability insurance alone wonât provide money to replace or repair your own vehicle or the cost of renting a vehicle while yours is being repaired. It also wonât provide money to cover your own bodily injuries incurred in your accident. Typically, liability insurance is usually not enough coverage for most people unless you have access to a spare car or the funds to repair or replace your damaged vehicle out of pocket. 


Texas Car Insurance Requirement Minimums


In Texas, the minimum car insurance requirements are Bodily Injury Liability and Property Damage Liability, which we discussed above. This coverage is often referred to as 30/60/25, which reflects the bodily injury and property damage coverage limits. Texas law also requires you to provide proof of insurance when required. 


Minimum Insurance Requirements In Texas For A Leased Vehicle


When leasing a vehicle in Texas, the minimum insurance requirements are typically higher than the stateâs basic requirements for ownership. Lease agreements usually mandate that you carry certain types of coverage to protect both the vehicle and the lessor (the leasing company). The key insurance requirements for a leased vehicle are:


    

  • Liability Insurance: Minimum Texas liability insurance requirements are 30/60/25 as stated above.
    • 



    

  • Collision Insurance: Leasing companies generally require collision coverage, which pays for repairs to the vehicle after an accident, regardless of who is at fault.
    • 



    

  • Comprehensive Insurance: This is often required by the lease agreement to cover non-collision damage, such as theft, vandalism fire, or weather-related damage.
    • 



    

  • Uninsured/Underinsured Motorist Coverage: While Texas does not require this coverage by law, many leasing companies mandate uninsured/underinsured motorist coverage to protect you in case you are involved in an accident with a driver who lacks sufficient insurance.
    • 



    

  • Personal Injury Protection (PIP): While this is optional under Texas law, your lease may require you to carry PIP coverage to help cover medical costs for you and your passengers.
    • 

  • Gap Insurance: Gap insurance may not always be mandatory, but it is highly recommended and often required by leasing companies. It covers the difference between what you owe on the lease and the vehicleâs actual cash value if the car is totaled in an accident. 
    • 



In addition to the required coverage, the leasing company may have specific requirements about coverage limits or deductible amounts, which can vary by the leasing company. It is a good rule of thumb to carefully review your lease agreement to make sure you meet the required insurance standards.

Penalties For Driving Without Car Insurance In Texas


Simply put, driving without car insurance is not a good idea and can lead to significant penalties. Here are the main consequences:


    

  • Fines: $350 for a first offense, $1000 for subsequent offenses. If youâre caught driving without insurance, you may face additional fines and fees associated with reinstating your driverâs license or registration. 
    • 

  • License and Registration Suspension: Your driverâs license and vehicle registration may be suspended until proof of insurance is provided and fees are paid.
    • 

  • SR-22 Requirement: You may be required to file an SR-22 (a certificate of financial responsibility) for up to two years, which is a higher-risk insurance policy that proves you have the required coverage.
    • 

  • Impoundment of Vehicle: If youâre caught driving without car insurance in Texas, your vehicle may be impounded, and youâll have to pay towing and storage fees to retrieve it.
    • 

  • Court Appearance: You may be required to appear in court, where you could face further fines or penalties. 
    • 



If youâre involved in an accident and donât have insurance, youâll be personally liable for all damages and medical expenses, which can lead to significant financial hardship. Itâs essential to carry at least the minimum required coverage to avoid these penalties and financial risks.


Good Car Insurance Companies In Texas


There are a variety of reputable car insurance companies in Texas to choose from, offering a variety of coverage options and customer service experiences. The best one for you will depend on your individual needs. Allstate, State Farm, Farmers Insurance, and Texas Farm Bureau all have local agents, but you can still get insurance from other national companies. Some factors to consider when choosing an insurer are: 


    

  • Customer Service: Look for an insurer with a solid reputation for handling claims and addressing customer issues efficiently.
    • 



    

  • Coverage Options: Consider the different coverage options offered, including liability, collision, comprehensive, uninsured motorist, and personal injury protection. 
    • 



    

  • Discounts: Many insurers offer discounts for safe driving, bundling policies, having safety features in your car, and more.
    • 



    

  • Financial Strength: Choose a company that is financially stable, so you know they can handle your claims.
    • 

  • Premiums: Compare rates and coverage limits, as premiums can vary significantly between insurers.
    • 



Itâs always a good idea to get quotes from a few companies to compare pricing and find coverage that best fits your needs and budget.


Least Expensive Texas Car Insurance Companies


When looking for the least expensive car insurance companies in Texas, rates can vary significantly depending on factors like your driving history, age, vehicle type, and where you live. You can also save money if your car has airlocks, anti-lock brakes, and antitheft devices. The best way to find affordable car insurance is to compare quotes from several providers. 


Is Texas A No-Fault Car Insurance State?


No, Texas is not a no-fault state. It is an at-fault state, meaning that the driver who is responsible for the accident (the âat-faultâ driver) is typically liable for the damages, injuries, and other costs associated with the crash.


In Texas, if you are in an accident, the at-fault driverâs insurance is responsible for covering the costs, including bodily injuries to others involved in the accident and property damage to vehicles or other property. 


If you are not at fault, you can file a claim with the other driverâs insurance or your own insurance(if you have the appropriate coverage, like collision or uninsured motorist). Texas law allows you to sue the at-fault driver if needed for additional damages. 


How Much Does Car Insurance Cost In Texas?


The cost of car insurance in Texas depends on a number of factors, including the type of coverage, your age, driving history, and the condition and type of your vehicle. The average cost for full coverage insurance in Texas is $1750 to $2000 per year. In comparison, minimum liability insurance typically costs between $600 and $700 per year.
