**How Auto Insurance Companies Check Driving Records**
How do insurance companies check driving records? Insurance companies know how to check a driving record with one simple legal identifier: your driver’s license. Any time you have an accident, make an insurance claim, or receive a citation for a traffic violation, your driver’s license number is associated with these events. 
> Just as creditors can attain information about you through your Social Security number, insurance companies can get the information they require through your driver’s license number.
What do car insurance companies check? Insurance companies want to see your driving habits and how likely you are to make a claim. They will check for a history of speeding tickets, at-fault accidents, and DUI/DWIs. Driving behaviors like this increase your risk as a driver, which leads to higher insurance rates.
How far back do insurance companies check for DUI? State laws determine how long insurance companies can look back, but DUI charges stay on your driving record the longest — in some states as long as ten years.
### **What Insurance Companies Look for on Your Driving Record**
Can insurance companies see your driving record? Yes, and they look for any negative marks on your driving record that may indicate you are a high-risk driver. 
> What driving record do insurance companies look at? Insurers view accidents, excessive insurance claims, and traffic violations as the biggest red flags. If your record check turns up any combination of these factors, your insurer will likely charge you a higher rate.

## Frequently Asked Questions
### How do insurance companies check your driving record?
Auto insurance companies typically check your auto insurance record by accessing information from various sources, including the Department of Motor Vehicles (DMV) or similar government agencies. They use this information to assess the risk associated with insuring you. The specific methods of checking driving records may vary among insurance companies, but common practices include electronic data exchange with the DMV, requesting driving record reports, and utilizing specialized databases.
### Do insurance companies need your driving license number?
Yes, most insurance companies will ask for your drivers license number to check your driving record through the DMV. They will likely ask for it when you request quotes online or when you buy a policy.
### How often do insurance companies check driving records?
Insurance companies won’t check your driving record on a whim. Providers only check your driving history for insurance when necessary, primarily when you get a quote for a new policy or renew your existing policy every six or 12 months.
### What can insurance companies see on your driving record?
When providers check your car insurance, they can typically see traffic violations, accidents, license suspensions or revocations, DUIs, and any other relevant driving-related incidents. Your insurance record may also include details such as the date of the incidents, points on the driver’s license, and any restrictions or endorsements on the license.
### Why do auto insurance companies want to track my driving record and insurance history?
Insurance companies want to know your driving habits and history to determine your risk. High-risk drivers are more likely to file claims and pay higher rates.
### What do auto insurance companies do with driving data?
Car insurance companies use driver data to determine rates based on driving habits and accident and claim statistics in the area.
### Can auto insurance companies access my car insurance record from another state?
Yes, insurance companies can access driving records from other states. They have the ability to request driving record information from the relevant DMV or motor vehicle agencies in different states to gather a comprehensive picture of your driving history. This allows them to assess your risk profile accurately.
### How long does my car insurance driving record affect auto insurance rates?
The impact of driving records for insurance can vary depending on the insurance company and state regulations. Insurance companies may run an insurance record check for the past three to ten years when calculating your premium. The more recent and severe violations or accidents you have, the more likely they will affect your rates.
### How far back can insurance companies check driving records?
Generally, violations and accidents remain on your insurance driving record for a certain period set by state law, typically ranging from three to five years.
### Do all insurance companies check your driving record?
Yes, every insurance provider will check your car insurance driving history and number of claims to set rates.
### Can I check my own driving record?
Yes, in most cases, how to check your car insurance record is by requesting a copy of your driving record from the DMV or the relevant motor vehicle agency in your state. The process for obtaining a driving record may vary depending on your location, but it typically involves submitting a request form, providing identification, and paying a fee. Regular car insurance record checks allow you to verify the information it contains and address any inaccuracies or discrepancies.
### I’m in the military, how far back does USAA look at your driving record?
How far USAA looks back at your driving history depends on the laws in the state where you’re licensed. Most companies can only look back three to five years per state law.
### Do insurance companies check your driving record at renewal?
Yes, this is the most common time for insurance companies to check your driving record.
